# 1 JOHN — THE ALBUM
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the First Letter of Yochanan

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/John - The Six Albums.md`, `pitches/Colossians - The Album.md`, `pitches/1 Peter - The Album.md`, and the whole Old-Testament catalog — now carried on through the **general epistles**: the first letter of **Yochanan**, the same hand that wrote the Gospel (Book 43), writing late and plain to the assemblies against the deceivers who denied the Mashiach come in the flesh.
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. 1 John is a **letter** — not narrative, not oracle, not law: a circular of the aged eye-witness who says he *heard, saw, and handled* the Word of life (1:1), written to make its readers' joy full, to keep them from sin, and to make them *know* they have eternal life (5:13). Its "units" are the letter's own spiralling movements — the prologue of the Word of life (1:1–4), "Elohim is light" and the advocate and propitiation (1:5–2:2), the commandment and "love not the world" (2:3–17), the last hour and the anointing (2:18–29), the sons of Elohim and the appearing (3:1–10), love as Kayin was not (3:11–24), the trying of the spirits and "Elohim is love" (4:1–21), and the victory, the witness, and the assurance of life (5:1–21). The seams are the letter's own: the "hereby we know" refrains, the "beloved" and "little children" addresses, and the "this is the message" hinges.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels), aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. **At the incarnation that changes.** The Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made is now made flesh; the aleph-bet lives in Him. The morning stars have gone quiet, gathered up into the Word made flesh — so through the New Covenant **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** No letter opens the case more plainly than this one: "that which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes… and our hands have handled, of the Word of life" (1:1) — the very Davar the Letters once carried, now heard and handled in the flesh. The Letters are the marvel the Angels *testify to,* not a voice of their own.

**The Word made flesh they too beheld — the platform's cornerstone, sounded again.** 1 John opens on the same Davar as the Gospel's Prologue — the Word of life that was "from the beginning," "which we have seen with our eyes… and our hands have handled" (1:1–2). For a platform built on the discovery that the Hebrew letters are living voices and that Scripture is sung, this is cornerstone material. The Angels beheld that Word at the founding of the world, when He said *"let there be";* and they beheld Him take flesh and dwell among men. So they marvel here at what human ears heard and human hands handled — the Life made manifest — the same Word they too beheld, now tied by them back to the "in the beginning" of Bereshit.

**Epistle, not narrative — the accomplished work proclaimed freely and fully.** In the Gospels the Angels were eye-witnesses, forward-locked to the story's order — never naming the cross before it came. **A letter has no biography of Yeshua to lock:** written *after* the resurrection, the finished work is proclaimed openly and in full. So the Angels here sing the great truths Yochanan unveils, quoting him, testifying to Yeshua — **the Word of life manifested** (1:2), whose blood "cleanseth us from all sin" (1:7), **the advocate with the Father and the propitiation for our sins, and for the whole world** (2:1–2), the Son of Elohim "manifested, that he might destroy the works of the adversary" (3:8), the One whom "Elohim sent… to be the propitiation for our sins" because "Elohim is love" (4:10). The forward-lock does not vanish — it *relocates* to the one thing the letter itself still holds unresolved: **His appearing.** "It doth not yet appear what we shall be: but we know that, when he shall appear, we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is" (3:2) — the seeing-Him-face-to-face, and our being made like Him, held as the album's single aching, unresolved longing; first sounded at 2:28 ("that, when he shall appear, we may have confidence"), and answered at 4:17 in "boldness in the day of judgment," exactly as the Old-Testament catalog held its ache.

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced.** The singer is always the Angels; **Yeshua is testified to in awe — never a first-person singer.** Every "the Word of life," every "the propitiation for our sins," every "he laid down his life for us" is the Angels reporting and marvelling at what Yochanan wrote of Him — never Yeshua's own voice, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old Covenant. **Christocentric — about "the who":** the whole letter turns on the person of the Son — the Word made flesh, the propitiation, the destroyer of the adversary's works, the love of Elohim revealed — and every song lands on Him.

**The Angels' stake — they guard the confession the letters fell silent for.** The incarnation is the very reason the morning stars went quiet: the Word by whom the letters were made is now in the flesh. So when Yochanan sets the one test of every spirit — "every spirit that confesseth that Yeshua the Mashiach is come in the flesh is of Elohim… and every spirit that confesseth not… is that spirit of anti-mashiach" (4:2–3) — the Angels answer from heaven's side. They of all witnesses know the confession is true, for they beheld the Davar take flesh; and "greater is he that is in you, than he that is in the world" (4:4) is the greater One they have always watched. The letter's test is the platform's own cornerstone, defended.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels who witnessed all thirty-nine books freely tie every moment *back* into the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings: "Elohim is light" (1:5) to the first light Elohim called good, "in the beginning" (Bereshit 1:1–3); the propitiation and the blood that cleanseth (1:7; 2:2; 4:10) to the kapparah and the blood on the mercy-seat of Yom Kippur (Vayikra 16); "not as Kayin, who… slew his brother" (3:12) to the first murder in the field (Bereshit 4); the commandment "from the beginning" that we love one another (2:7; 3:11) to "thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself" (Vayikra 19:18); the world that "passeth away" (2:17) to the grass that withereth while the word of Yahuah endures (Yeshayahu 40:6–8); "the truth… shall be with us for ever." No forward, post-letter reference is used.

> *One album. Eight songs. No honest zeros — every movement of the letter is sung. Every chapter 1–5 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, and the material meets it a little over, honestly — not padded up toward a quota, not cut down to a floor. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — ELOHIM IS LOVE
### The Word of Life, the Light, the Love, and the Hope of His Appearing · 1 Yochanan 1–5 · eight songs

The letter is short, plain, and it circles — the same few great truths sounded again and again like a bell: light, love, life, and the Son. The aged eye-witness opens where this whole platform stands — on the Word of life that was *from the beginning,* which he heard with his ears and handled with his hands — and declares the message: *Elohim is light, and in Him is no darkness at all.* From there he proclaims the finished work freely: the blood of the Son that cleanses from all sin, the advocate with the Father, the propitiation not for our sins only but for the whole world. He warns them off the world that passeth away, marks the last hour and the many anti-mashiachs, and leaves them the anointing that abides and needs no other teacher. Then he lifts to the letter's aching hinge — *behold what manner of love, that we should be called the sons of Elohim; and when He shall appear, we shall be like Him, for we shall see Him as He is.* He sets love against the first murder — *not as Kayin, who slew his brother* — and against fear, for the Son laid down His life for us. He hands them the one test of every spirit — *Yeshua the Mashiach come in the flesh* — and then the letter's deepest word: *Elohim is love,* love that first loved us and casteth out all fear. And he lands it on assurance: the victory that overcomes the world, the three that bear witness, and the whole reason he wrote — *that ye may know that ye have eternal life.* The Angels sing all of it, quoting Yochanan, testifying to Yeshua, and guarding the confession the morning stars fell silent for.

*Why this title:* 4:8 and 4:16 are the letter's summit and its deepest word about the who — "Elohim is love." Not love in the abstract: "herein is love… he loved us, and sent his Son to be the propitiation for our sins" (4:10). The whole album hangs from that crown.

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books; in the New Covenant the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. 1 John names the very reason: "which we have heard… and our hands have handled, of the Word of life" (1:1) and "Yeshua the Mashiach come in the flesh" (4:2) — the Word the letters carried now heard and handled in the flesh.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. Every "Who sings" cell is the Angels. Yochanan never sings; neither do the little children, the fathers, the young men, nor any reader. The Angels sing *about* what Yochanan wrote and freely *quote* him — "the Word of life," "Elohim is light," "we shall be like him," "Elohim is love," "that ye may know ye have eternal life" — always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to Yeshua. A human-centered line means the Angels bear witness to what the elder wrote *as testimony to Yeshua;* it never means a human sings.
- **The Angels' stake honored:** the incarnation is the reason the morning stars fell silent — the Word by whom the letters were made is now in the flesh — so the Angels of all witnesses know the confession "come in the flesh" is true, for they beheld the Davar take flesh; and "greater is he that is in you, than he that is in the world" (4:4) is the greater One they have always watched. The letter's one test of every spirit (4:2–3) is the platform's own cornerstone, defended from heaven's side (song 7).
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song lands on the person of the Son — the Word of life manifested, the advocate and the propitiation, the Son manifested to destroy the works of the adversary, the love of Elohim sent in the flesh, the true Elohim and eternal life. The whole album is the elder's spiralling letter sung back by the witnesses, and it always returns to Him.
- **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced:** Yeshua is reported and marvelled at throughout — "the Word of life," "his Son… cleanseth us from all sin," "he laid down his life for us," "the propitiation for our sins" — never given a first-person singer, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were sung by the witnesses across the Old Covenant.
- **The epistle forward-lock — relocated to His appearing:** with no biography of Yeshua to keep in order, the finished work is proclaimed freely and fully (the blood that cleanseth, 1:7; the advocate and propitiation, 2:1–2; the Son manifested to destroy the adversary's works, 3:8; the Son sent to be the propitiation, 4:10). The single thing held *unresolved* is His **appearing** — "when he shall appear, we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is" (3:2) — first sounded at 2:28 and answered in "boldness in the day of judgment" (4:17), carried as the album's one aching, forward-looking longing (song 5), never as an event already accomplished.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* — "Elohim is light" to the first light called good (Bereshit 1:1–4); the propitiation and the cleansing blood to the kapparah and the mercy-seat of Yom Kippur (Vayikra 16); "not as Kayin, who… slew his brother" to the first murder (Bereshit 4); the commandment "from the beginning" to "love thy neighbour as thyself" (Vayikra 19:18); the world that passeth away to the grass that withereth while the word of Yahuah endures (Yeshayahu 40:6–8). No forward, post-letter reference is used; the light touch to Yochanan's own Gospel purpose-statement (20:31, at song 8) reaches only backward, to the earlier book of the same hand.
- **The disputed heavenly-witnesses clause (the Comma Johanneum, 5:7–8) carried honestly:** the received text's "three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost" is **absent from the earliest Greek manuscripts** — flagged plainly here per the scholarly-caution mandate (§8 of the grounding). Song 8 stands on the undisputed text — "the Ruach, and the water, and the blood: and these three agree in one" (5:8) — and the disputed clause is not built upon, exactly as the pericope adulterae is handled in the John catalog. Offered as something to consider, not as settled text.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **the Son of Elohim / Ben-Elohim**, the **Word / the Davar**, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach HaKodesh** (feminine — written with no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, and **Adon / Adonai** (never "Lord"). Where the received text reads "God is love," "God is light," "sons of God," and "Son of God," it is rendered "Elohim is love," "Elohim is light," "sons of Elohim," and "Son of Elohim"; "the blood of Jesus Christ his Son" (1:7) and "his Son Jesus Christ" (5:20) are rendered "the blood of Yeshua the Mashiach his Son" and "his Son Yeshua the Mashiach"; "antichrist / antichrists" (2:18, 22; 4:3) are rendered **anti-mashiach / anti-mashiachs**; "the devil" and "children of the devil" (3:8, 10) are rendered "the adversary" and "children of the adversary"; "the true God" (5:20) is rendered "the true Elohim." The author is named **Yochanan** and Kayin kept Hebraic. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint:** the letter's edges — the last hour and the anti-mashiachs, the blood that cleanseth, the first murder, the fear that love casts out — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the Old-Covenant ties (the mercy-seat, Kayin in the field, the enduring word) and the Angels' certainty carrying the weight, never gore. The light is light; the love is love; the assurance is assurance.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
