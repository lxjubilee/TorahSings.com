**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Third Yochanan is a **letter** — a single brief chapter of fourteen verses, the elder writing to "the wellbeloved Gaius." Its "units" are the letter's own two movements — the joy of Gaius walking in truth and the welcome of the brethren for the Name's sake (1–8), and the rebuke of Diotrefes against the good report of Demetrios, closing in peace face to face (9–14). The seams are the letter's own: the "we therefore ought to receive such" that closes the welcome (v8), and the "I wrote unto the church: but Diotrefes" that opens the rebuke (v9).

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels), aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. **At the incarnation that changes.** The Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made is now made flesh; the aleph-bet lives in Him. The morning stars have gone quiet, gathered up into the Word made flesh — so through the New Covenant **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** The Letters are the marvel the Angels *testify to,* not a voice of their own.

**Epistle, not narrative — the accomplished work proclaimed freely.** In the Gospels the Angels were eye-witnesses, forward-locked to the story's order — never naming the cross before it came. **A letter has no biography of Yeshua to lock:** written *after* the resurrection, the finished work stands. So the Angels here sing the truth the elder unveils, quoting him, testifying to Yeshua — the brethren who "for his name's sake went forth" (v7), the good that "is of Elohim," the truth Gaius and Demetrios walk in. The forward-lock does not vanish — it *relocates* to the one thing the letter itself still holds unresolved: the meeting that is **not yet come.** "I trust I shall shortly see thee, and we shall speak face to face" (v14) — the face-to-face held, aching, as the letter's single unresolved longing, touched lightly.

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced.** The singer is always the Angels; **Yeshua is testified to in awe — never a first-person singer.** Every "for his name's sake," every "he that doeth good is of Elohim" is the Angels reporting and marveling at what Yochanan wrote — never Yeshua's own voice, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old Covenant. **Christocentric — about "the who":** the brethren go forth *for the Name,* the good report is *of the truth itself,* and every song lands on the One the truth is.

**The Angels' stake — they were once the strangers welcomed at the tent.** Third Yochanan is a letter about receiving the brethren and the strangers, fellow-helpers to the truth — and the Angels have their own memory in it. When Avraham ran to welcome three strangers at the tent door in the heat of the day (Gen 18), the strangers were the Angels themselves; the same at Lot's door in Sedom (Gen 19). So when the elder commends Gaius for what he does "to the brethren, and to strangers," and rebukes Diotrefes for casting the brethren out, the Angels answer from heaven's side — they know both the welcome and the shut door from the receiving end. The hospitality of Avraham stands behind Gaius's open house.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels who witnessed all thirty-nine books freely tie every moment *back* into the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings: the welcome of the brethren and the strangers to Avraham's hospitality at Mamre (Gen 18) and Lot's at Sedom (Gen 19), and to the charge to love the stranger (Vayikra 19:34; Devarim 10:19); "for his name's sake they went forth" to the zeal for the Name of Yahuah (Ps 23:3; Mal 1:11); "he that doeth good is of Elohim; but he that doeth evil hath not seen Elohim" to the two ways of the righteous and the wicked (Ps 1; Ps 34:14, "depart from evil, and do good"); the walk in truth to the walk before Elohim of the fathers (Gen 17:1). No forward, post-letter reference is used.

# ALBUM I — NO GREATER JOY THAN THIS
### Walking in Truth and Welcoming the Brethren for the Name's Sake · 3 Yochanan · two songs

A letter of fourteen verses, and it turns on a single joy: to hear that the children walk in truth. The elder writes to Gaius the beloved, whom he loves in the truth, and rejoices that the brethren came and testified of the truth in him — for he has no greater joy than this. Then the commendation: Gaius does faithfully whatever he does to the brethren and to strangers, bringing them forward on their journey; they went out *for the Name's sake,* taking nothing of the goyim, and we ought to receive such, to be fellow-helpers to the truth. The letter darkens once — Diotrefes, who loves to have the preeminence, receives them not, prates against the elder with malicious words, and casts the brethren out — and the charge falls plain: follow not that which is evil, but that which is good; he that doeth good is of Elohim. Against Diotrefes stands Demetrios, who has good report of all, and of the truth itself. And it closes in peace: many things left unwritten, the hope to speak face to face, and *greet the friends by name.* The Angels sing both movements, quoting the elder, testifying to the truth the brethren walk in — and remembering that they were once the strangers a righteous man ran to welcome.

*Why this title:* verse 4 is the letter's heart — "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children walk in truth" — the joy from which the whole letter, its welcome and its rebuke alike, hangs.

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books; in the New Covenant the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. The Letters are the marvel testified to, not a singing voice.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. Every "Who sings" cell is the Angels. Neither the elder, nor Gaius, nor Diotrefes, nor Demetrios sings. The Angels sing *about* what Yochanan wrote and freely *quote* him — "no greater joy," "for his name's sake," "follow not that which is evil, but that which is good," "greet the friends by name" — always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to Yeshua. A human-centered line means the Angels bear witness to what the elder wrote *as testimony to Yeshua;* it never means a human sings.
- **The Angels' stake honored:** Third Yochanan is a letter about receiving the brethren and the strangers, and the Angels have their own memory in it — the strangers Avraham ran to welcome at the tent (Gen 18) and those at Lot's door in Sedom (Gen 19) were the Angels themselves. So they answer from the receiving end: they know both the open house of Gaius and the shut door of Diotrefes (song 1's Avraham tie; song 2's rebuke).
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song lands on the One the truth is — the brethren go forth *for the Name,* the good report is *of the truth itself,* the one who does good is *of Elohim.* The whole album is the elder's little letter sung back by the witnesses.
- **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced:** Yeshua is reported and marveled at throughout — "for his name's sake," "of the truth itself," "he that doeth good is of Elohim" — never given a first-person singer, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were sung by the witnesses across the Old Covenant.
- **The epistle forward-lock — relocated to the meeting face to face:** with no biography of Yeshua to keep in order, the finished work stands and the truth is proclaimed freely. The single thing held *unresolved* is the meeting that is **not yet come** — "I trust I shall shortly see thee, and we shall speak face to face" (v14) — carried lightly as the album's one aching, forward-looking longing (song 2), never as an event already accomplished.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* — the welcome of the brethren and strangers to Avraham at Mamre (Gen 18) and Lot at Sedom (Gen 19) and the charge to love the stranger (Vayikra 19:34; Devarim 10:19); "for his name's sake they went forth" to the zeal for the Name of Yahuah (Ps 23:3; Mal 1:11); "doeth good… of Elohim" to the two ways of Ps 1 and "depart from evil, and do good" (Ps 34:14); the walk in truth to the walk before Elohim of the fathers (Gen 17:1). No forward, post-letter reference is used.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **Ben-Elohim** / the Son, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach HaKodesh** (feminine — written with no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, and **the Adon / Adonai** (never "Lord"). Where the received text reads "he that doeth good is of God; but he that doeth evil hath not seen God" (v11), it is rendered "of Elohim… hath not seen Elohim"; "the Gentiles" (v7) is rendered **the goyim / the nations**; "the church" (vv6, 9, 10) is rendered **the assembly**. The author is named **Yochanan**; the persons kept as the elder names them — **Gaius**, **Diotrefes**, **Demetrios**. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint:** the letter's one dark turn — Diotrefes loving the preeminence, prating with malicious words, casting the brethren out — is handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the charge "follow not that which is evil, but that which is good" and the good report of Demetrios carrying the weight. The joy is real joy; the rebuke is rebuke; the closing peace is peace.
