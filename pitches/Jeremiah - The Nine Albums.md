# JEREMIAH — THE NINE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Weeping Prophet, Yirmeyahu

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Job - The Seven Albums.md`, and `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Jeremiah is prophecy, poetry, and narrative braided together; its "units" are the oracle-formulas ("the word of Yahuah came unto me"), the datelines by reign, the symbolic-act units (the almond branch and the boiling pot, the marred linen belt, the potter's clay, the shattered jar, the yokes of wood and iron, the deed bought during the siege), the prophet's own confessions, and the fall-of-Yerushalayim narrative. The book's own seams draw the boundaries, not verse-blocks. The many short nation-oracles (46–51) are gathered into one coherent album rather than stranded one by one, exactly as Isaiah's burdens (13–23) and Exodus' plagues were.

**The weeping prophet — witnessed, never voiced.** Yirmeyahu's confessions and laments are the most naked interior monologue any prophet leaves us — "cursed be the day wherein I was born" (20:14), "O Yahuah, thou hast deceived me, and I was deceived" (20:7), "my joy is gone, grief is come upon me" (8:18), "oh that my head were waters, and mine eyes a fountain of tears" (9:1). Per the voice rule, **none of it is sung as the prophet.** The Angels witness his tears and testify to them from heaven's side — they watch the fire shut up in his bones, they see him eat the words he was given, they hear him curse his birthday — but the first person of the lament is never taken onto the singers' own lips. The weeping is real; it is overheard, not impersonated.

**The new covenant and the Branch — held, never resolved. The sharpest narrative-time gate in the book.** Jeremiah carries two promises the whole platform must hold as aching, unresolved longing. The first is the **righteous Branch** raised to David, "Yahuah Tzidqenu — Yahuah Our Righteousness" (23:5–6; 33:15–16). The second, and the deepest well in the book, is the **new covenant** — "I will put my Torah in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts… they shall all know me, from the least to the greatest… I will forgive their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more" (31:31–34). Both are sung from inside their own hour, as the Angels' ache — never named forward, never resolved to fulfillment, no New-Testament hindsight, no Messiah unveiled. "A voice in Ramah, Rachel weeping for her children… they were not" (31:15) is likewise held within its own moment, answered only by the promise given her there — "they shall come again from the land of the enemy." This is the gate most easily failed; it is guarded on every promise-title below, and Album VI is built to give the new covenant its full weight.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah, the Holy One of Israel, Yahuah Tzeva'ot — and, from *within* each moment of the book, toward the Branch and the covenant longed for but not yet unveiled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or a personified Letter — never a human (not Yirmeyahu, not Barukh, not the kings, not Pashhur, not the false prophets). The prophet's words, visions, and tears are *witnessed and testified to*, never voiced as his own first person. Yahuah's own oracles — "before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee," "return, ye backsliding children," "I will write it in their hearts" — are **His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said** (the Deuteronomy "I kill, and I make alive" / Job whirlwind model). Impersonal signs the text supplies (the almond branch and the boiling pot, the marred linen belt, the potter's clay, the broken earthen jar, the two baskets of figs, the yoke of wood then iron, the deed sealed in a jar, the doom-scroll bound to a stone and sunk in the Euphrates) are **invoked as image, never voiced.**

> *Nine albums. Fifty-seven songs. No honest zeros — every oracle, sign-act, confession, and narrative unit of Jeremiah is sung. Every chapter 1–52 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its material holds. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THEY FORSOOK THE FOUNTAIN
### The Call and the Faithless Bride · Jeremiah 1–6 · six songs

The book opens with a Voice that knew the prophet before the womb and two signs to steady him — an almond branch that means *watching*, and a pot tilting from the north. Out of that call pours the great indictment: a bride who once followed her Husband through the wilderness has committed two evils, forsaking the fountain of living waters to hew herself cracked cisterns that hold nothing. The Angels witness the whole arraignment — a betrayed love, a land run backward into formlessness, a fruitless search for one honest man, and the summons to stand at the crossroads and ask for the ancient paths.

*Why this title:* 2:13 is the wound at the heart of the whole book — "they have forsaken me the fountain of living waters, and hewed them out cisterns, broken cisterns, that can hold no water" — a people digging leaking wells while the living spring stands untouched.

|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Before I Formed You, I Knew You** | Jeremiah 1 | The Angels, singing Yahuah's word to the one He called | "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee, and before thou camest forth out of the womb I sanctified thee"; then the two signs — the almond branch (*shaqed*) that means "I watch (*shoqed*) over my word to perform it," and the seething pot tilting its mouth from the north |
| 2 | **No Nation Ever Traded Its Gods** | Jeremiah 2 | The Angels, astonished | "Hath a nation changed their gods, which are yet no gods? but my people have changed their glory"; the kindness of the bride's youth remembered, and the two evils — the fountain forsaken, the cracked cisterns hewn that hold no water |
| 3 | **Return, You Backsliding Children** | Jeremiah 3:1–4:4 | The Angels, singing Yahuah's plea | The law says a divorced wife may not return — yet the betrayed Husband calls the treacherous one home: "return, ye backsliding children, and I will heal your backslidings"; faithless Israel and treacherous Judah, the two sisters, and the offer of a healed heart |
| 4 | **The Land Was Tohu Again** | Jeremiah 4:5–31 | The Angels, who sang when it was first formed | The shofar of the foe from the north; the fourfold vision of creation running backward — "I beheld the earth, and, lo, it was without form, and void; and the heavens, they had no light"; the mountains trembling, no man, the birds all fled |
| 5 | **They Sought One Honest Man** | Jeremiah 5 | The Angels, searching the streets | "Run ye to and fro… seek… if ye can find a man… that executeth judgment… and I will pardon it" — the whole city spared for a single doer of justice, and none found; the sea held by a rope of sand it dares not cross, while the people cross every bound |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the call and its two signs open the whole book; the two evils and the plea to return are the Veil and the ache beneath it — a love betrayed and still invited home; the earth unmade is the Turn, the northern foe imagined as de-creation; the fruitless search for one just man is the Selah; and the ancient paths land the album at a crossroads the people refuse.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the call, the almond, the pot · 2 the two evils and the forsaken fountain · 3:1–4:4 the plea to the backsliding bride · 4:5–31 the foe from the north and the land turned tohu · 5 the search for one just man · 6 the siege and the ancient paths. Six for six.

# ALBUM II — DO NOT TRUST IN LYING WORDS
### The Temple Sermon and the Tears · Jeremiah 7–10 · four songs

At the temple gate the prophet is set to preach the sermon that will nearly cost him his life: stop chanting "the temple of Yahuah" like a charm while you steal, murder, and burn. The Angels witness the whole descent — a house turned into a hiding-place for robbers, a season closed with no rescue, a physician sought in vain, and the prophet's eyes overflowing until they will not run dry. And against a nation that nails its mute gods upright, the album sets the living Elohim at whose wrath the earth quakes.

*Why this title:* 7:4 is the sermon's keynote and the album's — "trust ye not in lying words, saying, The temple of Yahuah, The temple of Yahuah, The temple of Yahuah" — a building leaned on as a spell by hands that would not do justice.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **They Chanted, The Temple of Yahuah** | Jeremiah 7:1–8:3 | The Angels, at the temple gate | The crowd trusting the building like a charm while stealing, murdering, burning incense to the queen of heaven; "is this house become a den of robbers in your eyes?"; and the warning — go and see Shiloh, my first house, now ruins; the valley of slaughter named, sung with grave restraint |
| 2 | **Is There No Balm in Gilead?** | Jeremiah 8:4–22 | The Angels, grieving | The stork keeps her seasons but the people know not Yahuah's judgment; "the harvest is past, the summer is ended, and we are not saved"; "my joy is gone, grief is come upon me"; the cry — "is there no balm in Gilead; is there no physician there?" |
| 3 | **His Tears Would Not Run Dry** | Jeremiah 9 | The Angels, who witness the weeping and hear the summons | The prophet's eyes made a fountain, weeping day and night for the slain of his people — witnessed, never voiced; death climbed up into the windows; the mourning-women called to teach their daughters wailing; and the one true boast — not wisdom, might, or riches, but to know Yahuah |
| 4 | **They Nailed Their Gods Upright** | Jeremiah 10 | The Angels, astonished | A god cut from a tree, decked with silver, "fastened with nails and with hammers, that it move not"; upright as a palm but speechless, a scarecrow in a cucumber field that must be carried because it cannot walk — set against the living Elohim, the Portion of Yaakov, who made the earth |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the temple sermon opens the album under the false trust it means to shatter; the closing season and the missing balm deepen the grief; the fountain of tears is the Selah, the weeping-prophet emblem held from heaven's side; and the nailed-up idols land the album on the contrast that answers the whole sermon — carried gods that cannot save, against the Elohim who cannot be carried.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 7:1–8:3 the temple sermon and the valley of slaughter · 8:4–22 the closed harvest and the balm of Gilead · 9 the fountain of tears and the true boast · 10 the mute idols and the living Elohim. Four for four.

# ALBUM III — A PEN OF IRON ON THE HEART
### The Covenant, the Belt, and the Drought · Jeremiah 11–17 · eight songs

The covenant of the fathers is read again — and already broken, a shrine on every corner and a god for every town. The album gathers the sign-acts and the confessions that follow: a linen belt worn close and then buried by the river until it rots, a drought that dries the wells and drives the doe from her fawn, and a prophet who eats the words he is given yet asks why his wound will not heal. The Angels witness two of his laments here without ever taking his voice — and hold the album's paradox, the sin engraved on the heart with an iron stylus that only Yahuah will one day rewrite.

*Why this title:* 17:1 is the album's engraved center — "the sin of Judah is written with a pen of iron, and with the point of a diamond: it is graven upon the table of their heart" — the very tablet on which, chapters later, Yahuah promises to write His Torah instead.

# ALBUM IV — CLAY MARRED IN HIS HAND
### The Vessels, the Fire, and the Branch · Jeremiah 18–24 · nine songs

Every figure on this record is a vessel. Soft clay marred on the wheel and remade; a fired jar shattered beyond all mending; a prophet nearly broken, a fire shut in his bones he cannot hold; worthless kings and lying prophets; two baskets of figs, one good, one too rotten to eat. The Angels witness the potter's whole workshop of judgment — and set at its center the one figure who is not clay at all: a righteous Branch raised to David, whose name is Yahuah Our Righteousness, held as longing the singers cannot yet see kept.

*Why this title:* 18:4 is the album's controlling image — "the vessel that he made of clay was marred in the hand of the potter: so he made it again another vessel" — the hand that both breaks and remakes, working over a nation of failed pots.

# ALBUM V — THE CUP AND THE YOKE
### The Sentence, the Trial, and the Yokes · Jeremiah 25–29 · five songs

The sentence is pronounced and measured: seventy years, and a cup of wrath passed from Yahuah's hand to nation after nation until they reel. The Angels witness the years of conflict that follow — a prophet tried for his life at the temple gate and spared by one man's hand; bonds and yokes worn on his own neck with the hardest counsel in the book, *bow to Bavel and live*; a rival snapping the wooden yoke only to forge an iron one; and a letter carried to the exiles telling them to build, plant, and seek the peace of the city that holds them captive.

*Why this title:* the album's two great signs — the cup of the wine of fury (25:15) that every nation must drink, and the yoke of wood then iron (27–28) laid on the neck — are the twin symbols of a sentence there is no arguing past, only surviving.

| 2 | **This Man Is Worthy to Die** | Jeremiah 26 | The Angels, witnessing the trial | The temple sermon repeated, and the seizure at the gate — "thou shalt surely die"; the elders recalling Mikhah, who said Zion would be plowed as a field and was not killed; Uriyahu, who prophesied the same and was hunted into Egypt and slain; and Ahiqam's hand keeping the prophet from the mob |

# ALBUM VI — I WILL WRITE IT ON THEIR HEARTS
### The Book of Consolation · Jeremiah 30–33 · five songs

In the middle of a book of tearing-down, four chapters of pure consolation open like a spring. The Angels sing the wound the people called incurable now bound up by the One who struck it; an everlasting love drawing the exile home to dance again; and, at the album's center, the promise that answers the whole book — a new covenant written not on stone or on a heart of iron-graven sin, but inside, on hearts of flesh, so that all shall know Yahuah and their sin be remembered no more. Held as aching promise, never named as fulfilled. Then a prophet in prison buys a field in a doomed city, wagering that houses and vineyards will be owned here again.

*Why this title:* 31:33 is the deepest well in the book, and the exact answer to the sin "graven upon the table of their heart" in Album III — "I will put my Torah in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts… and I will remember their sin no more." It is carried as the Angels' longing, never resolved forward.

|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **I Will Heal the Incurable Wound** | Jeremiah 30 | The Angels, singing Yahuah's word | "The time of Jacob's trouble," great and unequaled, "but he shall be saved out of it"; the wound the people called incurable and their lovers forgot — "I will restore health unto thee, and I will heal thee of thy wounds"; the city built again upon her own ruin-heap |
| 2 | **An Everlasting Love Drew Her Home** | Jeremiah 31:1–30 | The Angels | "I have loved thee with an everlasting love: therefore with lovingkindness have I drawn thee" — the virgin of Israel back to dance with timbrels and plant vineyards; and held within its own hour, "a voice in Ramah, Rachel weeping for her children… they were not," answered only by the promise given her there, "they shall come again from the land of the enemy"; Ephraim the dear son, still pitied |

# ALBUM VII — THE WORD THEY COULD NOT BURN
### The Scroll, the Cistern, and the Fall · Jeremiah 34–39 · six songs

The word is defied every way a word can be — a covenant of freedom made and revoked, a nation's disobedience shamed by a clan that would not even drink wine, and a king who slices the scroll column by column and feeds it to his fire. But the word will not stay burned: it is written again, with more added, and it comes true to the letter. The Angels witness the prophet imprisoned, sunk in a cistern of mire and drawn up on old rags, and the long-foretold breach at last opened in the wall.

*Why this title:* 36 is the album's hinge — Yehoyaqim cuts the scroll with a scribe's penknife and burns it piece by piece, unafraid, and Yahuah has it written all over again "and there were added besides unto them many like words." The fire could not end the word; the wall it foretold falls in the album's last song.

# ALBUM VIII — BACK DOWN INTO EGYPT
### The Remnant and the Descent · Jeremiah 40–45 · six songs

A people brought out of Egypt with a strong hand ends the book fleeing back into it against Yahuah's plain word. The Angels witness the last unraveling — a trusting governor who would not believe a warning, a guest who drew a sword at the table, a remnant that swore to obey every word and then called the prophet a liar, stones buried in Pharaoh's own pavement as a sign the pursuer will follow even here, and a stubborn defense of the queen of heaven. It closes, quietly, with a word to one exhausted scribe: seek not great things for yourself.

*Why this title:* 42–43 is the album's tragedy in a direction — a people commanded "go ye not into Egypt" who go into Egypt anyway, dragging the prophet with them, undoing the exodus that made them a nation.

# ALBUM IX — BABYLON DRINKS LAST
### The Nations, Bavel, and the Lifted Head · Jeremiah 46–52 · eight songs

The cup that went round the nations in Album V is poured out in full here, throne by throne — Mitzrayim swelling like the Nile and broken at the river, Peleshet under a flood from the north, Moav settled on his lees and never yet decanted, the high nests of Edom, the snapped bow of Elam. And then the cup comes round to the one who poured it: Bavel, the golden cup that made all the earth drunk, the hammer of the whole earth, itself shattered and sunk like a stone in the river. The Angels weigh every nation with grave restraint — and let the whole book end on a single lifted head, a captive king brought up out of prison to eat at a table.

*Why this title:* 25:26 and 51:7 frame the arc — the cup Bavel made all the nations drink comes round at the last to Bavel herself, "and the king of Sheshach shall drink after them." The album ends past the fall, on the book's own final mercy: a prisoner king's head lifted (52:31).

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Elohim, and the book's own honest titles as it uses them: Yahuah Tzeva'ot (of hosts), the Holy One of Israel, Yahuah Tzidqenu (Yahuah Our Righteousness), the Portion of Yaakov. Yeshua and Ruach HaKodesh (feminine) are reserved to the platform framing; neither is voiced inside a Jeremiah-moment song. **No English "God" or "Lord" appears anywhere in any title or copy** — Elohim and Yahuah stand in their place. The "queen of heaven" is the text's own name for the idol of 7 and 44, never a title of Yahuah. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no doubled English "the" on any Ha- form; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **The new covenant honored (its full weight):** Album VI is built around 31:31–34, and "I Will Remember Their Sin No More" is sung as the aching promise the Angels hear and cannot yet see kept — the Torah written on the inward heart, all knowing Yahuah, the sin remembered no more — with no New-Testament hindsight, no fulfillment language, no Messiah unveiled. It deliberately answers Album III's "A Pen of Iron on the Heart" (17:1): the sin once engraved with an iron stylus is the very tablet Yahuah promises to rewrite.
- **The Branch honored as longing:** 23:5–6 ("A Branch Raised Up for David") and 33:15–16 (inside "Call to Me, and I Will Answer") both carry Yahuah Tzidqenu as unresolved ache, sung from within their own hour, never resolved forward. Rachel weeping in Ramah (31:15) is held within its own moment, answered only by the promise given her there.
- **The weeping prophet honored, never voiced:** every confession and lament — "thou hast deceived me" (20:7), the fire shut up in the bones (20:9), the cursed birthday (20:14–18), the incurable wound (15:18), the eaten words (15:16), the fountain of tears (9:1), "my joy is gone" (8:18) — is **witnessed and testified to by the Angels from heaven's side**, never taken onto the singers' own first person. Yahuah's own oracles ("before I formed thee I knew thee," "return, ye backsliding children," "I will write it in their hearts," "seek the peace of the city") are His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a personified Letter. Yirmeyahu, Barukh, Pashhur, Hananyah, the kings (Tzidqiyahu, Yehoyaqim, Konyahu, Shallum), Gedalyahu, Yishmael, Ebed-Melekh, the Rechabites, and the false prophets are *witnessed*, never made the singer. The almond branch and boiling pot, the marred belt, the potter's clay and the shattered jar, the two baskets of figs, the yokes of wood and iron, the cup of wrath, the sealed deed in its jar, and the doom-scroll sunk in the Perath are invoked as image, never voiced. Song 3 of Album VII gives the Picture Letters a witness stake — the words that would not stay burned.
- **Restraint:** the siege, famine, captivity, and judgment oracles — the valley of slaughter, the kings' deaths, the fall of the city, the murder at Mitzpah, the cup of wrath, the fall of Bavel — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, never rendered for shock.
- **Text-numbers as content, letters never a count:** the seventy years of exile, the two baskets of figs, the two years of Hananyah's false promise, the fifteen (and more) added words of the rewritten scroll, the seventeen shekels of the bought field, and the numbers of the appendix are sung as the text's own numbers; the Picture Letters are named as personae, never totaled, never reduced to a gematria mechanism in a title.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
