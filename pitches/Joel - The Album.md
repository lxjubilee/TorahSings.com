# JOEL — THE ALBUM
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Day of Yahuah

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`, `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Hosea - The Two Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Joel is three chapters (four in the Hebrew versification — the English 2:28–32 is the Hebrew chapter 3, and the English chapter 3 is the Hebrew chapter 4) and one continuous prophecy, so it is one album. Its "units" are its own movements, drawn at the book's own seams: the locust devastation and the summons to lament (1); the Day of Yahuah as an invading locust-and-fire army (2:1–11); the call to rend the heart and the solemn assembly (2:12–17); the restored years and "I am Yahuah your Elohim, and none else" (2:18–27); the poured-out Spirit and the wonders before the great and terrible Day (2:28–32); the nations gathered to the valley of Yehoshafat and the valley of decision (3:1–17); and the fountain from the House watering the dry valley (3:18–21). The seams are the shofar-blasts, the "turn ye even to me," the "then was Yahuah jealous," the "and it shall come to pass afterward," the muster-cry to the nations, and the "in that day."

**The spine — the Day of Yahuah, named in every chapter.** *The day of Yahuah is at hand* (1:15); *the day of Yahuah cometh, for it is nigh* (2:1); *the great and the terrible day of Yahuah* (2:31); *the day of Yahuah is near in the valley of decision* (3:14). The whole book is one Day approached from every side — a locust plague that is its shadow, an army that is its front edge, a repentance that would turn it, a restoration and an outpouring that lie beyond it, a judgment of the nations that is its verdict, and a fountain that flows once it has passed. The album follows that spine.

**On-theme — the lights the Angels sang beside, going dark.** Twice the book says *the sun and the moon shall be dark, and the stars shall withdraw their shining* (2:10; 3:15). This platform's Angels sang beside those very lights at the foundations — the morning stars of Job 38:7. The cosmic Day-of-Yahuah imagery is handled as its shadow-side echo: the witnesses testifying that the lights they once sang beside are drawing back their shining before the Day. Grave, family-friendly restraint governs every stroke of the locust-devastation and the Day-of-Yahuah terror.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator — **Yahuah** — and, from *within* each moment of the book, toward the restoration and the outpouring longed for but not yet unveiled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or a personified Letter — never a human (not Yoel, not the priests, not the elders or the drunkards). The call to the priests and elders to weep, and the priests weeping between the porch and the altar, are **witnessed and testified to** — the Angels sing *about* the summons, never voice the mourners. Yahuah's own first-person oracles — "rend your hearts, and not your garments" (2:13); "I will restore to you the years that the locust hath eaten" (2:25); "I will pour out my Spirit upon all flesh" (2:28); "I am Yahuah your Elohim, and none else" (2:27) — are **His own words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said** (the Deuteronomy / Job / Isaiah model). Impersonal creation the text supplies (the four kinds of locust — the palmerworm, the locust, the cankerworm, the caterpillar; the withered vine and the barked fig, the failed grain offering, the shofar in Zion, the sun and moon darkened and the stars withdrawing their shining, the beasts that pant for the dried rivers, the sickle in the ripe harvest and the overflowing winepress, the fountain from the House watering the valley of acacias) is **invoked as image, never voiced.**

**Narrative-time — the messianic ache held, never resolved forward.** No New-Testament hindsight anywhere. The book's great promises are carried within their own hour, unresolved: **"I will pour out my Spirit upon all flesh; your sons and your daughters shall prophesy"** (2:28–29); the wonders in the heavens and the earth before the great and terrible Day (2:30–31); **"whosoever shall call on the name of Yahuah shall be delivered"** (2:32) — sung as mystery and longing the witnesses have just overheard and cannot yet name. No Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment language inside a song. The book's own hope — **"I will restore to you the years that the locust hath eaten"** (2:25) — is sung in full.

> *One album. Seven songs. No honest zeros — every movement of Joel is sung. All three chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes: this book holds seven movements, and seven is what it gets — one per unit, never a split to pad. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE DAY OF YAHUAH IS AT HAND
### The Locust, the Day, and the Poured-Out Spirit · Joel 1–3 · seven songs

A land is stripped bare by four waves of locust, each finishing what the last began, until the vine is dried and the fig is barked and the grain offering itself is cut off from the House — and the prophet cries that this ruin is only the shadow of a nearer thing: the Day of Yahuah is at hand. The Angels sing the whole approach of that Day — the locust-and-fire army spread like dawn on the mountains while the sun and moon go dark and the stars they once sang beside withdraw their shining; the astonishing summons to rend the heart and not the garment; the turn where the wronged Elohim grows jealous for His land and vows to give back the very years the locust ate; the Spirit poured out on all flesh and the wonders before the great and terrible Day; the nations mustered to the valley of decision where Yahuah roars from Zion; and, past the Day, a fountain breaking out of the House to water a dry valley. What the locust devoured, the outpouring answers.

*Why this title:* the phrase is the book's own repeated cry — 1:15, 2:1, 2:31, and 3:14 — the one thread running through every chapter, and the Day it names is double-edged: terror for the nations and deliverance for whosoever calls on the Name. The album runs the whole approach of that Day, from the first stripped field to the fountain that flows once it has passed.

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the locust devastation opens the album in lament — the Veil, the ruin stated and the Day first named; the invading army with the darkened lights is the Turn, the Day at the wall; the call to rend the heart is the still, pleading center — the Selah where the shofar sounds and the priests weep; the restored years are the answer, mercy overruling the ruin; the poured-out Spirit is the Declaration held as longing, the outpouring that answers the devouring; the valley of decision is the verdict on the nations, the roar from Zion; and the fountain from the House lands the album — a spring breaking into a dry valley once the Day has passed. The three chapters already run devastation → Day → repentance → restoration → outpouring → judgment → fountain, so the text's own order is the shape; no reordering improves on it.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the four locusts, the stripped land, and the summons to mourn · 2:1–11 the Day as the invading army and the withdrawn lights · 2:12–17 "rend your heart," the solemn assembly, and "spare thy people" · 2:18–27 the restored years and "I am Yahuah your Elohim, and none else" · 2:28–32 the poured-out Spirit and the wonders before the Day · 3:1–17 the nations in the valley of decision, the plowshares to swords, and the roar from Zion · 3:18–21 the fountain from the House and the land that dwells for ever. Seven for seven. *(Chapter 2 splits at three seams the register itself marks — the "turn ye even to me" of 2:12, the "then was Yahuah jealous" of 2:18, and the "and it shall come to pass afterward" of 2:28; chapter 3 splits at the "in that day" of 3:18, where the verdict on the nations turns to the fountain.)*

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy — Yahuah and Elohim stand in their place. Place-names follow the text in Hebrew as the shipped prophet records do — Zion, Yerushalayim, Yehudah, the valley of Yehoshafat ("Yahuah judgeth," 3:12), the valley of Shittim (acacias), Mitzrayim (Egypt), Edom, Tzor (Tyre), Tzidon (Zidon), Peleshet (Philistia). The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **The poured-out Spirit given its full weight:** 2:28–32 is its own song — "Poured Out Upon All Flesh" — sung in awe by the witnesses as Yahuah's own oracle, and held strictly as **unresolved longing**: the Spirit on all flesh, sons and daughters prophesying, and "whosoever shall call on the name of Yahuah shall be delivered" are carried as mystery the Angels have just overheard and cannot yet name. No Pentecost hindsight, no Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment language. The book's own hope — "I will restore to you the years that the locust hath eaten" (2:25) — is sung in full in song 4.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** every promise is carried within its own hour, never resolved forward — the outpouring (2:28–29), the wonders before the great and terrible Day (2:30–31), and the deliverance for whosoever calls (2:32). The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside Joel's own hour, do not.
- **The on-theme lights honored (Job 38:7):** the cosmic Day-of-Yahuah imagery — the heavens trembling, the sun and moon dark, the stars withdrawing their shining (2:10; 3:15) — is sung as its shadow-side echo of the morning stars of Job 38:7: the Angels witnessing the very lights they once sang beside going dark before the Day. Song 2 hangs on it, and song 6 returns to it.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Vav keyed to the binding "and" that chains the giving-back of the years (2:25), the Bet keyed to the House from whose door the fountain breaks (3:18, *beit* — house/tent). Yoel, the priests, the elders, and the drunkards are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the summons to weep and the priests' plea "spare thy people" are testified to from heaven's side, never sung as theirs; Yahuah's first-person oracles — "rend your hearts," "I will restore the years," "I will pour out my Spirit," "I am Yahuah your Elohim, and none else" — are His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said. The four kinds of locust, the withered vine and barked fig, the failed grain offering, the shofar in Zion, the darkened lights, the panting beasts, the sickle and the overflowing winepress, and the fountain in the dry valley are invoked as image, never voiced.
- **Restraint:** the locust devastation and the Day-of-Yahuah terror — the army spread on the mountains, the winepress of judgment, the valley of decision, the darkened heavens — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, never rendered for shock.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the four kinds of locust (1:4), "the years that the locust hath eaten" (2:25), the former and the latter rain.
- **Craft:** no two titles share a formula or cadence — the seven run *What One Left / The Stars Withdrew / Rend the Heart / The Years… Given Back / Poured Out / Multitudes in the Valley / A Fountain From the House*, their shapes moving from the relay of devouring to the witnessed cosmic dark to the imperative paradox to the reversed loss to the outpouring to the place of verdict to the paradox-image; each is recognizable from its title and passage; paradox is prized where the text makes it true (four mouths finishing one field, the rent heart that spares the garment, the eaten years given back, the Spirit poured on *all* flesh, plows hammered into swords, a fountain from the House run down into a dry valley).
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
