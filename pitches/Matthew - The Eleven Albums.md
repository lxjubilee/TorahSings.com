# MATTHEW — THE ELEVEN ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Gospel of Mattityahu

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced the Genesis–Malachi pitches
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the fulfillment-formulas ("that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet"), the five discourse markers ("and it came to pass, when Yeshua had ended these sayings"), the dreams and angel-visitations, and the movement from Galil up to Yerushalayim that is Matthew's own spine.

**THE NEW-COVENANT TURN — the point of this record.** Through all thirty-nine books before it, the two voices of Job 38:7 — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels) — ached toward a promised One they were never permitted to name, holding Him as unresolved longing. **Now, in the Gospel, that ache is answered: the witnesses become eye-witnesses, and the One they longed for is unveiled before them, named — Yeshua — and revealed step by step.** Four rules govern the whole catalog from here:

1. **Christocentric.** Every song is about the *person* of Yeshua — who He is, what the Abba declares over Him, what He said and did, what the witnesses saw. Not abstract events, not the human bystanders as subject; Yeshua Himself is the subject of every track.
2. **Eye-witness chronology, forward-locked.** The Angels unveil Him exactly as Matthew's narrative reveals Him, in order. A song may reach back to anything already shown up to its point in the book, but never forward: a Nativity song may not reach the baptism, the cross, or the empty tomb; and where Yeshua Himself *foretells* His death or His return, the Angels witness Him speaking the dark saying and hold it unresolved — they do not yet see its end. This is the exact inverse of the Old-Covenant "held unnamed" rule.
3. **Old-Covenant cross-reference is always open.** The same host that sang creation and witnessed all thirty-nine books freely ties every moment back to the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings — **even when the human characters do not realize it.** This is the payoff of the whole arc, and Matthew is its perfect vessel: at the manger the Angels hear Micah's Beit-Lechem and the star of Yaakov; "out of Mitzrayim have I called my son"; Rachel weeping; the *almah*'s Immanu-El; at the baptism, Yeshayahu's servant; at the healings, "himself bare our sicknesses"; at the entry, Zecharyah's lowly king; at the betrayal, the thirty pieces; at the cross, the twenty-second Psalm and the fifty-third of Yeshayahu — the very aches they once carried unresolved, now seen fulfilled.
4. **Voice rule — and the silence of the Letters.** Through the whole Old Covenant the two voices of Job 38:7 sang together: the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels). **But at the incarnation a shift falls across the whole platform.** The Word — the Davar who spoke the Letters into being at the foundation — is now present in the flesh in Yeshua, and so **all the Picture Letters live in Him** and fall silent as separate singers. From this Gospel onward **the only singers are the Angels.** The Letters may still be named as *image and theme* — and the shift itself is worth marking, the aleph-bet who sang beside the Angels at creation now gone quiet before the Word made flesh, their whole meaning gathered into one Body — but never again do they carry a line. The singer is therefore always the Angels — never a human (not Mattityahu, not Miryam, not Yochanan the Immerser, not Kepha, not Pilate) and never a Letter. **Yeshua Himself is witnessed and testified to in awe — never a first-person singer** — exactly as Yahuah's own words were handled across the Old Covenant. The angelic figures Matthew supplies *are* the Angels of this platform: the angel who speaks to Yosef in the dreams (1:20; 2:13,19), the angels who ministered to Him after the temptation (4:11), the little ones' angels who behold the Abba's face (18:10), the shofar-angels who gather the chosen (24:31), and the angel of Yahuah who rolled back the stone (28:2). Impersonal creation and signs — the star, the water of the Yarden, the dove, the wind and waves, the loaves, the torn veil, the earthquake, the empty tomb — are invoked as image, never voiced.

**A song may center on a human and still be about Yeshua.** Christocentric does not mean Yeshua is the grammatical subject of every title. A song may hang on the magi, on Kepha, on a healed leper, on a Kena'anite mother, on the characters of a parable — but always as *testimony to Him*: the human story is sung by the Angels as a witness that reveals who Yeshua is. The Angels bear witness to the testimony of Yeshua — "the Scriptures testify of Me" — so a human-centered song is welcome wherever it ultimately points at Him. The humans themselves never sing; the Angels sing *about* them and *quote* them, always from heaven's own eye-witness side.

**The voices:** in the Gospel, only the Angels — the sons of Elohim — sing. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah, and now, for the first time in the whole catalog, to the Son unveiled before them: Yeshua, HaMashiach, Immanu-El. The morning stars who sang with them at the foundation have fallen silent, for the Letters now live in the incarnate Word. We overhear the Angels, and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Eleven albums. Eighty-two songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Matthew is sung. Every chapter 1–28 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — EL WITH US, UNVEILED AT LAST
### The Nativity · Matthew 1–2 · six songs

For thirty-nine books the Angels carried a longing they could not name; here it is born in Beit-Lechem, and every prophet they once ached through is fulfilled in front of them in a single chapter. A genealogy runs down to a cradle. A sign Yeshayahu gave a faithless king draws its first breath. A star the Angels remember from Bilam's mouth stands still over a house, and pagan magi kneel where Israel's own king plots murder. The witnesses sing the whole unveiling — and it costs: the flight into the very Mitzrayim their people once fled, a mother's weeping the prophet Yirmeyahu foresaw, and a King hidden away in a town so small its name became a slur.

*Why this title:* the banner is the whole New-Covenant turn — the "El with us" of Yeshayahu 7:14, held unresolved across the entire Old Covenant, is now unveiled to the Angels as a living child (1:23).

|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Names Ran Down to Him** | Matthew 1:1–17 | The Angels — who watched the whole ledger of the line | *Sefer toledot* opening again as in Bereshit — the line from Avraham through David to the Mashiach, tolled in three fourteens, with Tamar, Rachav, Ruth, and Uriyah's wife woven in; every name the Angels watched, and every letter that wrote it, now running down to one cradle — the aleph-bet gone quiet as the Word it spelled takes flesh |
| 2 | **The Almah's Sign Drew Breath** | Matthew 1:18–25 | The Angels — the messenger who spoke in Yosef's dream | "Call his name Yeshua, for he shall save his people"; and the sign Yeshayahu gave Achaz — *the almah shall conceive and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanu-El* — held unresolved for centuries, now taking its first breath |
| 3 | **The Star of Yaakov Stood Still** | Matthew 2:1–12 | The Angels — the star invoked as image | A star out of Yaakov, as Bilam foresaw, standing over the house; magi from the east kneeling with gold and frankincense and myrrh; and Beit-Lechem Ephratah, the town Mikhah named, holding the Shepherd of Israel while Herodus schemes |

# ALBUM II — THIS IS MY BELOVED SON
### The Voice, the Dove, the Wilderness · Matthew 3–4 · five songs

Thirty years of silence break with a voice in the wilderness — the herald Yeshayahu promised and the Eliyahu Malakhi foretold, crying "prepare the way." Then the scene the whole platform was built to overhear: the sinless One steps into a sinner's water, the heavens tear open, the Ruach descends as a dove, and the Abba's own voice names Him aloud. The Angels sing the testing that follows — forty days answered word for word out of Devarim — and step into the story themselves to minister to Him. And the light Yeshayahu saw dawning on Galil, held as longing on the Isaiah records, at last breaks over the sea, and four fishermen leave their nets.

*Why this title:* the album's banner is the Abba's own declaration over the Yarden (3:17) — the first time in the whole catalog the witnesses hear the Son named aloud from heaven.

# ALBUM III — GREATER THAN MOSHEH ON THE MOUNTAIN
### The Sermon on the Mount · Matthew 5–7 · seven songs

Once, the Angels watched a mountain wrapped in smoke and a people beg the Voice to stop. Now One climbs a hillside, sits down, and gives the Torah from His own mouth — not "thus saith Yahuah" but "but I say unto you" — and no one begs Him to stop; they lean in. The witnesses sing the whole discourse from heaven's side: a kingdom whose blessed are the poor and the mourning, a Torah not destroyed but filled full to its last stroke, a hidden Abba who sees in secret, a life freed from anxiety by the lilies and the birds, and two builders whose houses the same storm tests. The paradox the Angels cannot get over: a greater than Mosheh, teaching as One who owns the words.

*Why this title:* the Angels who stood at Sinai recognize the impossible — the Torah given again, from a mountain, by One who speaks it with His own authority; the crowds themselves end the sermon "astonished, for he taught as one having power, and not as the scribes" (7:28–29).

# ALBUM IV — THE WIND AND THE SEA OBEYED
### The Miracles and the Mission · Matthew 8–10 · nine songs

Down from the mountain, the words become deeds, and the Angels watch creation itself answer its Maker's voice. A leper is touched clean; a Roman's faith outruns Israel's; a storm lies down mid-sentence; demons name Him before men will; a paralytic is forgiven before he is healed; a tax-collector rises from his booth and follows. Heaven hears Yeshayahu's fifty-third song quoted over the sick — *himself bare our sicknesses* — and the Angels, who once carried that chapter as an unanswered ache, now watch its opening lines walk the roads of Galil. Then the Twelve are named and sent as sheep among wolves, and the witness goes out.

*Why this title:* 8:27 is the disciples' own astonishment, sung by the Angels who already knew the answer — "what manner of man is this, that even the winds and the sea obey him?"

# ALBUM V — HE WHO HAS EARS, LET HIM HEAR
### The Kingdom in Parables · Matthew 11–13:52 · nine songs

The crowds gather, but the seeing grows dim, and Yeshua begins to speak in parables — the kingdom hidden in a field, in a seed, in a net — so that hearing, they hear not. The Angels sing the whole strange middle of the Gospel: Yochanan sending from prison the question the prophets answer for him; woe over towns that watched miracles and would not turn; the invitation "come unto me" and the yoke that turns out to be rest; the Sabbath's own Adon judged for keeping it wrong; and Yeshayahu's gentle servant, held as ache on the Isaiah records, now named aloud over Him. And when they demand a sign, He gives them only Yonah — a dark riddle of three days the Angels cannot yet read.

*Why this title:* the album's banner is the refrain that seals every parable (13:9, 13:43) — the kingdom offered to whoever has ears; the mystery given to some and veiled from others, exactly as Yeshayahu 6 foretold.

## Compliance Notes

- **The Letters have fallen silent — the Angels are the only voice.** Through the whole Old-Covenant catalog the two voices of Job 38:7 sang together: the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels). At the incarnation the Word who spoke the Letters into being is present in the flesh in Yeshua, so **all the Picture Letters now live in Him and fall silent as singers.** In every song of this Gospel the singer is the Angels alone — including the specific angels the text supplies, who are the Angels of this platform: the messenger to Yosef (1:20; 2:13,19), the angels who ministered after the temptation (4:11), the little ones' angels who behold the Abba's face (18:10), the shofar-angels who gather the chosen (24:31), and the angel of Yahuah at the tomb (28:2). The Letters are named only as *image and theme* (the *yod* and the least stroke of 5:18, the aleph-bet gone quiet before the Word made flesh) — never as a singing voice.
- **The humans never sing.** Not one song is voiced by a human — not Miryam, not Yochanan the Immerser, not Kepha, not a disciple, not a crowd, not Pilate. The Angels are the only voice; they sing *about* what the humans said and did and may *quote* them (Kepha's confession, the centurion's word, the crowd's Hoshana), always framed from the Angels' own eye-witness testimony. A human-centered song means the Angels bear witness to that human as testimony to Yeshua — never that the human sings.
- **Christocentric, but not grammatically narrow.** Every song is ultimately about the person of Yeshua — who He is, what the Abba declares over Him, what He said and did. Songs may center on humans and events (the magi, the centurion, the Kena'anite mother, Kepha, the parables' characters), but always as witness that reveals Him: "the Scriptures testify of Me." Yeshua Himself is witnessed and testified to in awe — never a first-person singer; His own words are reported and marveled at, exactly as Yahuah's were across the Old Covenant.
- **Eye-witness chronology, forward-locked.** No song reaches past its own place in the book. The Nativity songs do not touch the baptism or the cross; the healing songs do not anticipate the passion. Where Yeshua *foretells* His death or His return — the sign of Yonah (12:40), "get thee behind me" (16:21–23), the ransom (20:28), the Olivet discourse (24–25) — the Angels witness Him speaking the dark saying and **hold it unresolved**, not yet seeing its end; they do not depict the cross or the resurrection before Album XI. The one deliberate resolution is His own: "I am with you alway" (28:20) answering "Immanu-El, El with us" (1:23), the Gospel's own inclusio.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference, always backward, freely made — even when the humans miss it.** This is the payoff of the whole arc: the star of Yaakov (Num 24:17) and Beit-Lechem (Mic 5:2) at the manger; "out of Mitzrayim have I called my son" (Hos 11:1); Rachel weeping (Jer 31:15); the *almah* and Immanu-El (Isa 7:14); the light on Galil (Isa 9:1–2); the servant who bore our sicknesses (Isa 53:4) and would not cry in the street (Isa 42:1–3); "I will have mercy, not sacrifice" (Hos 6:6); the honouring lips (Isa 29:13); Bereshit's "male and female" (Gen 1:27; 2:24) and Lemekh's seventy-sevenfold turned to mercy (Gen 4:24); the lowly king on the donkey (Zech 9:9); the rejected stone (Ps 118:22); the Elohim of the living (Ex 3:6); the Shema (Deut 6:5) and David's Adon (Ps 110:1); the smitten shepherd (Zech 13:7); the thirty pieces (Zech 11:12–13); the covenant-blood (Ex 24:8) and the Pesach lamb (Ex 12); the twenty-second and sixty-ninth Psalms and the great shofar (Isa 27:13) — the very aches the Angels once carried unresolved, now seen fulfilled. Every ancient tie reaches *backward* only.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yeshua (never "Jesus"); the Mashiach / HaMashiach (never "Christ," and no stacked English article on the *Ha-* form); Ben-Elohim, Ben-David, Ben-Adam (His own self-title as the text uses it), Immanu-El, Yahuah, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine), Abba, Adon / Adonai for the text's *kurios* where a human or the Psalmist addresses Him. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy.** Hebraic names and places throughout — Mattityahu, Miryam, Yosef, Yochanan the Immerser, Kepha, Beit-Lechem, Natzeret, the Yarden, Galil, Yerushalayim, Herodus, Kayafa, Bar-Abba, Gat-Shmanim, Gulgolta, Beit-Anya, Kefar-Nachum. No internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint on the passion:** the death of Yochanan, the scourging, and the crucifixion are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side — the weight carried by the Angels' awe and the Old-Covenant ties (the Pesach lamb, Yeshayahu 53, the twenty-second Psalm) and the cosmic signs (the darkness, the torn veil, the earthquake), never by any wound shown. The resurrection is the triumph, and it is sung as triumph.
