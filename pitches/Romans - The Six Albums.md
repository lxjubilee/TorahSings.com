# ROMANS — THE SIX ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Letter of Shaul to the Assembly at Rome

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`, and the four Gospels (`pitches/_New Testament - The Gospels.md`)
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Romans is **epistle** — a letter, not a narrative and not an oracle; its "units" are the letter's own movements: the salutation and thesis, the diagnosis of the whole world under sin, the righteousness of Elohim through faith, Avraham the father of faith, the second Adam, death to sin and the war with the flesh, the summit of chapter 8, the mystery of Yisrael and the olive tree, and the long paraenesis that closes on a doxology. The seams are the letter's own doctrinal turns ("but now the righteousness of Elohim," "what shall we say then," "there is therefore now no condemnation," "I would not, brethren, that ye should be ignorant of this mystery," "I beseech you therefore") and its bursts of praise ("O the depth of the riches," "to Elohim only wise, be glory").

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters have fallen silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels) — aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. At the incarnation that changed: the Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made came in the flesh in Yeshua, and the Picture Letters now live in Him and fall silent as separate singers. Therefore in this letter, as in the Gospels, **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** The Letters are the marvel the Angels testify to, not a voice of their own.

**Epistle, not narrative — the forward-lock is lifted; the finished work is proclaimed freely.** In the Gospels the Angels held the cross forward as unspoken mystery, because they were unveiling Yeshua in the order the story revealed Him. **Romans is written from the far side of the empty tomb.** There is no biography to lock: Shaul writes to an assembly he has never seen, *after* the incarnation, the cross, the resurrection, the ascension, and the outpouring of the Ruach — and he proclaims all of it, fully and openly, from his first breath ("declared to be the Son of Elohim with power… by the resurrection from the dead," 1:4). So the Angels proclaim it with him, freely and fully: the blood, the mercy seat, the buried-and-raised, the Ruach given, the Abba-cry loosed. **The one thing still held as unresolved, aching longing is His return and the redemption not yet consummated** — the whole creation groaning for the revealing of the sons (8:18–25), the fulness of Yisrael still to come (11:25–26), the night far spent and the day only "at hand" (13:11–12), Satan to be bruised under their feet "shortly" (16:20). That eschatological ache is the platform's held-longing ethos, kept true even here: the work is *finished,* but the day is *not yet.*

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced — and Shaul quoted, never sung.** The singer is always the Angels. Yeshua is testified to in awe, never a first-person singer, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old Covenant. And Shaul himself — like every human in this catalog — **never sings.** The Angels sing *about* what he wrote and freely *quote* him ("the just shall live by faith," "O wretched man," "who shall separate us"), but always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to the One his letter is about. A human-centered line is testimony *to Yeshua;* it never means the human sings.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** Romans is stitched with the Tanakh, and the Angels — the host that witnessed all thirty-nine books — draw every thread back: Havakuk 2:4 ("the just shall live by faith"); the glory the nations exchanged (Ps 106:20); "there is none righteous" (Ps 14; Ps 5; Ps 140; Yeshayahu 59); Avraham's faith reckoned as righteousness (Gen 15:6); the mercy seat of the Day of Coverings (Lev 16); Adam and the trespass that entered (Gen 3); "Yaakov have I loved, but Esav have I hated" (Mal'akhi 1:2–3; Gen 25); the potter and the clay (Yeshayahu 29; Yirmeyahu 18); the remnant (Yeshayahu 10; Hoshea 1–2); "the word is nigh thee" (Devarim 30:14); "whosoever shall call upon the name of Yahuah" (Yoel 2:32); "how beautiful are the feet" (Yeshayahu 52:7); Eliyahu's seven thousand (1 Melakim 19); the olive tree and its root; "there shall come out of Tzion the Deliverer" (Yeshayahu 59:20); "vengeance is mine, saith Yahuah" (Devarim 32:35); "every knee shall bow" (Yeshayahu 45:23); the root of Yishai in whom the nations trust (Yeshayahu 11:10); and Satan bruised under the heel (Gen 3:15).

> *Six albums. Twenty-nine songs. No honest zeros — every one of the letter's sixteen chapters is sung. Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its movement holds.*
> *Not canon — a reading, held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE GOSPEL OF ELOHIM
### The Righteousness Revealed and the Whole World Guilty · Romans 1–3 · four songs

The letter opens like a trumpet: a gospel "promised afore by his prophets in the holy scriptures," concerning the Son declared with power by the resurrection — and the Angels proclaim it freely, for it is already done. Then the ground goes dark. Shaul turns the light first on the nations who traded the glory of the incorruptible Elohim for images, then on the one who judges yet does the same, and levels the whole race with a chain of the Psalms and Yeshayahu: *there is none righteous, no, not one.* The album is the diagnosis before the cure — the courtroom where every mouth is stopped and all the world stands guilty, so that the mercy of the next album lands as sheer gift.

*Why this title:* 1:1 names the album's whole subject — "the gospel of Elohim… concerning his Son" — and 1:16–17 states its thesis, the righteousness of Elohim revealed from faith to faith.

# ALBUM II — THE RIGHTEOUSNESS OF FAITH
### Apart from the Law — Avraham, Peace, and the Second Adam · Romans 3–5 · four songs

Into the sealed courtroom comes the great *but now.* A righteousness of Elohim without the law is manifested — Yeshua set forth as the mercy seat, the covering where wrath is met by blood — and the Angels who watched the atonement-lid sprinkled once a year name the true one at last. Then Shaul reaches back before the law was ever given, to Avraham, who "believed Elohim, and it was counted unto him for righteousness" — reckoned righteous while still uncircumcised, the father of all who believe. The record climbs to peace with Elohim, to enemies reconciled while they were yet enemies, and to the great parallel that swallows the fall: as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners, so by one man's obedience many are made righteous.

*Why this title:* 3:22 and 4:13 name the album's spine — "the righteousness of Elohim which is by faith," "the righteousness of faith" — the gift the courtroom could never earn.

# ALBUM III — DEAD TO SIN, ALIVE IN THE MASHIACH
### Buried in Baptism, Freed from Sin, and the War with the Flesh · Romans 6–7 · four songs

If grace abounds where sin abounds, shall we sin the more? *Elohim forbid.* The album answers with the grave and the resurrection made personal: buried with Him by immersion into death, raised to walk in newness of life; no longer slaves whose wages are death, but freedmen whose free gift is life. Then Shaul turns to the law itself — the believer dead to it through the body of the Mashiach, wed now to another, to Him who is raised from the dead — and closes on the honest agony of the divided man: the good he would he does not, the evil he would not he does, until the cry breaks out, *O wretched man that I am! who shall deliver me?* — and the answer comes, *I thank Elohim through Yeshua the Mashiach.*

*Why this title:* 6:11 is the album's spine — "reckon ye also yourselves to be dead indeed unto sin, but alive unto Elohim through Yeshua the Mashiach."

# ALBUM IV — THERE IS NO CONDEMNATION
### The Summit — the Ruach, the Cry of Abba, the Groaning Creation, and the Love That Will Not Let Go · Romans 8 · four songs

This is the mountaintop of the letter, and the Angels sing all four songs inside one chapter. It opens with the verdict the whole diagnosis was driving toward — *there is therefore now no condemnation* — and the Ruach of life who does what the flesh never could. It rises to the cry the Ruach loosens in the adopted sons: *Abba, Father* — heirs, and joint-heirs with the Mashiach. Then the one held note in the whole letter sounds: the whole creation groaning, travailing, waiting for the revealing of the sons and the redemption of the body — a longing kept unresolved, for the day is not yet come. And it lands where nothing in the catalog lands harder: that neither death nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor height, nor depth can part the redeemed from the love of Elohim — the Angels confessing, in awe, that *not even they* could do it.

*Why this title:* 8:1 is the summit stated — "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in the Mashiach Yeshua."

# ALBUM V — THE OLIVE TREE
### Yisrael's Election, the Remnant, the Grafted Branches, and the Depth of the Riches · Romans 9–11 · five songs

From the summit Shaul descends into his deepest grief — his own kinsmen, to whom belong the adoption and the covenants, and who have stumbled. Three chapters wrestle it: the election that ran through Yitzchak and Yaakov, not by works; the potter's freedom over the clay and the remnant kept by grace; the stumbling-stone and the word so near it is in the mouth, with salvation flung open to whosoever shall call. Then the great horticultural mystery — natural branches broken off, a wild branch grafted in against nature, and a warning to the graft not to boast against the root that bears it. And at the end, the future held open and unresolved — *so all Yisrael shall be saved* — resolving not into a diagram but into worship: *O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of Elohim!*

*Why this title:* 11:17–24 gives the album its governing image — the cultivated olive, its branches broken and grafted, the root that carries them all — the picture that holds the whole wrestling of chapters 9–11.

# ALBUM VI — A LIVING SACRIFICE
### The Renewed Mind, Love Unfeigned, the Day at Hand, the Strong Bearing the Weak, and the Final Amen · Romans 12–16 · eight songs

The great "therefore" turns doctrine into a body offered. The Angels sing the whole paraenesis — the living sacrifice and the renewed mind; the one body and love without dissimulation, leaving vengeance to Yahuah and heaping coals of kindness; the governing powers ordained and the sword not borne in vain; love as the fulfilling of the law under a sky where the night is far spent and the day is at hand. They sing the strong bearing the weak, every knee bowing, the root of Yishai in whom the nations trust, and the nations themselves brought as a priestly offering sanctified by the Ruach Kodesh. And they close the letter and the whole segment on its own last breath: the household at Rome named one by one, Satan to be bruised under their feet *shortly* — the day still held, not yet — and the doxology sealing all of it, *to Elohim only wise, be glory through Yeshua the Mashiach for ever. Amen.*

*Why this title:* 12:1 is the hinge of the whole ethical turn — "present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto Elohim, which is your reasonable service."

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters are silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books; from the incarnation onward the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. Every "Who sings" cell across all six albums is the Angels, framed as heavenly witnesses testifying to Yeshua.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by Shaul or by any person. The Angels sing *about* what Shaul wrote and freely *quote* him — "the just shall live by faith," "O wretched man that I am," "who shall separate us," "O the depth of the riches" — but always from their own witness perspective, testifying to the One the letter is about. A human-centered line means the Angels bear witness to that truth *as testimony to Yeshua;* it never means the human sings. The one first-person title in the catalog, "Not Even We Could Part Them," has *we* refer to the **Angels themselves** (8:38, "nor angels… shall separate") — the singers' own voice, not a human's.
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song is ultimately about the person of Yeshua — declared the Son with power by the resurrection (1:4), set forth as the mercy seat (3:25), the second Adam (5), the risen Bridegroom (7:4), the ground of no condemnation (8:1), the stone and the end of the law (9:32; 10:4), the root of Yishai (15:12), the name in the closing doxology (16:27).
- **The finished work proclaimed freely; His return held unresolved (the epistle adaptation):** because Romans is written after the resurrection, the accomplished work — incarnation, cross, the mercy seat, the buried-and-raised, the ascension, the gift of the Ruach — is proclaimed **fully and openly** throughout (1:4; 3:24–25; 4:25; 5:8; 6:4; 8:11; 10:9), not held forward as in the Gospels. The **one** thing kept as aching, unresolved longing is His return and the redemption not yet consummated: the creation groaning for the revealing of the sons (8:18–25), the fulness of Yisrael still to come (11:25–26), the day only "at hand" (13:12), Satan bruised "shortly" (16:20). Those four held notes are flagged in the tracklists and are the platform's held-longing ethos kept true.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* into the Torah, Prophets, and Writings — Havakuk 2:4; Ps 106 and the Psalms of 3:10–18; Bereshit 15:6 and the garden of Bereshit 3; Vayikra 16; Bereshit 25 and Mal'akhi 1; Yeshayahu 29 and Yirmeyahu 18; Hoshea and Yeshayahu on the remnant; Devarim 30:14; Yoel 2:32; Yeshayahu 52:7; 1 Melakim 19; the olive root; Yeshayahu 59:20; Devarim 32:35; Mishlei 25:21–22; Yeshayahu 45:23; Yeshayahu 11:10; and Bereshit 3:15. No forward, post-letter reference is used.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach / HaMashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **Ben-Elohim**, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach HaKodesh** / **Ruach Kodesh** (feminine — written with no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, **Adon / Adonai** (rendering the letter's "Lord," as in "the Adon Yeshua" and "call upon the name of Yahuah"). Hebraic names and places throughout — Shaul, Avraham, Yitzchak, Yaakov and Esav, Yisrael, Yerushalayim, Tzion, Adam; and the prophets and writers cited by their Hebraic names (Havakuk, Yeshayahu, Yirmeyahu, Hoshea, Yoel, Eliyahu, Mal'akhi, Moshe, Yishai). **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint — grave and family-friendly:** the wrath of chapter 1, the courtroom of chapter 3, and the war with the flesh in chapter 7 are handled gravely and without spectacle, the Old-Covenant ties and the Angels' awe carrying the weight; triumph is triumph — the mercy seat, no condemnation, the love nothing can part, the final Amen.
