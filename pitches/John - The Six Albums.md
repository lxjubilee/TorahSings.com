# JOHN — THE SIX ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Gospel of Yochanan

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Job - The Seven Albums.md`, and `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. John is Gospel — eye-witness testimony, not oracle and not law; its "units" are John's own architecture: the Prologue, the witness of the Baptizer, the **seven signs**, the seven **"I AM" (ego eimi) sayings**, the farewell discourse, the High-Priestly prayer, the passion, and the resurrection. The book's own seams draw the boundaries — the sign-formulas ("this beginning of miracles," "the second miracle"), the feast-datelines ("the Jews' passover was at hand," "it was the feast of dedication, and it was winter"), the "hour" markers ("mine hour is not yet come" → "the hour is come"), and the discourse-openings.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels) — aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. **At the incarnation that changes.** John's Prologue is the very hinge: the Word — the Davar, the Logos — *by whom the Letters themselves were made* is now present in the flesh in Yeshua, so **all the Picture Letters now live in Him and fall silent as separate singers.** The morning stars who sang beside the Angels at the foundations have gone quiet, gathered up into the Word made flesh. Therefore in this Gospel **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** The Letters are now the marvel the Angels *testify to*, not a voice of their own. And the Angels the text itself supplies are the Angels of this platform: the ones who "ascend and descend upon the Son of Man" (1:51 — Yaakov's ladder, Gen 28), and the **two angels in white sitting in the empty tomb** where the body had lain (20:12).

**The Word made flesh — the platform's cornerstone, now sung by the Angels.** For a platform built on the discovery that Scripture is sung and that the Hebrew letters are living voices, **the Word made flesh is cornerstone material.** Album I opens on the Prologue (1:1–18): the Angels witness the Davar "by whom all things were made" — the same Word who, in the beginning, said *"let there be"* and spoke the aleph-bet into being — now made flesh and dwelling among us; and they testify, with awe, that the morning stars who sang with them at creation have gone silent, for the letters they carried now *live in Him.* John rewrites Genesis 1's "in the beginning," and the Angels make the tie.

**Eye-witness chronology — forward-locked.** In the Old-Testament catalog the ache was held *unresolved*; here the Angels become **eye-witnesses who see Him unveiled** — and they unveil Him exactly as John's narrative reveals Him, in order. A song may reference anything already come to pass up to its point in the text and **nothing later.** John's own "hour" language is the gift that makes this clean: "mine hour is not yet come" lets the Angels hold the coming hour as *anticipation the text itself voices* — the temple He will raise, the Son of Man lifted up like the serpent, the grain that must fall — without narrating the cross before it comes.

**Old-Testament cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels — the host that sang creation and witnessed all thirty-nine books — freely tie any moment back to the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings, *even where the human hearers do not realize it.* John is woven with it, and the Angels draw the threads: the Prologue rewrites Genesis 1; "behold the Lamb of Elohim" recalls the Passover and Isaiah 53; the bronze serpent lifted (Num 21); the manna (Ex 16); the fountain of living waters (Jer 2:13); the pillar of fire (Ex 13); the shepherd of Israel (Ps 23; Ezek 34); the true vine where the old vine yielded wild grapes (Ps 80; Isa 5); "before Avraham was, I AM" tied to the Ehyeh of the bush (Ex 3:14); the parted garments and "I thirst" (Ps 22; Ps 69); "not a bone broken" (Ex 12:46; Ps 34:20) and "they shall look on him whom they pierced" (Zech 12:10).

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced — and the "I AM" sayings held in awe.** The singer is always the Angels; **Yeshua is witnessed and testified to in awe — never a first-person singer.** His words — including the seven great **"I AM" (ego eimi) sayings** (the bread of life, the light of the world, the door, the good shepherd, the resurrection and the life, the way and the truth and the life, the true vine, and the absolute "before Avraham was, I AM") — are *His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said,* exactly as Yahuah's own oracles were handled across the Old-Testament catalog. The Angels tie every ego eimi back to the divine Name **Ehyeh — "I AM THAT I AM"** — of Exodus 3. **Christocentric, not grammatically rigid:** every song bears witness to Yeshua — who He is, what He said and did, what the eye-witnesses saw — but a song may center on a human and a human moment (Nikodemos, the woman at the well, the man born blind, Marta and Miryam, Thomas, Kepha on the shore) *as testimony to Him,* for "the Scriptures testify of me" (5:39) and the whole book is written "that ye might believe that Yeshua is the Mashiach, the Son of Elohim" (20:31). Impersonal creation and the signs the text supplies (the water made wine, the pool, the loaves, the mud and the washed eyes, the grave-clothes folded, the pierced side, the charcoal fire on the shore) are **invoked as image, never voiced.**

> *Six albums. Forty-four songs. No honest zeros — every unit of John's testimony is sung. Every chapter 1–21 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its testimony holds. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE WORD MADE FLESH
### The Word, the Witness, and the First Signs · John 1–4 · nine songs

The Gospel opens where this whole platform stands: *in the beginning was the Word.* The Angels witness the Davar by whom all things — and the very letters — were made, now made flesh and pitching His tent among us; the morning stars who sang beside them at creation have gone silent, for the letters they carried now live in Him. From there the witness widens: the Baptizer points and cries *behold the Lamb of Elohim;* the Angels recognize Yaakov's own ladder named as a man; and the signs begin — water blushing into wine, a temple driven clean, a ruler born again by night, a Samaritan given living water, a dying son healed by a word across the miles.

*Why this title:* 1:14 is the album's spine and the platform's cornerstone — "the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, and we beheld his glory."

# ALBUM II — BEFORE AVRAHAM WAS, I AM
### The Bread, the Light, and the Name · John 5–8 · eight songs

The signs deepen and the sayings begin to thunder. A man thirty-eight years lame is told to rise and carry his bed — on the Sabbath — and the controversy opens the Father-and-Son testimony. Five thousand are fed from a boy's lunch; the Angels see a figure walk the dark sea and hear the ego eimi, *it is I;* and the great sayings come — *I am the bread of life… I am the light of the world.* The album climbs to the boldest word in the Gospel, spoken in the temple with stones already in hand: *before Avraham was, I AM* — and the Angels tie it straight back to the Name at the burning bush.

*Why this title:* 8:58 is the album's summit and the sharpest tie in the book — the ego eimi that reaches back to Ehyeh, "I AM THAT I AM," and drew up stones.

# ALBUM III — LAZAROS, COME FORTH
### The Shepherd, the Resurrection, and the Hour · John 9–12 · nine songs

The last public signs blaze and the hour begins to turn. A man born blind is sent to wash at Shiloach and comes back seeing; the shepherd-sayings open the fold — *I am the door… the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep;* and at Beit-Anya the greatest sign of all: a man four days dead is called by name out of his tomb, *Lazaros, come forth,* and the grave-clothes walk. That raising sets the hour in motion — a high priest prophesies more than he knows, a woman pours out burial-spice before its time, a King rides in on a colt, and a grain of wheat is told it must fall to bear its harvest.

*Why this title:* 11:43 is the album's summit and its most arresting image — the loud cry into a sealed tomb, "Lazaros, come forth," and the dead man obeying.

# ALBUM IV — LET NOT YOUR HEART BE TROUBLED

The Book of Glory opens on the floor: the One by whom all things were made girds a towel and washes dust from human feet. Then, through the long last night, the tenderest teaching in the Gospel — *let not your heart be troubled… I am the way… I am the true vine… another Comforter… my peace I give.* The Angels overhear a betrayer go out into the night while love is being commanded, and hear the Son declare His victory before the hour that wins it: *be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.* And at the last they overhear the prayer itself — the Son asking the Father *that they all may be one.*

*Why this title:* 14:1 (and 14:27) is the keynote of the whole farewell — the command that frames the discourse, spoken on the edge of the hour: "let not your heart be troubled."

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books; in this Gospel the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. John 1 is where that silence is sung: Album I opens on the Prologue, the Angels witnessing the Davar by whom the very letters were made now made flesh, the aleph-bet that sang at creation now gathered up into Him. The Letters are the marvel testified to, not a singing voice.
- **The Prologue cornerstone honored:** "The Davar Who Spoke Us Took Flesh" (1:1–18) is the platform's cornerstone song — the Word made flesh, tied by the Angels to Genesis 1's "and Elohim said," treated as cornerstone material beside Job 38:7 and the seraphim of Isaiah 6.
- **The ladder and the two angels honored:** 1:51 ("ye shall see the angels of Elohim ascending and descending upon the Son of Man") is sung by those Angels as their own — Yaakov's ladder (Gen 28) named as a man; and the **two angels in white** of 20:12 open Album VI from inside the empty tomb. Both are the Angels of this platform, never a human's voice.
- **Christocentric, not grammatically rigid:** every song bears witness to Yeshua — who He is, what He said and did, what the eye-witnesses saw. Songs may center on humans and human moments (Nikodemos, the Samaritan woman, the man born blind, Marta and Miryam, Thomas, Kepha) *as testimony to Him* — "the Scriptures… testify of me" (5:39) — for the whole book is written "that ye might believe that Yeshua is the Mashiach, the Son of Elohim" (20:31).
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. Every "Who sings" cell in all six albums is the Angels (or the specific angels the text supplies — the ladder-angels of 1:51, the two angels in white of 20:12 — who are the Angels of this platform). The Angels sing *about* what the humans said and did and freely *quote* their words where the text supports it — Nikodemos's questions, the Samaritan woman's astonishment, the blind man's "whereas I was blind, now I see," Kepha's "to whom shall we go?", Thomas's "my Adonai and my Elohim," Miryam's "I have seen Yahuah" — but always framed from the Angels' eye-witness perspective, testifying. A "human-centered" song means the Angels sing about that human as a witness to Yeshua; it never means the human sings.
- **Eye-witness chronology, forward-locked:** each song unveils Yeshua exactly as John's narrative reveals Him, referencing only what has already come to pass at that point and nothing later. John's "hour" language carries the anticipation the text itself voices — the temple He will raise (2:19), the Son of Man lifted up like the serpent (3:14), "when ye have lifted up the Son of Man" (8:28), the grain that must fall (12:24), "I lay down my life, that I might take it again" (10:17) — all held *forward as mystery,* never narrated as an accomplished cross before the passion comes.
- **The seven "I AM" (ego eimi) sayings witnessed, never voiced:** the bread of life, the light of the world, the door, the good shepherd, the resurrection and the life, the way and the truth and the life, and the true vine — together with "it is I; be not afraid" (6:20), the quiet "I that speak unto thee am he" (4:26), "I am he" in the garden (18:5), and the absolute "before Avraham was, I AM" (8:58) — are all sung *in awe by the Angels who testify to what He said,* never as His own first person; and the Angels tie the ego eimi to the divine Name **Ehyeh, "I AM THAT I AM"** of Exodus 3:14. The seven "I AM" song-titles deliberately carry John's own refrain — this is the book's signature, not a repeated cadence by accident.
- **Old-Testament cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* into the Torah, Prophets, and Writings — Genesis 1 (the Prologue); the Passover lamb and Isaiah 53 (the Lamb of Elohim; the unbroken bones); the ladder of Genesis 28; the bronze serpent of Numbers 21; the manna of Exodus 16; the fountain of living waters of Jeremiah 2:13; the pillar of fire of Exodus 13; the shepherd of Psalm 23 and Ezekiel 34; the vine of Psalm 80 and Isaiah 5; the Name at the bush of Exodus 3; the parted garments of Psalm 22, the vinegar of Psalm 69, "not a bone broken" of Exodus 12 and Psalm 34, and the pierced One of Zechariah 12:10. No forward, post-narrative reference is used.
- **The pericope adulterae (7:53–8:11) carried honestly:** "Neither Do I Condemn Thee" is included as it stands in the received text and is beloved, but it is **absent from the earliest manuscripts** — flagged plainly here per the scholarly-caution mandate (§8 of the grounding), handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, and offered as something to consider, not as settled text.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach / HaMashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), Ben-Elohim, Ben-Adam, the **Word / the Davar / the Logos** (the Prologue, rendered honestly as "the Word"), the **Lamb of Elohim**, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach Kodesh** / **Ruach HaKodesh** (feminine — written with no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, and **"I AM" / Ehyeh** where the text uses ego eimi. Thomas's confession is rendered "my Adonai and my Elohim" and Miryam's cry "I have seen Yahuah." Hebraic names and places throughout — Yochanan, Miryam, Kepha, Andrai, Natan'el, Lazaros, Marta and Miryam of Beit-Anya, the Yarden, Kefar-Nachum, the pool of Beit-Chesed, Shiloach, Golgotha, the Kidron, Tiveryah, Bar-Abba. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint:** the passion — the arrest, the denials, the trial, the crowning with thorns, the crucifixion, the pierced side — is handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the Old-Testament ties (the Passover lamb, "not a bone broken," the pierced One of Zechariah 12:10) and the Angels' awe carrying the weight, never gore; the resurrection and the shore-breakfast are the triumph.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
