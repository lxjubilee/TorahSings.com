# COLOSSIANS — THE ALBUM
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for Shaul's Letter to the Assembly at Kolossae

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/John - The Six Albums.md`, `pitches/Matthew - The Eleven Albums.md`, and the whole Old-Testament catalog — now carried across the New-Testament frontier from Gospel into **epistle.**
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Colossians is a **letter** — not narrative, not oracle, not law: Shaul's circular to the assembly at Kolossae (and, by his own instruction, to Laodikeia, 4:16). Its "units" are the letter's own movements — the thanksgiving and prayer (1:1–14), the great cosmic Mashiach-hymn (1:15–23), the labor for the mystery (1:24–2:5), the argument against the vain deceit and the record nailed to the tree (2:6–15), the shadow and the body (2:16–23), the risen life and the ache of His appearing (3:1–17), and the household and the closing greetings (3:18–4:18). The seams are Shaul's own: the hymn-formula ("who is the image of the invisible Elohim"), the "therefore" hinges ("as ye have therefore received," 2:6; "if ye then be risen," 3:1), the "let no man" warnings, and the final-greeting roll.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels), aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. **At the incarnation that changes.** The Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made is now made flesh; the aleph-bet lives in Him. The morning stars have gone quiet, gathered up into the Word made flesh — so through the New Covenant **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** This letter names the reason directly: in Yeshua "are hid all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge" (2:3), and "in him dwelt all the fullness of Elohim, bodily" (2:9) — the Word the letters once carried now dwells in Him in the flesh. The Letters are the marvel the Angels *testify to,* not a voice of their own.

**Epistle, not narrative — the accomplished work proclaimed freely and fully.** In the Gospels the Angels were eye-witnesses, forward-locked to the story's order — never naming the cross before it came. **A letter has no biography of Yeshua to lock:** written *after* the resurrection, the finished work is proclaimed openly and in full. So the Angels here sing the great truths Shaul unveils, quoting him, testifying to Yeshua as the risen and reigning Mashiach — **the image of the invisible Elohim, the firstborn of every creature, in whom all things were created and hold together, the head of the body, the firstborn from the dead, in whom all the fullness dwells, reconciling all things by the blood of His tree** (1:15–20). He "spoiled principalities and powers, triumphing over them" on the tree (2:15). The forward-lock does not vanish — it *relocates* to the one thing the letter itself still holds unresolved: **His appearing.** "When the Mashiach, who is our life, shall appear, then shall ye also appear with him in glory" (3:4) — the hope laid up in heaven (1:5), held as the album's single aching, unresolved longing, exactly as the Old-Testament catalog held its ache.

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced.** The singer is always the Angels; **Yeshua is testified to in awe — never a first-person singer.** Every "the Mashiach in you," every "he is before all things," every "the body is the Mashiach" is the Angels reporting and marveling at what Shaul wrote of Him — never Yeshua's own voice, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old Covenant. **Christocentric — about "the who":** the whole letter is about the *person* of the cosmic Mashiach, and every song lands on Him.

**The Angels have skin in this letter.** Colossians is one of the few books that sings *of the Angels themselves* — and it hands them their own witness-stake. The "thrones, dominions, principalities, and powers" of 1:16 are the angelic ranks, and the letter says flatly that they too "were created by him, and for him" — so the Angels sing the astonishing confession that *they themselves* were made in Him and for Him. And when Shaul warns against "worshipping of angels" (2:18), the Angels answer from heaven's side: they refuse all worship and turn every knee to Yeshua the Head. The rebel "principalities and powers" they watch **spoiled** and led in open triumph (2:15).

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels who witnessed all thirty-nine books freely tie every moment *back* into the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings: the image of the invisible echoing Adam made in Elohim's image (Gen 1:26–27) and Wisdom beside Him before the deep (Mishlei 8:22–30); the beloved Son of Ps 2:7; the inheritance-portion of the saints in light (Num 18:20; Ps 16:5); the circumcision made without hands and the circumcised heart (Deut 10:16; 30:6; Jer 4:4); the festivals and new moons and Sabbaths as the mo'edim of Vayikra (Lev 23) — "a shadow of things to come, but the body is the Mashiach"; the right hand of Ps 110:1; the renewed image of Gen 1. No forward, post-letter reference is used.

> *One album. Seven songs. No honest zeros — every movement of the letter is sung. Every chapter 1–4 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim the material meets on its own — not padded up, not cut down. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE IMAGE OF THE INVISIBLE
### The Cosmic Mashiach and the Hope of His Appearing · Kolossae 1–4 · seven songs

The letter is short and its ceiling is the highest in the New Covenant. Shaul opens on thanksgiving and a prayer, then lifts — without warning — into the greatest Mashiach-hymn in all his letters: the image of the invisible Elohim, the firstborn of every creature, in whom all things were made and in whom all things hold together, the head of the body, the firstborn from the dead, in whom all the fullness dwells and by whose blood all things are reconciled. From that summit the letter comes down into the assembly's danger — a "philosophy and vain deceit" that would judge them by food and festival, puff them up with the worship of angels, and bind them with "touch not, taste not." Against it Shaul sets the finished work: the record of debt blotted out and nailed to the tree, the powers disarmed and led in open triumph, the shadow given way to the body. And he leaves them looking up — risen with the Mashiach, seeking the things above, waiting for the one thing still to come: *when He who is our life shall appear.* The Angels sing all of it, quoting Shaul, testifying to Yeshua, and confessing that they themselves were made in Him and for Him.

*Why this title:* 1:15 is the letter's spine and its highest word — "who is the image of the invisible Elohim, the firstborn of every creature" — the crown from which the whole album hangs.

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books; in the New Covenant the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. Colossians names the reason itself: "in him are hid all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge" (2:3) and "in him dwelt all the fullness of Elohim, bodily" (2:9) — the Word the letters carried now dwells in Him in the flesh.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. Every "Who sings" cell is the Angels. Shaul never sings; the Angels sing *about* what he wrote and freely *quote* him — "the Mashiach in you, the hope of glory," "the body is the Mashiach," "remember my bonds" — always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to Yeshua. A human-centered line means the Angels bear witness to what Shaul wrote *as testimony to Yeshua;* it never means Shaul sings.
- **The Angels' own witness-stake honored:** where the letter sings *of* angels, the Angels answer from heaven's side. The "thrones, dominions, principalities, and powers" of 1:16 are confessed by the Angels as themselves "created by him, and for him" (song 2); the warning against "worshipping of angels" (2:18) is answered by the Angels refusing all worship and turning every knee to Yeshua the Head (song 5); the rebel "principalities and powers" of 2:15 are witnessed spoiled and led in open triumph (song 4).
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song lands on the person of the cosmic Mashiach — the image of the invisible, the firstborn, the fullness, the head, the reconciler, the risen life. The whole album is the one hymn Shaul lifted at Kolossae, sung back by the witnesses.
- **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced:** Yeshua is reported and marveled at throughout — "he is before all things," "the body is the Mashiach," "the Mashiach is all, and in all" — never given a first-person singer. His work is testified to, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were sung by the witnesses across the Old Covenant.
- **The epistle forward-lock — relocated to the appearing:** with no biography of Yeshua to keep in order, the finished work is proclaimed freely and fully (the hymn of 1:15–20; the record nailed to the tree and the powers triumphed over, 2:14–15). The single thing held *unresolved* is His **appearing** — "when the Mashiach, who is our life, shall appear, then shall ye also appear with him in glory" (3:4) and "the hope which is laid up for you in heaven" (1:5) — carried as the album's one aching, forward-looking longing (song 6), never as an event already narrated.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* — Adam in Elohim's image and Wisdom of Mishlei 8 (the image of the invisible; the renewed image of 3:10); the beloved Son of Ps 2:7; the inheritance-portion of Num 18:20 and Ps 16:5; the circumcised heart of Deut 10:16, 30:6, and Jer 4:4 (the circumcision made without hands); the mo'edim of Lev 23 (the festivals, new moons, and Sabbaths as "a shadow of things to come, but the body is the Mashiach"); the right hand of Ps 110:1. No forward, post-letter reference is used.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **Ben-Elohim** / the Son, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach HaKodesh** (feminine — written with no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, and **the Adon / Adonai** (never "Lord"). Where the received text reads "the Godhead" (2:9), it is rendered honestly as "all the fullness of Elohim, bodily"; where it reads "the cross," it is rendered "the tree" (the blood of His tree, 1:20; nailed to His tree, 2:14). Hebraic and Grecized names throughout — Shaul, Timotheos, Epaphras, Tychikos, Onesimos, Aristarchos, Markos, Loukas, and the places Kolossae and Laodikeia. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **The singing of 3:16 — sung by the Angels:** Shaul tells the assembly to let the word of the Mashiach dwell in them richly, "singing with grace in your hearts" (3:16). On this platform it is the **Angels** who take up that song — the humans are told to sing, and the witnesses do the singing, testifying. It is carried inside song 6, not made a human voice.
- **Restraint:** the letter's weight — the sufferings of Shaul, the blood of the tree, the record of debt, the bonds at the close — is handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the Old-Covenant ties and the Angels' awe carrying it, never gore. The triumph over the powers is triumph.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
