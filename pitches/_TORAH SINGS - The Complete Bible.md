## The one story the platform tells

Two voices sang together at the foundations of the world — the **morning stars** (the Picture Letters, the aleph-bet) and the **sons of Elohim** (the Angels), the two choirs of Job 38:7.

- **Through the 39 books of the Old Testament,** both voices sang — and held one ache unresolved: the coming One the letters spelled and the Angels awaited, longed for and never seen. No New-Testament hindsight; the messianic aches kept open.
- **At the incarnation, the singing changed.** The Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made became flesh; the aleph-bet came home into Him. So through the **Gospels and the Letters** the Picture Letters fall silent, and the **Angels sing alone** — the humans never sing; they are witnessed and quoted; Yeshua is testified to in awe, never first-person-voiced; every song bears witness to Him.
- **In the Gospels,** the Angels unveil Yeshua in eye-witness order, the cross and empty tomb held to their place. **In Acts,** the risen Adon works by the Ruach to the ends of the earth. **In the Letters,** the finished work is proclaimed freely, and the one ache still held open is **His return.**
- **In the Revelation, the singing returns in full** — the living creatures, the elders, the myriads of angels, and every creature take up the new song to the Lamb — and the platform's every longing is answered: the Word declares Himself **the Aleph and the Tav,** the whole aleph-bet gathered into His person. And still the last note is a cry: *"Even so, come, Adon Yeshua."*

> *The stars sang. The angels sang. Now the stars live in the Word — and the angels sing on.*

## The doctrinal through-line, honored end to end

- **Only the Angels sing** across all 27 New-Testament books (the Picture Letters live in the incarnate Word). Every one of the 620 "Who sings" cells is the Angels or the text's own heavenly choir (the ladder-angels, the tomb-angels, the heavenly host over Beit-Lechem, the Gethsemane angel, Mikha'el, the living creatures, the elders, the myriads) — never a human, never a first-person Yeshua.
- **The humans never sing** — they are witnessed, quoted, and carried (the Magnificat and Benedictus and Nunc Dimittis; Kepha's sermons and Stefanos's speech; Shaul's letters; the 144,000's new song "no man could learn but the redeemed," carried by the Angels who could not learn it).
- **Christocentric** — every song is ultimately about the person of Yeshua.
- **Eye-witness / narrative-time** honored: the Gospels and Acts forward-locked; the Letters proclaiming the finished work while holding His return; the Revelation answering the ache yet still crying "Come."
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference free but always backward.**
- **OHI sacred names** throughout — no English "God/Lord/Jesus/Christ" in any title or copy; Yeshua, the Mashiach, Yahuah, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine), the Aleph and the Tav.
- **Grave, family-friendly restraint** on every passion and judgment; **the Hebraic Music Mandate** on every track; and the standing posture — *a reading, held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.*
- Textually disputed passages flagged honestly wherever they fall — the pericope adulterae (John 7:53–8:11), the longer ending of Mark (16:9–20), the 1 Timothy 3:16 variant, the Comma Johanneum (1 John 5:7–8), and Jude's extrabiblical citations.

Two doctrinal jewels of the New Testament arc: **Hebrews**, where the platform's own singers gladly confess the Son *above* themselves and marvel that He stooped below them to save a race not their own; and **Revelation**, where the morning-star Letters that went silent are revealed as Him — *the Aleph and the Tav* — a thread that opens the book, returns at the new creation, and closes the whole 66-book project.
