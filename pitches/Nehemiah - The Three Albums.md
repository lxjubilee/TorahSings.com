# NEHEMIAH — THE THREE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Rebuilt Wall

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the report-and-prayer opening, the king's leave and the night survey, the builders' roster, the armed guard, the crisis, the plots and the finished wall, the register, the reading of the Law, the confession, the sealed covenant, the repopulation by lot, the dedication of the wall, and the four reforms that close the book, in place of the Genesis *toledot*.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the faithfulness promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own hour of the restoration, with no later hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Nehemiah-moment song the aches (a city's shame stood up in stone, the Law heard until the people weep and then feast, "the joy of Yahuah is your strength," a covenant sealed and then quietly broken again by the last chapter) are carried as mystery and longing, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Nechemyah, not Ezra, not Sanballat, not Tobiah) or anything derived from one; impersonal creation and the works of the story the text supplies (the broken-down wall and the burned gates, the trowel and the sword carried together, the Water Gate, the sealed scroll, the two great thanksgiving choirs on the wall) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Three albums. Twenty songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Nehemiah is sung. All thirteen chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes below it: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THEY BUILT WITH A SWORD IN HAND
### The Wall · Nehemiah 1–7 · nine songs

A man in a Persian palace hears that the wall of a city he has never seen lies broken and its gates burned, and he weeps for months over rubble a thousand miles away. Then he asks a king for the ruin, prays in the breath between a question and its answer, and rides the broken circuit alone by night before he tells a soul — and what follows is the fastest wall in Scripture, parceled out family by family, built with a trowel in one hand and a weapon girded at the hip, held against mockery and ambush and four summons to a deadly meeting, and closed whole in fifty-two days. The witnesses sing the reproach that stood up in stone, and the strange emptiness at the end of it: a great wall around a city with almost no one home.

*Why this title:* the wall of Nehemiah is raised under threat, every builder laboring with a weapon at his side (4:17–18) — the one image the whole book is remembered by, construction and war carried in the same two hands.

# ALBUM II — THEY WEPT TO HEAR IT READ
### The Word and the Covenant · Nehemiah 8–10 · four songs

The wall is finished, and now the Book is opened. From a wooden platform at the Water Gate the Law is read aloud from morning until midday, and a people who understand it plainly for the first time begin to weep — and are told to stop, and eat the fat, and drink the sweet, for the joy of Yahuah is their strength. They find a forgotten command and roof the whole city in green booths; they retell their entire history in sackcloth and lay it all on one word — You are just in all that has come upon us; and they seal a written covenant, name beneath name, swearing the very things the last chapter will break. The witnesses sing the Book opened over a city, and the weeping that turned to feasting.

*Why this title:* the hinge of the album is a crowd weeping at the sound of the Law read and understood (8:9) — the shock of Scripture heard clearly, before the command comes to turn the weeping into a feast.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Joy of Yahuah Was Their Strength** | Nehemiah 8:1–12 | The Lamed — the shepherd's-staff letter, root of the word for teaching — with the Angels; the Water Gate (the Mem) invoked | The Book opened above the standing people at the Water Gate from morning to midday, the Levites giving the sense until the crowd understands and weeps — and the weeping answered "this day is holy, mourn not; eat the fat, drink the sweet, for the joy of Yahuah is your strength" |
| 2 | **Booths Rose on Every Roof** | Nehemiah 8:13–18 | The Angels, watching a city roof itself in branches from one line just found | On the second day, studying, they find the command to dwell in booths; and the city breaks into green — branches on every roof and court and temple gate — a gladness not seen since the days of Yehoshua son of Nun, the Law read aloud each of the seven days |
| 3 | **You Are Just in All of It** | Nehemiah 9:1–37 | The Tsade — the letter that opens *tsaddiq*, "just" — sounding it over the retold story; the Angels | On a day of sackcloth and earth the Levites retell the whole story — creation, Avraham, Egypt, the sea, Sinai, the calf, the manna, the rebellions, the mercies without end — and lay it all on one word: You are just in all that has come upon us; behold, we are servants this day in the land You gave |
| 4 | **They Sealed What They Swore to Keep** | Nehemiah 9:38–10:39 | The Tav — the mark, the seal pressed beneath the covenant — with the Angels, who have seen such vows before | Because of all this they cut a sure covenant and write it, and the princes and priests and Levites press their seals beneath — an oath entering a curse to walk in the Law: no daughters given to the peoples of the land, no trade on the Sabbath, the temple dues, the firstfruits and the tithes — "we will not forsake the house of our Elohim" |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the reading and the weeping-turned-feast open the album; the booths are the glad obedience; the confession is the Selah — the whole story wept back in sackcloth and set down on one word; and the sealed covenant is the Declaration, name pressed beneath name — the vow whose breaking Album III will sing.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 8:1–12 the reading at the Water Gate · 8:13–18 the booths · 9:1–37 the great confession · 9:38–10:39 the sealed covenant. Four for four.

# ALBUM III — THE JOY WAS HEARD FAR OFF
### The Dedication and the Reforms · Nehemiah 11–13 · seven songs

The wall stands, but the holy city is nearly empty — so lots are cast to bring one of every ten inside, and the priests and Levites are named down the years, and then the joy breaks: two great thanksgiving choirs walk the top of the finished wall in opposite directions, trumpets and the instruments of David going before, to meet at the house of Elohim with a gladness heard even afar off. And then, quietly, the covenant sealed a few chapters back begins to unravel — an enemy lodged in the temple courts, the singers gone back to their fields for want of bread, the Sabbath sold in the gates, the children of foreign wives unable to speak the language of Yehudah — and the book ends not in triumph but in a plea: remember me, O my Elohim, for good. The witnesses sing the joy that circled the wall, and the slow ache underneath it.

*Why this title:* the album's summit is the dedication of the wall, where the joy of Yerushalayim was heard even afar off (12:43) — worship circling the finished stone, loud enough to carry over the fragile, half-filled city it crowned.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| 2 | **Name Upon Name, Priest and Levite** | Nehemiah 12:1–26 | The Letters, the ledger-keepers who trace the servants of the house down the years | The roll of the priests and Levites who came up from exile, and the high priests generation upon generation, and the singers and the gatekeepers — the servants of the house named down the years so none who ministered would be forgotten |

| 4 | **An Enemy's Room Inside the House** | Nehemiah 13:1–9 | The Angels, who saw a foe given a chamber in the courts, and his goods flung to the street | The Law read aloud parts Ammon and Moav from the congregation — yet in the very courts of the house a great chamber has been given to Tobiah the enemy; and returning, the governor grieves, and throws all his household goods out into the street, and commands the chambers cleansed |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the lot-cast repopulation and the priestly ledger fill the guarded but empty city; the dedication with its two choirs is the Declaration — the joy heard afar off, the album's and the book's summit; and the four reforms descend from it in the text's own order — the enemy cast out, the tithes restored, the Sabbath shut in, the foreign marriages purged — the Benediction that is really a longing, closing the book on "remember me for good."

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 11 the repopulation by lot · 12:1–26 the priests and Levites · 12:27–47 the dedication and the two choirs · 13:1–9 the enemy cast from the courts · 13:10–14 the tithes restored · 13:15–22 the Sabbath guarded · 13:23–31 the foreign marriages purged. Seven for seven.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy — always Elohim or Yahuah. The high priest and the leader who bear the name Yeshua/Jeshua and the son of Nun who bears the name Yeshua/Joshua are written by other honest forms (the high priest; Yehoshua son of Nun) so the name Yeshua is reserved, in this document, for the Son alone. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Nehemiah-moment song the aches — a reproach stood up in stone, the Law heard until the people weep and then feast, "the joy of Yahuah is your strength," a covenant sealed and then broken clause by clause, a joy heard afar off over a still-fragile city, and the book's final "remember me for good" — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. David is named because the text names his instruments (12:36); the faithfulness the covenant reaches for is held as a longing the witnesses cannot yet see resolved. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour of the restoration, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Gimel keyed to the foot that traced the ruin by night, the Vav to the roster's "and next… and next," the Zayin to the weapon at the builder's hip, the Chet to the wall closed whole, the Lamed to the Law taught until it was understood, the Tsade to *tsaddiq* ("You are just"), the Tav to the seal pressed beneath the covenant, the Dalet to the gate shut for the Sabbath, the Pe to the children's half-tongue, and the Letters as the ledger-keepers of the registers. Nechemyah, Ezra, Sanballat, and Tobiah are *witnessed*, never made the singer; Nechemyah's arrow-prayers, Ezra's public reading, and the great confession are testified to from heaven's side, never sung as theirs. The broken wall and burned gates, the trowel and the sword carried together, the Water Gate, the sealed scroll, and the two great thanksgiving choirs are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the fifty-two days of the wall, the register of chapters 7 and 11, one of every ten chosen by lot to dwell in the holy city, the seven days of booths.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
