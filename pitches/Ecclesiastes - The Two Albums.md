# ECCLESIASTES — THE TWO ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Searcher Under the Sun

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Ecclesiastes is not a narrative but a single reflective voice — Qoheleth, the Assembler, the Preacher — searching for meaning, and its seams are its thematic movements. Each of the twelve chapters is one movement and one song; the book breaks cleanly at 6:9/6:10, where the long investigation ("all is vapor") turns to the counsel of the second half, and so it becomes two tight albums of six.
**The angle (the spine):** the Preacher searches *under the sun* — the phrase tolls through the book — and everything he grasps there turns to *hevel*: vapor, breath, a passing mist, the thing you cannot hold. The Angels who sing this book see precisely what he cannot: what is *above* the sun. They witness the human search from the one vantage that already holds the answer he strains toward, and they keep their peace until the book's own conclusion.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator — and, from *within* each moment of the search, toward the judgment and the eternity the Preacher can feel but not find. **Sacred name:** Ecclesiastes names the Almighty **Elohim** throughout, not the covenant name, and these songs render Him as the text does — Elohim. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside Qoheleth's own hour, with no later hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside an Ecclesiastes-moment song the eternity set in the heart that no man can find out (3:11), the judgment Elohim will bring on every hidden thing (12:14), and the spirit that returns to the Elohim who gave it (12:7) are carried as mystery and longing, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Qoheleth, not the Preacher, not Shlomo) or anything derived from one; the meditation is *witnessed and testified to* from heaven's side, never voiced as the Preacher's own. Impersonal creation the text supplies — the sun that hurries back to its place, the rivers running to a sea never full, the threefold cord, the silver cord and the golden bowl and the pitcher broken at the well, the grasshopper that drags itself — may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Two albums. Twelve songs. No honest zeros — every chapter of Ecclesiastes is sung. All twelve accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes below it: the book splits at its own seam into two records of six, and six is what each one gets. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — VAPOR OF VAPORS
### The Search Under the Sun · Ecclesiastes 1–6 · six songs

A king who denies himself nothing — wisdom, wine, houses, vineyards, gold, singers, every desire the eye asks for — turns to survey it all and finds it vapor and a grasping after wind. He watches the sun hurry back to rise again and the rivers pour into a sea that is never full, and learns that the wise man dies exactly as the fool, that everything he built passes to a hand he never chose, that a man can be handed wealth and not the power to taste it. And the Angels sing from the one place he never thinks to look — above the sun — where the seasons are already beautiful in their time and eternity aches in a heart that cannot find out the work of Elohim.

*Why this title:* the book's own thesis, *hevel havelim* (1:2) — literally "vapor of vapors," the truest rendering of the old "vanity of vanities." Chapters 1–6 are the accumulation of everything the searcher lays hold of under the sun turning to breath in his hand.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Sea Was Never Full** | Ecclesiastes 1 | The Angels, above the sun, watching a round that never closes | A generation goes and a generation comes, the sun hurries back to where it rose, the rivers run to the sea and the sea is never full, and the eye is never satisfied with seeing — there is no new thing under the sun, and the searcher who set his heart to seek out all things by wisdom finds it a grasping after wind |
| 2 | **The Wise Man Dies Like the Fool** | Ecclesiastes 2 | The Angels, who keep the ledger of everything a builder must leave behind | He built houses and planted vineyards, gathered silver and gold and singers, kept his wisdom through it all — and looked, and it was vapor: the wise and the fool share one grave and one forgetting, and the toil of his hands passes to a man who comes after him, who may be a fool, and who never sweated for it |
| 3 | **A Time to Be Born, a Time to Die** | Ecclesiastes 3 | The Ayin — the eye keyed to *olam*, eternity — with the Angels, who see the whole he cannot | A season for everything under heaven — to be born and to die, to break down and to build, to keep silence and to speak — and beneath the turning of the times the line the whole book circles: He has made everything beautiful in its time, and set eternity (*olam*) in their heart, yet no man can find out the work Elohim does from beginning to end |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the endless round opens the album on the Veil — the weariness stated before the reason for it; the great experiment and its shared grave are the first turn into the dark; the seasons and the eternity set in the heart are the still center, the ache the whole book circles; the cord of three and the counsel on words and silver carry the search through company, worship, and wealth; and the good a man cannot eat lands the album on the sharpest vapor of all — dropping the listener at 6:9, the exact seam where the investigation closes and the counsel of Album II opens.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the endless round and the start of the search · 2 the great experiment and its oblivion · 3 the seasons and the eternity set in the heart · 4 the oppressed, the rivalry, and the threefold cord · 5 the vows and the riches that do not satisfy · 6 the good he was not given to enjoy. # ALBUM II — WE HELD OUR PEACE UNTIL THE END
### The Counsel and the Conclusion · Ecclesiastes 7–12 · six songs

The search turns from what cannot be held to how a mortal ought to live inside the mist. The Preacher weighs the house of mourning against the house of feasting and finds it the wiser room, watches justice run backward and the day of death overrule every king, and counsels a man to eat his bread with a glad heart, to do with his might whatever his hand finds, and to cast it on waters he cannot see the far side of. Then the last poem — the failing body, the silver cord loosed and the golden bowl broken, the pitcher shattered at the well — and the Angels, who held their peace through every vapor of the searching, break it at last for the conclusion: fear Elohim and keep His commandments, for the spirit returns to the One who gave it, and Elohim will bring every hidden thing into judgment.

*Why this title:* the spine of the whole book seen from heaven's side. The Angels above the sun already hold the answer the Preacher strains toward, and they keep silent through all his searching — until the book's own end (12:13), where the thing they knew all along is finally said, and their long witness becomes speech.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| 8 | **No Man Rules the Day of Death** | Ecclesiastes 8 | The Angels, above the sun, who see the work he cannot find out | No man has power over the wind to restrain it, and no one has power over the day of death; there is no discharge in that war — and the mystery beside it, the wicked buried with honor while the righteous are treated as though they were wicked, because sentence is not executed speedily; though a wise man claim to know, he cannot find out the work done under the sun |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the wisdom proverbs open the album in the house of mourning, the somber room where the second half thinks best; the day of death no king can rule and the justice that runs backward deepen the dark; the race that goes to neither the swift nor the strong and the little folly that outweighs honor are the turning middle, chance and ruin held together; the bread cast on the unseen waters is the first light of trust; and the poem of old age with its conclusion lands the album and the book — the Benediction, where the Angels who witnessed every vapor finally speak the answer the searching could not reach.

Six for six.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout. Ecclesiastes names the Almighty **Elohim**, not the covenant name, and every title and note renders Him as the text does — Elohim. Yahuah, Yeshua, and Ruach HaKodesh (feminine) are named and adored in the platform's framing per the grounding. The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy. The Hebrew article rule holds throughout (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Ecclesiastes-moment song the aches the searcher can feel but not resolve — the eternity (*olam*) set in the heart that no man can find out (3:11), the judgment Elohim will bring on every work and every hidden thing (12:14), the spirit that returns to the Elohim who gave it (12:7) — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. No Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment inside a song; the framing adores Yeshua, and the songs, standing inside Qoheleth's own hour, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Ayin keyed to *olam* (the eye that sees the whole set in the heart), the Hey keyed to *hevel* (the breath the whole book cannot hold). Qoheleth, the Preacher, and Shlomo are *witnessed* and testified to from heaven's side, never made the singer; the meditation is sung *about*, never voiced as the searcher's own first person. The sun that hurries back, the rivers to the sea never full, the threefold cord, the silver cord, the golden bowl, the pitcher broken at the well, and the grasshopper that drags itself are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — two better than one, a cord of three strands, "a time to… and a time to," a portion to seven and also to eight.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5) — a reflective, minor-keyed register for the vapor of Album I, resolving only at the conclusion of Album II.
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
