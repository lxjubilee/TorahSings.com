# DEUTERONOMY — THE NINE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Second Telling

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Leviticus - The Seven Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Deuteronomy is Mosheh's own farewell speeches on the plains of Moab, so every song is one narrative or discourse unit drawn at the text's own seams — the speech openings ("These are the words…", "And Mosheh called…", "Hear, O Israel…"), and the clear shifts between the historical review, the great exhortations, the statute-and-judgment code, the blessings and curses, the covenant renewal, the song, the blessing, and the death. The dense law code (roughly 12–26) is grouped into coherent thematic units rather than split verse by verse.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the speeches, toward the Son promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own place in Scripture, with no New-Testament hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Deuteronomy-moment song the prophet like Mosheh yet to come, the heart Yahuah Himself will circumcise, the word that is very near, the man hung on the tree and called a curse, the choice of life are carried as mystery and ache, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (above all not Mosheh, whose whole book this is — he is witnessed, never the singer) or anything derived from one; impersonal creation the text supplies (the two mountains Gerizim and Ebal, the manna, the plastered stones, the ark, the written song, Mount Nebo) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. The Angels overhear these speeches and testify to what Mosheh said and what it cost him. We overhear them — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Nine albums. Fifty-six songs. No honest zeros — every narrative and discourse unit of Deuteronomy is sung. Every chapter accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE ELEVEN-DAY ROAD TOOK FORTY YEARS
### The Road Behind · Deuteronomy 1–4 · eight songs

Before he gives them a single new command, Mosheh turns the whole nation around to look at the road behind them. It is a road that should have taken eleven days and took forty years — a generation buried in the sand because they called deliverance hatred and refused a land they had already been shown was good. The witnesses sing the review from heaven's side: the burden divided among judges, the slander at Kadesh, the long circling past the kindred nations, the two giant-kings brought down, the leader told he may bring them to the river but never cross it, and the great cry that closes the first speech — *you saw no form; there is none else.*

*Why this title:* Deuteronomy 1:2 measures Horeb to Kadesh-barnea as eleven days' journey, and the review that follows is the reckoning of why those eleven days became forty years of graves.

# ALBUM II — THESE WORDS UPON YOUR HEART
### The Great Commands and the Shema · Deuteronomy 5–11 · seven songs

The second speech does not begin with new law but with a claim that collapses time: the covenant of Horeb was cut *not with our fathers, but with us, we who are alive here today.* Then comes the heart of the whole Torah — *Hear, O Israel, Yahuah our Elohim, Yahuah is one* — and the command to love Him with everything and lay these words on the heart, teach them, bind them, write them on the door. The witnesses sing the great commands and the great remembering: chosen not for number but for love, fed to be taught, spared in spite of the calf, and told to circumcise the heart the flesh cannot reach — a longing the Angels carry unresolved.

*Why this title:* the album's spine is the Shema (6) — love Yahuah and lay these words upon the heart — the phrase that threads forward to the heart that must be circumcised (10) and the word that will be very near the heart (30).

# ALBUM III — THE PLACE HE CHOSE TO PUT HIS NAME
### The Law of Worship · Deuteronomy 12–16:17 · six songs

An Elohim who is everywhere insists on one place. The law of worship opens by tearing down every hilltop shrine and gathering all offering to the single place Yahuah will choose to set His Name — and then guards that worship from every seduction, even a beloved's whisper, even a sign that comes true. The witnesses sing a set-apart people learning what may pass their lips, what comes off the top of the harvest, when debts are released and slaves go free, what is given back to Yahuah, and the three feasts that turn the whole nation toward His place three times a year.

*Why this title:* chapters 12–16 gather all worship to the one place Yahuah chooses to put His Name — the high places torn down, the feasts, the tithe, and the firstborn all carried there.

# ALBUM IV — JUSTICE, JUSTICE YOU SHALL PURSUE
### The Law of Authority and Justice · Deuteronomy 16:18–20 · seven songs

Once worship is settled, the law builds a nation of righteous authority — and hedges every office with limits. Judges in every gate take no bribe; a court exists whose verdict may not be defied; a king may sit only under the scroll he copies out in his own hand; a tribe is given no land because it is given Yahuah Himself; a prophet like Mosheh is promised and left aching, unfulfilled. The witnesses sing justice pursued into the smallest case — the refuge reached before the avenger's blood runs hot, the liar caught in his own scheme, the fruit tree spared even in siege.

*Why this title:* Deuteronomy 16:20 doubles the word — *tzedek, tzedek tirdof*, justice, justice you shall pursue — and the whole album is that pursuit through judge, king, priest, prophet, and war.

# ALBUM V — DO NOT HIDE YOURSELF
### The Household Under Law · Deuteronomy 21–22 · five songs

The law now reaches into the household and the body, and forbids Israel to look away. It will not let a murder go unanswered though no one knows the killer; it gives a captive woman a month to weep before she is taken, and freedom rather than sale if she is unloved; it takes a corpse down from the tree before nightfall; it sends the mother bird from the nest before the young are taken; it hears the cry that the open field could not. The witnesses sing a people commanded never to hide themselves — from blood no one claims, from a neighbor's stray ox, from the smallest life on the road.

*Why this title:* Deuteronomy 22 commands, three times, that Israel may not "hide itself" from a brother's need — and chapters 21–22 are that refusal to look away, from unclaimed blood to a bird on the nest.

# ALBUM VI — BECAUSE YOU WERE STRANGERS ONCE
### The Assembly, the Poor, and the Sealed Covenant · Deuteronomy 23–26 · six songs

Every mercy in these chapters rests on a single memory: *you were slaves, you were strangers.* So the assembly of Yahuah opens, by the third generation, even to the children of Egypt; a runaway slave is sheltered, not returned; a poor man's cloak comes back to him by nightfall so he can sleep and bless; the corner of the field is left for the sojourner. The witnesses sing the law's long tenderness toward the outsider and the vulnerable — and its final turn, when a redeemed people lifts a basket of firstfruits and confesses that its own father was a wandering Aramean, and the whole code is sealed with a mutual vow.

*Why this title:* chapters 23–26 ground their mercies again and again in one motive — do not abhor the Egyptian, restore the pledge, leave the gleanings, bring the firstfruits — *because you were a stranger, because you were a slave.*

# ALBUM VII — BLESSING ON GERIZIM, CURSE ON EBAL
### The Two Mountains · Deuteronomy 27–28 · five songs

The covenant is staged between two facing mountains. On plastered stones the whole law is written out plainly; an altar of unhewn stones rises on Ebal; six tribes stand on Gerizim to bless and six on Ebal to curse, and the people answer *Amen* over their own curses. Then the blessings, which chase down the obedient like a pursuer — and the curses, which sink from a sky turned to bronze all the way to ships sailing back to the house of bondage, the going-out run backward. The witnesses sing the mountains face to face, and the Amen that binds a nation to both.

*Why this title:* Deuteronomy 27:12–13 sets the ceremony exactly so — the blessing proclaimed from Gerizim, the curse from Ebal — two mountains facing each other across the valley, with the law on stone and the people's Amen between them.

# ALBUM VIII — CHOOSE LIFE
### The Covenant in Moab · Deuteronomy 29–30 · five songs

A third covenant is cut, in the land of Moab, besides the one at Horeb — and it binds not only those standing there but those who are not yet born. Mosheh warns of the root that bears gall, and of a future so veiled that the secret things must be left to Yahuah. Then the turn toward hope: after the scattering, a return; a heart Yahuah Himself will circumcise; a word not far off in heaven or across the sea, but very near, in the mouth and the heart. And the whole book gathers to a single command, with heaven and earth summoned as witnesses — *I have set before you life and death; choose life.*

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine); the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms; *Ha'azinu* and *Shema* stand as the text's own words, never with a stacked English "the"). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Deuteronomy-moment song the messianic aches — the prophet like Mosheh yet to come, the heart Yahuah Himself will circumcise, the word that is very near, the man hung on the tree and called a curse of Elohim, the choice of life — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. The framing names Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour on the plains of Moab, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter. Mosheh above all is *witnessed, never made the singer* — his whole book is his speeches, and the Angels overhear and testify to what he said and what it cost him; Yehoshua, the elders, the judges, and the king are likewise witnessed only. The two mountains, the plastered stones, the manna, the ark, the written song, and Mount Nebo are invoked as images, never voiced. Where a title carries a first-person line ("I kill, and I make alive"), it is Yahuah's own word within the Song, sung in praise by the witnesses — never a human's first person.
- **The uncrossed river:** the ache of the leader who may bring them to the border but never cross it recurs by the text's own repetition (3:23–29; 31:2; 32:52; 34:4) — each song sounds it from a distinct angle, and it lands only at the death; this is fidelity to Deuteronomy's own refrain, not a repeated title formula.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae — the Shin and Dalet the scribes enlarged in the Shema until they spell "witness," the Tsade of *tzedek*, the Tav of *Torah*, the Zayin of *zakhor*, the Pey of the near word, the Hey of *Ha'azinu*, the Samekh that upholds — with no total ever stated and no gematria mechanism sung. The text's own numbers (the eleven days, the forty years, the seventy souls, the two and a half tribes, the three cities of refuge, the forty stripes, the seventh year) are content.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
