# LEVITICUS — THE SEVEN ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of Drawing Near

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md` and `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Leviticus is law more than narrative, so every song is one ritual or narrative unit drawn at the text's own seams — the speech formulas ("And Yahuah spoke unto Mosheh, saying" · "And Yahuah called unto Mosheh") and the ritual-instruction blocks that structure the book in place of the Genesis *toledot*: each offering type, each purity domain, each feast cluster, each release.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the ritual, toward the Son promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own place in Scripture, with no New-Testament hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Leviticus-moment song the substitute dying under a pressed hand, the sin carried outside the camp, the goat that bears it all away, the blood that covers, the once-a-year entry behind the veil are carried as mystery and ache, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Mosheh, not Aharon, not the priest) or anything derived from one; impersonal creation the text supplies (the altar fire, the blood, the scapegoat, the two birds, the incense, the lamp, the Jubilee trumpet) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Seven albums. Thirty-two songs. No honest zeros — every ritual and narrative unit of Leviticus is sung. Every chapter accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE ONLY WAY TO COME NEAR
### The Five Offerings · Leviticus 1–7 · six songs

Between a holy Elohim and an unclean people stands a door, and the only way through it is a gift that costs — a life laid down under a hand pressed to its head, a field's flour, a shared meal, blood daubed on the horns of the altar, restitution paid with a fifth added. Five offerings, five ways to draw near, and the witnesses keep the smoke-count as gift after gift ascends. Over it all a fire the priests must never let die — the flame heaven itself will kindle on the eighth day.

*Why this title:* *korban*, the word for every offering, means *that which is brought near*; chapters 1–7 are the whole grammar of approach — the only way an unclean people comes near a holy Elohim.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Whole of It Went Up** | Leviticus 1 | The Angels, watching the smoke ascend | The *olah* — the only offering wholly burned, nothing returned to the giver; a hand laid on the head so the beast ascends in his place, all of it rising as a soothing aroma, total surrender made visible in smoke |
| 2 | **No Blood, Only Flour and Oil** | Leviticus 2 | The Angels (the grain invoked as image) | The one bloodless gift — fine flour, oil and frankincense, salt of the covenant, no leaven and no honey; a handful burned as a memorial, the rest feeding the priests — a way for the empty-handed and the poor to still come near |
| 3 | **They Ate Before His Face** | Leviticus 3 | The Angels, who do not eat, watching mortals share a table with the Holy | The *shelamim* — the fat and the blood given up, but the flesh handed back to the worshiper to eat in the presence; the covenant meal, peace made edible, the one offering that ends in a feast |
| 4 | **Carried Outside the Camp to Burn** | Leviticus 4:1–5:13 | The Chet — the breach in the wall — with the Angels | Sin done unwittingly, discovered, atoned; blood daubed on the horns and sprinkled before the veil, the fat to the altar, and the body of the sin-bearer taken outside the camp to be consumed — the poor bringing two birds or a handful of flour; the fault lifted off one and carried out |
| 5 | **Restore It, and Add a Fifth** | Leviticus 5:14–6:7 | The Angels | The *asham* — trespass against the holy or against a neighbor, and the strange arithmetic of repentance: return what was taken and add a fifth, then bring the ram; guilt repaid to man before it is covered before Yahuah |
| 6 | **The Fire Must Never Go Out** | Leviticus 6:8–7:38 | The Angels (the altar fire invoked as image) | The priests' own torah of every offering — the burnt offering left on the hearth all night, the fire fed each morning and never let die; the portions of the most holy things, the fat and the blood forbidden as food; a perpetual flame waiting for heaven to light it |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the five offerings run in the text's own order — the ascending *olah*, the bloodless grain-gift, the shared peace-meal, the sin borne outside the camp, the guilt repaid — the people's approach in chapters 1–5; then the priests' torah of the same offerings closes the album, and with it the fire that must never go out, a flame the next album will watch fall from heaven.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the *olah* · 2 the *minchah* · 3 the *shelamim* · 4:1–5:13 the *chatat* · 5:14–6:7 the *asham* · 6:8–7:38 the priests' torah of the offerings. Six for six.

# ALBUM II — THE SAME FIRE BLESSED AND KILLED
### Ordination, the Fire That Fell and the Fire That Killed · Leviticus 8–10 · four songs

Seven days Aharon and his sons are washed and robed and bloodied and held at the door, and on the eighth the fire falls — comes out from before Yahuah and devours the offering while a whole nation shouts and drops to its face. Then the same fire, unbidden, comes out and devours two of the priests who brought what He never asked. The witnesses sing the terror folded inside the privilege — the flame that blesses and the flame that kills are one flame — and the father who lost his sons only held his peace.

*Why this title:* in chapter 9 the fire falls and consumes the offering in acceptance; in chapter 10 the same fire falls and consumes the priests in judgment — one fire, two verdicts.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Seven Days at the Door of the Tent** | Leviticus 8 | The Dalet — the door they could not pass — with the Angels | Aharon and his sons washed, robed, anointed, the ordination blood on ear and thumb and toe, their hands filled — then held seven days and nights at the entrance of the tent, forbidden to go out lest they die; the priesthood sealed by waiting |
| 2 | **Fire Came Out and the People Fell** | Leviticus 9 | The Shin — the fire — with the Angels | On the eighth day Aharon lifts his hands and blesses, the glory appears, and fire comes out from before Yahuah and devours the offering on the altar; and a whole nation shouts and falls on its face — the flame that will now never go out, kindled by heaven itself |
| 3 | **And Aharon Held His Peace** | Leviticus 10:1–7 | The Angels, who knew the two young priests | Nadab and Abihu bring strange fire He never commanded, and the same fire that had just blessed the offering comes out and devours them; the cousins carry the bodies out by their coats, no mourning permitted — and the father, in the ruin of it, holds his peace |
| 4 | **He Could Not Eat That Day** | Leviticus 10:8–20 | The Angels, who watched the grieving father | The wine set aside so the priest may tell holy from common; and the sin offering left uneaten, and Mosheh angry — until Aharon answers only, "such things have befallen me, would it have been good in the eyes of Yahuah?" — and Mosheh hears, and it is good; grief given a place inside the law |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the seven days of ordination open in confinement and dread; the eighth-day fire is the Unveiling — glory and flame and a nation on its face; the strange fire is the Turn, the same flame become judgment, and Aharon's silence the Selah; and the uneaten offering lands the album quietly, grief given room inside the law.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 8 the ordination · 9 the eighth day and the fire that fell · 10:1–7 the strange fire · 10:8–20 the sober priesthood and the uneaten sin offering. Four for four.

# ALBUM III — TO TELL THE CLEAN FROM THE UNCLEAN
### Clean and Unclean, All the Way to the Body · Leviticus 11–15 · six songs

The Elohim of the smoking mountain now bends to legislate the plate, the childbed, the skin, the sickroom, the bed of the unclean — holiness reaching all the way down into the body's most ordinary and most private hours. What may be eaten, what a new mother must wait through, the priest's long look at a bright spot, two birds over a healed leper, a plague that lives in a house's walls, the flows that bar a body from the tent. The witnesses watch a people learn, one washing at a time, to tell the clean from the unclean — lest their defilement touch the Holy and they die.

*Why this title:* the stated purpose of all five chapters (11:47) — to make a difference, to tell the clean from the unclean — pressed even into the flesh.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Split Hoof and the Cud** | Leviticus 11 | The Angels | The marks of the clean — the parted hoof, the raised cud, the fin and the scale — the swarming things an abomination; and at the end the reason given: be holy, for I am holy — even the plate a place of separation |
| 2 | **New Life Left Her Waiting Outside** | Leviticus 12 | The Angels, watching the mother | Birth, the most life-giving act, leaving her unclean — the days of separation and the blood of her purifying, the eighth-day sign on the child, and the offering that ends the waiting: a lamb, or, if her hand cannot reach one, two doves received in its place |
| 3 | **He Cried Unclean and Dwelt Alone** | Leviticus 13 | The Angels, watching the exiled | The priest's long looking, shut up seven days and seven more, and the verdict that empties a life — torn clothes, bare head, covered lip, "unclean, unclean," and a dwelling outside the camp, alone; even a garment with the plague in it burned |
| 4 | **One Bird Killed, One Let Go Free** | Leviticus 14:1–32 | The Angels (the two birds invoked as image) | The healed leper's cleansing — two birds, one killed over living water in a clay vessel, the other dipped in that blood and released alive into the open field, carrying the death away on its wings; the cleansed man washed, shaved, marked with blood and oil; one life given so another goes free |
| 5 | **The Plague Was in the Walls** | Leviticus 14:33–57 | The Angels | A house with the plague in its very stones — greenish, reddish streaks — shut up seven days, the infected stones torn out and carried outside the city, the walls scraped or the whole house pulled down; even a dwelling could be judged unclean |
| 6 | **Kept From the Tent, Lest They Die** | Leviticus 15 | The Mem — the waters of the body and the washing — with the Angels | The flows of the flesh, the bed and the seat that carry uncleanness, the washings and the counted days and the pair of birds at the end — and beneath it all the reason: keep the unclean from the dwelling of Yahuah in their midst, lest their defilement touch the Holy and they die |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the domains run in the text's order — the clean and unclean animals, the mother after childbirth, the tzaraat in skin and cloth, the two birds that cleanse the healed, the plague in the walls of a house, the discharges of the body — each a testing of what may approach; and the album lands on the reason beneath them all: keep the unclean from the tent, lest they die.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 11 clean and unclean animals · 12 purification after childbirth · 13 tzaraat in skin and garment · 14:1–32 the two birds · 14:33–57 the leprous house · 15 the discharges. Six for six.

# ALBUM IV — THE LIFE IS IN THE BLOOD
### The Day of Atonement and the Blood · Leviticus 16–17 · three songs

Once in the year, and no more, one man passes behind the veil into the cloud of incense with blood, on the single day the whole nation afflicts its soul. Two goats stand at the door: the lot falls, one is killed for Yahuah, and the other — hands pressed to its head, every sin confessed onto it — is led alive into the wilderness, to a land cut off, carrying it all away. The witnesses sing the blood brought within the veil and the life that is in it, and the ache that will not resolve — that a covering this costly must be made again next year, and the year after that.

*Why this title:* 17:11 is the key that unlocks the whole sacrificial book — the life of the flesh is in the blood, and it is the blood that covers the soul upon the altar.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Goat Carried It All Away** | Leviticus 16 | The Kaph — the covering palm, sounding *kaparah* — with the Angels | The one day of the year: the high priest in linen entering the cloud of incense behind the veil with blood for the mercy seat; two goats, the lots cast, one slain for Yahuah and one sent alive into the wilderness bearing all their iniquities to a land not inhabited — the people afflicting their souls, the ache of a covering renewed every single year |
| 2 | **No More Altars in the Field** | Leviticus 17:1–9 | The Angels | Every slaughtered ox and lamb and goat brought to the one door, no blood spilled in the open field, an end to the goat-demons they had whored after — all worship gathered to a single altar where the blood is handled rightly; the wildness of private sacrifice forbidden |
| 3 | **The Blood Was Never Theirs to Eat** | Leviticus 17:10–16 | The Angels (the blood invoked as image) | The life of the flesh is in the blood, and so it is never food — it is given for the altar, to cover the soul; the hunter's blood poured out and hidden under dust; the ache that only a life can stand for a life |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the Day of Atonement opens the album at the veil — the still, holy center of the whole book; then the altar centralized and the goat-demons forsaken; and the life in the blood lands it. Three songs, and no more, is the honest measure of this ground.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 16 the Day of Atonement, the two goats, the veil · 17:1–9 the one altar · 17:10–16 the life in the blood. Three for three.

# ALBUM V — BE HOLY, FOR I AM HOLY
### The Holiness Code · Leviticus 18–22 · five songs

Be holy, for I am holy — the refrain that turns a rescued mob into a set-apart people, and reaches into the marriage bed, the harvest corner, the wage held overnight, the gray head, the priest's own grief and body. The land itself is made a moral thing that grows sick and vomits out its defilers; a neighbor is to be loved as one's own self; a child is not to be passed through fire; and even the priests who serve the altar must be whole and undefiled to draw near. The witnesses sing a holiness that is not withdrawal but likeness — a people asked to resemble the One who separated them to be His own.

*Why this title:* 19:2 is the spine of the whole code — you shall be holy, for I, Yahuah your Elohim, am holy — the reason under every law from chapter 18 to 22.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Land Vomited Them Out** | Leviticus 18 | The Angels | The nakedness not to be uncovered, the practices of Egypt and Canaan forbidden, no child given to Molech — and the warning that the land itself grows sick and vomits out those who defile it, as it vomited those before; holiness written into the ground |
| 2 | **Love Your Neighbor as Yourself** | Leviticus 19 | The Qof — sounding *qadosh* — with the Angels | The harvest edge left uncut for the poor and the stranger, the wage not held overnight, the deaf not cursed and the blind not tripped, honest weights, the gray head honored — a hundred small holinesses, and at their heart the word that gathers them: love your neighbor as yourself |
| 3 | **Cut Off From Among the People** | Leviticus 20 | The Angels | The one who gives his seed to Molech, the mediums and the wizards, the sins that defile — and the sentence that severs a soul from the people; all of it sealed with the reason: I have set you apart from the nations to be mine |
| 4 | **No Blemish Draws Near the Altar** | Leviticus 21 | The Angels | The priests set apart even in grief — not defiled for the dead, the high priest not leaving the sanctuary even for his father or mother — and the blemished among Aharon's own sons kept back from the altar though still fed at the holy table; nearness demanding a wholeness no one fully owns |
| 5 | **Nothing Broken Could Be Given** | Leviticus 22 | The Angels | The holy things guarded from the unclean and the stranger's mouth, and the offering that must be whole — nothing blind or broken or maimed brought to the altar, none offered before the eighth day, a beast and its young not killed in one day; only the flawless received, an ache no herd could always meet |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the code runs in the text's order — the forbidden relations and the land that vomits; the great chapter of holiness crowned by the love of neighbor; the penalties that set a people apart; the holiness of the priests; and the unblemished offering that closes the album — a whole people, and its priests, asked to be whole.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 18 forbidden relations · 19 be holy, love your neighbor · 20 the penalties · 21 the holiness of the priests · 22 holy things and the unblemished offering. Five for five.

# ALBUM VI — HE SET TIMES TO MEET THEM
### The Appointed Times and the Broken Name · Leviticus 23–24 · three songs

Yahuah keeps a calendar — sets appointed times to meet His people, feast after feast from the Passover lamb to the booths where a settled nation camps like wanderers again to remember the going-out. Between the great days, a lamp that must never go dark and twelve loaves always before His face; and then, jarringly, the one story in this stretch — a man who curses the Name and is carried outside the camp, and the law of the exact wound given in answer. The witnesses sing time itself carved into meetings with heaven, and the terrible weight of a Name a careless mouth can break.

*Why this title:* *mo'ed* means an appointed meeting; chapter 23 is Yahuah setting standing appointments with His people — He set times to meet them.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **From Passover to the Booths** | Leviticus 23 | The Zayin — value seven, the sabbaths and the seventh month — with the Angels | The weekly Sabbath, then the year's appointed meetings — the Passover lamb, the unleavened bread, the first sheaf waved, the fifty-day count to the two loaves of Weeks, the trumpet opening the seventh month, the day of affliction, and the booths where a settled people camps like wanderers to remember the going-out; time carved into meetings with Yahuah |
| 2 | **Bread and Light That Never Went Dark** | Leviticus 24:1–9 | The Angels | Pure beaten oil kept burning from evening to morning, never dark; twelve cakes in two rows on the pure table, fresh every Sabbath with frankincense, bread always before Yahuah's face and food for the priests — the sanctuary's quiet rhythm, light and bread that never cease |
| 3 | **Eye for Eye, One Law for All** | Leviticus 24:10–23 | The Ayin — the eye, the measure — with the Angels | A man of mixed blood who cursed the Name in a camp quarrel, held in ward until Yahuah's word came, then taken outside the camp where all who heard laid their hands on his head and stoned him; and from it the law of the exact measure — life for life, eye for eye, tooth for tooth, one law binding stranger and native alike |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the appointed times open the album in their yearly sweep; the continual lamp and bread are the quiet daily holiness between the feasts; and the blasphemer — the rare narrative — is the Turn, a rupture in the holy order answered by the exact measure of the wound, one law for stranger and native alike.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 23 the appointed times · 24:1–9 the lamp and the bread · 24:10–23 the blasphemer and the law of the wound. Three for three.

# ALBUM VII — THE YEAR EVERYTHING WENT HOME
### Sabbath Year, Jubilee, and Covenant · Leviticus 25–27 · five songs

Every seventh year the land itself rests, and after seven sevens a trumpet sounds on the Day of Atonement and the whole nation goes home — every field back to its family, every debt undone, every bondservant freed, because the land is Yahuah's and they are only sojourners with Him. A near kinsman may always buy a fallen brother back; blessing follows those who walk with Him and a sky of iron those who walk against; and the book ends counting what a vowed life is worth in silver, and what is too devoted ever to be bought back. The witnesses sing release as the deepest law of all — a people who cannot finally belong to anyone but the One who redeemed them out of Egypt.

*Why this title:* the Jubilee returns every person and every field to its own — the fiftieth year when everything, and everyone, went home (25:10).

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Even the Land Kept Sabbath** | Leviticus 25:1–7 | The Angels | Six years of sowing, and the seventh a rest for the ground itself — no sowing, no pruning, no harvest hoarded; what the land yields untended feeding master and servant and stranger and wild beast alike; the very soil taught to trust Yahuah for a year |
| 2 | **The Horn That Freed the Land** | Leviticus 25:8–24 | The Nun — value fifty, the fiftieth year — with the Angels | Seven sabbaths of years counted, and then on the Day of Atonement a trumpet sounded through all the land, proclaiming liberty to every inhabitant — each home to his own field and family, the ground unsold forever because the land is Yahuah's and they are but sojourners with Him; a nation's ledger wiped clean by a horn |
| 3 | **A Kinsman Bought Him Back** | Leviticus 25:25–55 | The Gimel — the foot that lifts, sounding *ga'al* — with the Angels | The brother fallen into poverty, his field sold, himself sold — and always the right of a near kinsman to redeem him back, no interest taken, no brother made a permanent slave, all set free at the fiftieth year; because they are Yahuah's own servants, redeemed from Egypt, they can belong to no other |
| 4 | **Rain, or a Sky of Iron** | Leviticus 26 | The Angels | Walk in His statutes and the rains come, the land yields, He walks among them as their Elohim; refuse, and the sky turns to iron and the ground to bronze, the land lies desolate enjoying its lost sabbaths, the people scattered — yet if they confess their iniquity, He remembers Yaʿaqov and Avraham and will not cast them wholly away |
| 5 | **What a Life Was Worth in Silver** | Leviticus 27 | The Lamed — the shepherd's rod under which the tenth passes — with the Angels | The priest's valuation of a vowed life in shekels by age and strength, the poor priced by what his hand can reach, fields and houses redeemed by adding a fifth — but the firstborn already Yahuah's, and the devoted thing never bought back at all, and the tenth passing under the rod, holy to Yahuah; the strange arithmetic of what belongs to heaven |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* release builds through the album in the text's order — the land's own sabbath, the trumpet of the fiftieth year, the kinsman who buys a brother back; then the blessing and the curse frame it all with the cost of the covenant; and the valuations land the whole book, counting what belongs to Yahuah and what may be redeemed — Leviticus ending, as it lived, on the question of what a life is worth to heaven.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 25:1–7 the land's sabbath · 25:8–24 the Jubilee · 25:25–55 the kinsman-redeemer · 26 the blessing and the curse · 27 vows and valuations. Five for five.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine); the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Leviticus-moment song the messianic aches — the substitute dying under a pressed hand, the sin carried outside the camp to burn, the goat that bears it all away into the wilderness, the two birds (one slain, one set free), the blood that covers the soul, the once-a-year entry behind the veil, the kinsman who redeems — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. The framing names Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter. Mosheh, Aharon, the priests, the mother, the leper, the blasphemer are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the altar fire, the blood, the scapegoat, the two birds, the incense, the lamp, and the Jubilee trumpet are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters appear as personas keyed honestly to pictograph or value (the Chet the breach, the Kaph the covering palm, the Ayin the eye of "eye for eye," the Nun the fiftieth year, the Zayin the sevens, the Gimel the redeemer who lifts) — their total number is never stated and no gematria mechanism is sung; only the text's own numbers (the seven days, the eighth day, the two goats, the fiftieth year) carry weight.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
