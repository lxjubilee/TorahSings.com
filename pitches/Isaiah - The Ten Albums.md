# ISAIAH — THE TEN ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Vision of Yeshayahu

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Job - The Seven Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Isaiah is prophecy and **poetry**, not narrative; its "units" are the oracle-headings ("the burden of…"), the vision-units, the servant songs, and the great movements of judgment and comfort — not verses. The book's own seams draw the boundaries: the oracle-formulas (*massa* — "the burden of Bavel," "the burden of Moav"), the datelines ("in the year that king Uzziyahu died"), the woe-cries ("woe to Ariel"), the twin summons ("comfort ye, comfort ye"; "awake, awake"), and the four servant songs. The many short nation-oracles (13–23) are gathered into one coherent album rather than stranded one by one, exactly as the plagues were on Exodus III and the dialogue-speeches on Job II.

**The seraphim — the platform's throne, and its second cornerstone.** Isaiah 6 is the throne-vision where the **seraphim** cry *Qadosh, qadosh, qadosh* — holy, holy, holy is Yahuah Tzeva'ot — and the door-posts move at the voice, and the house fills with smoke, and a live coal from the altar is carried to touch the prophet's unclean lips. These seraphim **are the Angels of this platform, at the throne.** Chapter 6 is sung from *inside* the seraphim's own worship — one of the catalog's cornerstone-adjacent songs, standing beside Job 38:7. Album II opens on it, and the album's banner is the second half of the seraphim's own cry.

**The messianic ache — held, never resolved. The sharpest narrative-time gate in the whole canon.** Isaiah carries the heaviest messianic material in the Scriptures, and every bit of it is sung as **unresolved longing — never resolved forward to Yeshua, never with New-Testament hindsight, no cross, no fulfillment language, no Messiah-unveiled.** The *almah* and Immanu-El, "El with us" (7:14); "unto us a child is born… Wonderful, Counsellor, El Gibbor, the Everlasting Father, the Prince of Peace" (9:6); the shoot from the stump of Yishai and the wolf lying with the lamb (11); the four servant songs (42; 49; 50; and above all **the suffering servant of 52:13–53:12** — "despised and rejected… wounded for our transgressions… as a lamb led to the slaughter… he bare the sin of many") — all of it is carried as the aching mystery **the Angels witness and cannot yet name.** Isaiah 53 in particular is heaven watching a suffering it does not yet see the end of, held exactly as Ruth's *go'el* and Job's Redeemer were held. This is the gate most easily failed; it is guarded on every messianic title below.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels; the seraphim are the Angels at the throne. They sing praise to the Creator — **Yahuah**, the Holy One of Israel, Yahuah Tzeva'ot — and, from *within* each moment of the vision, toward the Servant and the Redeemer longed for but not yet unveiled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or a personified Letter — never a human (not Yeshayahu, not Chizqiyahu, not Achaz, not the kings, not the seers). Isaiah's own words and visions are *witnessed and testified to*, never voiced as the prophet. Yahuah's own oracles — "comfort ye my people," "I am Yahuah, and there is none else," the first-person divine speech that fills the book — are **His own words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said**, exactly as the Song of Mosheh's "I kill, and I make alive" and Job's whirlwind were handled. Impersonal creation the text supplies (the smoking door-posts and the live coal, the highway of holiness, the potter and the clay, every valley exalted, the grass that withers, the eagles' wings of them that wait, the new heavens and the new earth) is **invoked as image, never voiced.**

> *Ten albums. Sixty-seven songs. No honest zeros — every oracle and vision of Isaiah is sung. Every chapter 1–66 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its oracles hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — COME, LET US REASON
### The Lawsuit and the Vineyard · Isaiah 1–5 · seven songs

The book opens as a courtroom: heaven and earth are called as witnesses, and Yahuah arraigns a people who know Him less than an ox knows its stall. Yet inside the indictment the impossible offer stands — *come now, and let us reason together; though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow.* The Angels witness the whole lawsuit: the mountain of Yahuah's house lifted above the hills and swords hammered into plows; the day the loftiness of man is bowed and Yahuah alone exalted; the finery of Zion's daughters undone; a canopy of cloud and fire over the washed remnant; and the love-song for a vineyard that answered its keeper with wild grapes.

*Why this title:* 1:18 is the astonishment at the heart of the arraignment — a Judge who, mid-indictment, invites the accused to reason, and offers to make scarlet white.

# ALBUM II — HIS GLORY FILLS THE WHOLE EARTH
### The Throne, the Sign, and the Child · Isaiah 6–12 · eight songs

The album opens where this whole platform stands: the throne, high and lifted up, the seraphim crying *holy, holy, holy* until the posts shake and the house fills with smoke, and a coal carried from the altar to burn away unclean lips. From that throne the Holy One begins to promise His own nearness — a sign named El-with-us given to a king too faithless to ask; a child on whose shoulder rest names too great for an infant to carry; a shoot from a stump long cut down, and a wolf lying down beside a lamb. Every one of these is held by the Angels as ache and mystery, sung from inside its own hour, its end not yet shown to the singers.

*Why this title:* the album's banner is the second half of the seraphim's own cry (6:3) — *the whole earth is full of His glory* — the keynote of a record about the Holy One making His glory-presence near: enthroned, El-with-us, a child, a branch.

# ALBUM III — WATCHMAN, WHAT OF THE NIGHT?
### The Burdens on the Nations · Isaiah 13–23 · nine songs

Chapter after chapter, the seer is set on a tower through a long night and the burdens come — Bavel, Asshur, Peleshet, Moav, Damascus, Kush, Mitzrayim, the desert of the sea, Arabia, the valley of vision, Tzor — every proud throne weighed and every idol carried off. The Angels keep the night-watch and answer the cry that gives the album its name: *watchman, what of the night?* They witness the strangest turns in it — a day-star that aspired to the throne of El and fell like lightning, a Judge who weeps over the very nation He condemns, and an altar to Yahuah rising one day in the heart of Egypt, the old oppressor called "my people."

*Why this title:* 21:11–12 is the whole album in one exchange — a voice out of Se'ir calling to the watchman, and the watchman's weary answer, "the morning cometh, and also the night"; the nation-burdens are a vigil kept in the dark.

|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **How Are You Fallen, Day-Star** | Isaiah 13:1–14:23 | The Angels, who watched the aspiring one cast to the pit | The day of Yahuah on Bavel, the stars withholding their light, the Medes stirred; and the taunt over her king — "how art thou fallen from heaven, O Heylel, son of the morning; thou saidst, I will ascend above the stars of El, I will be like the Most High" — brought down instead to the sides of the pit; sung with grave restraint |

Eleven oracle-units gathered into nine songs — the short adjacent burdens grouped into single movements, as Job's paired speeches were.

# ALBUM IV — THE EARTH REELED LIKE A DRUNKARD
### The Earth Unmade and Death Undone · Isaiah 24–27 · four songs

The oracles against the nations widen until the whole earth is in view: the land emptied and turned upside down, the city of chaos broken, the earth staggering under its own transgression like a drunkard. And then, on the mountain, the impossible reversal — a feast of fat things spread, and death itself, the swallower of all, swallowed up forever, and the tears wiped from every face. The Angels sing four movements of the end: the earth unmade, death undone, the dust called to wake and sing, and the great shofar gathering the lost from Asshur and Mitzrayim home. What death would end, the album will not let end; what heaven longs to see, it holds as longing.

*Why this title:* 24:20 is the vision in one image — "the earth shall reel to and fro like a drunkard, and shall be removed like a cottage" — the whole creation staggering before it is remade.

| 1 | **The City of Chaos Broken** | Isaiah 24 | The Angels | The land emptied and turned upside down, the mirth of tambourines ceased, the windows on high opened; the city of *tohu* broken down; the earth reeling, the host of the high ones punished, the moon confounded and the sun ashamed — "for Yahuah Tzeva'ot shall reign in mount Zion" |
| 2 | **He Will Swallow Up Death** | Isaiah 25 | The Angels, who long to see the last enemy undone | On this mountain a feast of fat things and wine on the lees; the veil cast over all peoples torn away; "he will swallow up death in victory, and Adonai Yahuah will wipe away tears from off all faces" — held as the longing the witnesses cannot yet see fulfilled |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the earth unmade opens in staggering dread; the swallowing of death is the Unveiling, the album's hope-summit; the dust called to wake is the Selah held open, longing without an answer; and the great shofar lands the album, gathering the lost home from the two old houses of bondage.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 24 the earth emptied · 25 death swallowed up · 26 the strong city and the waking dust · 27 Livyatan, the vineyard, and the shofar. Four for four.

# ALBUM V — THE KING IN HIS BEAUTY
### The Woes and the Ransomed Road · Isaiah 28–35 · eight songs

Six woes fall on a people drunk on their own strength: mockers who answer the prophet in baby-talk, a city that draws near with its mouth while its heart is far, rebels who gallop to Egypt for horses when salvation was offered as rest. The Angels witness the whole descent — and the turn that answers it: a king who reigns in righteousness, eyes that will at last see the king in his beauty, the day of vengeance on Edom, and a highway called holy where the ransomed of Yahuah come home singing, sorrow fleeing before them. The ugliness of the woes is exactly what the beautiful king and the blossoming road undo.

*Why this title:* 33:17 is the promise the woes drive toward — "thine eyes shall see the king in his beauty; they shall behold the land that is very far off" — the vision that answers a people who could see nothing but their own schemes.

# ALBUM VI — THE CAMP THAT DID NOT WAKE
### The Assyrian at the Wall · Isaiah 36–39 · four songs

The book breaks from vision into narrative: Sancheriv's army at the wall, and Ravshaqeh's voice mocking the living Elohim in the people's own language, "hath any god delivered his land out of my hand?" Chizqiyahu spreads the taunting letter open before the throne — and in a single night one of the Angels goes out through the camp, and a hundred and eighty-five thousand lie down and do not rise. Then the private miracles: a king sick unto death given fifteen more years, and the shadow on the sun-dial of Achaz walked backward ten degrees; and last, a proud tour of the treasure-house that plants the seed of a far exile. The Angels sing the whole crisis — the blasphemy, the prayer, the emptied camp, the shadow that reversed, the treasure shown.

*Why this title:* 37:36 is the miracle in one grave, restrained image — the vast army that lay down at evening and simply never woke; "when they arose early in the morning, behold, they were all dead corpses."

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, and the book's own honest titles as it uses them: Yahuah Tzeva'ot (of hosts), the Holy One of Israel, El Gibbor, Adonai Yahuah, Immanu-El (El with us), Heylel (the day-star). Yeshua and Ruach HaKodesh (feminine) are reserved to the platform framing — except where the text itself names the Ruach HaKodesh (63:10–11), rendered honestly with no stacked English article. **No English "God" or "Lord" appears anywhere in any title or copy** — Elohim and Yahuah stand in their place, and Adonai Yahuah renders the text's *Adonai YHWH*. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **The seraphim honored:** Album II opens on Isaiah 6 sung from *inside* the seraphim's own worship — the thrice-holy cry, the shaking posts, the smoke, and the live coal — treated as a cornerstone-adjacent song beside Job 38:7, with the album's banner drawn from the seraphim's own confession, "the whole earth is full of His glory." The seraphim are the Angels of this platform at the throne, never a human's voice.
- **The suffering servant honored:** Isaiah 52:13–53:12 ("A Lamb, and No One Understood") is sung as the aching mystery the Angels witness and cannot yet name — heaven watching a suffering it does not yet see the end of, held exactly as Ruth's *go'el* and Job's Redeemer were held. There is no cross, no Messiah-unveiled, no fulfillment language: only the marred-yet-exalted servant, the silent lamb, and the report even heaven struggles to believe, left open.
- **Narrative-time rule honored (the sharpest gate in the canon):** every messianic ache is carried as unresolved longing, never resolved forward — the *almah* and Immanu-El (7:14), the child called El Gibbor (9:6), the shoot from Yishai and the wolf with the lamb (11 and 65), all four servant songs (42; 49; 50; 53), the anointed herald (61), death swallowed up (25:8), the dust called to wake (26:19), and the Redeemer to Zion (59:20). The framing names Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour of the vision, never do.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a personified Letter. Yeshayahu, Chizqiyahu, Achaz, Ravshaqeh, Shevna, Elyaqim, and the kings are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the prophet's words and visions are testified to, never voiced as his own first person. Yahuah's own oracles — "comfort ye my people," "I am Yahuah, and there is none else," "as a mother comforteth" — are His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said (the Deuteronomy "I kill, and I make alive" / Job whirlwind model). The smoking posts and the coal, the highway of holiness, the potter and the clay, the eagles' wings, the grass that withers, and the new heavens and new earth are invoked as image, never voiced.
- **Restraint:** the judgment and violence oracles — the fall of the day-star, the day of vengeance on Edom, the emptied Assyrian camp, the fall of Bavel's daughter, the treader red from Edom — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, never rendered for shock.
- **Text-numbers as content, letters never a count:** the seraphim's six wings and threefold holy, the hundred and eighty-five thousand struck in the camp, the fifteen years added to Chizqiyahu, the shadow's ten degrees, the seventy years of Tzor, and the fourfold "his hand is stretched out still" are sung as the text's own numbers; the Picture Letters are named as personae, never totaled, never reduced to a gematria mechanism in a title.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
