# HOSEA — THE TWO ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Covenant Love

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`, `pitches/Song of Songs - The Album.md`, and `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Hosea is fourteen chapters and it is the first of the Twelve. Its "units" are the enacted sign and its named children (1–3), then the covenant lawsuit (*rib*) and the oracles of unfaithfulness and love (4–14) — drawn at the book's own seams: the sign-commands ("go, take unto thee a wife"; "go yet, love a woman"), the naming-formulas ("call his name…"), the lawsuit-openings ("Yahuah hath a controversy with the inhabitants of the land"), the return-cries ("come, and let us return"), and the great first-person love-speeches that fill the book. The book splits near chapter 3/4 into two records: the living sign, then what the sign was for.

**The sign, and the restraint (the whole reason this book must be handled the way Song of Songs was).** Hoshea is told to marry **Gomer**, an unfaithful wife, and to name their three children as oracles against a nation — **Yizre'el** ("El sows/scatters"), **Lo-Ruchamah** ("not pitied"), and **Lo-Ammi** ("not my people") — and then to watch those very names reversed to mercy: **Ruchamah** ("pitied") and **Ammi** ("my people"). The marriage is the enacted allegory of **Yahuah's covenant love for unfaithful Israel.** The marriage-to-an-unfaithful-wife metaphor is rendered exactly as the Song was: **grave, family-friendly restraint — the covenant grief and love sung from heaven's side at a high, chaste altitude, no sensual or graphic detail.** The heart of the book is the tenderness of the wronged Husband: "How shall I give thee up, Ephrayim?… mine heart is turned within me, my repentings are kindled together" (11:8) — and the album-arc bends toward it.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator — **Yahuah** — and, from *within* each moment of the book, toward the redemption longed for but not yet unveiled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or a personified Letter — never a human (not Hoshea, not Gomer, not the priests). The prophet's marriage and the children's naming are **witnessed and testified to** — the Angels sing *about* the sign, never voice Hoshea or Gomer. Yahuah's own first-person love-speech, which fills the book — "I will allure her into the wilderness, and speak to her heart" (2:14); "How shall I give thee up?" (11:8); "I will heal their backsliding, I will love them freely" (14:4) — is **His own words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said** (the Deuteronomy / Job / Isaiah model). Impersonal creation the text supplies (the morning cloud and the early dew that pass away, the trained heifer, the silly dove without heart, the empty vine, the lily and the roots like Levanon, the east wind, the moth and the lion) is **invoked as image, never voiced.**

**Narrative-time — the messianic ache held, never resolved forward.** No New-Testament hindsight anywhere. The book's three great aches are carried within their own hour: **"out of Egypt I called my son"** (11:1); **"after two days will he revive us, in the third day he will raise us up"** (6:2); and **"O death, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction; I will ransom them from the power of Sheol"** (13:14) — the resurrection and redemption longing, sung as mystery the witnesses have just overheard and cannot yet name. No Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment language inside a song. The book's own hope is the **reversal of the names** — Lo-Ruchamah → Ruchamah, Lo-Ammi → Ammi — and that mercy-reversal is sung in full.

> *Two albums. Fifteen songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Hosea is sung. All fourteen chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories and oracles hold, one per unit and never a split to pad. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — NOT MY PEOPLE, THEN MY PEOPLE
### The Living Sign and the Reversed Names · Hosea 1–3 · four songs

A prophet is told to do the one thing a prophet should not: marry a wife who will betray him, and name their children as verdicts against a nation — Yizre'el, "El scatters"; Lo-Ruchamah, "not pitied"; Lo-Ammi, "not my people." The marriage is not a scandal to be dwelt on but a sign to be read: this is what Israel has done to her Husband, sung from heaven's side and kept chaste at every line. Then the sign turns — the wronged Husband alluring the wanderer back into the wilderness to speak to her heart, betrothing her to Himself for ever, striking the "not" clean off the children's names, and buying the woman back with silver and barley as though she were a slave. The Angels and the Letters witness the whole enacted allegory: a love commanded toward betrayal, and a mercy that renames the condemned.

*Why this title:* the album runs the arc of the third child's name in the book's own words — from the verdict "ye are not my people" (Lo-Ammi, 1:9) to the reversal "thou art my people" (Ammi, 2:23) — the sentence and its overturning, which is the whole meaning of the sign.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Three Names Against a Nation** | Hosea 1 | The Angels, who heard three children named as verdicts (Hoshea and Gomer witnessed, never voiced) | The sign begins: a wife of whoredoms taken at command, and three children named as oracles — Yizre'el, "El scatters"; Lo-Ruchamah, "not pitied"; Lo-Ammi, "not my people" — each naming a sentence on Israel; and yet, at the close, the "yet" — "ye shall be called the sons of the living El" (1:10) |
| 2 | **She Did Not Know Who Fed Her** | Hosea 2:2–13 | The Angels, who saw the wronged Husband's provision credited to her lovers | The covenant lawsuit against the mother, sung at a chaste altitude — she ran after lovers for bread and wine and oil, "and she did not know that I gave her the corn, and the wine, and the oil" (2:8); so He hedges her way with thorns to turn her home (2:6), grief before judgment |
| 3 | **The 'Not' Fell From Their Names** | Hosea 2:14–23 | The Letters, who watched the negating word struck off the names — with the Vav, the binding "and" of the eternal betrothal | "I will allure her, and bring her into the wilderness, and speak to her heart" (2:14); "I will betroth thee unto me for ever… in righteousness, and in judgment, and in lovingkindness… and thou shalt know Yahuah" (2:19–20); and the reversal — Lo-Ruchamah becomes Ruchamah, "I will have mercy"; Lo-Ammi becomes Ammi, "thou art my people" (2:23); the valley of Achor a door of hope |
| 4 | **Bought Back for Silver and Barley** | Hosea 3 | The Angels, who watched a redeemer pay for his own | "Go yet, love a woman… according to the love of Yahuah toward the children of Israel" (3:1) — the wife bought back for fifteen pieces of silver and a homer and a half of barley (3:2), love at the price of a slave; "thou shalt abide for me many days… so will I also be for thee" (3:3); the children of Israel to abide many days, then "return, and seek Yahuah their Elohim, and David their king" (3:5), held as longing |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the naming opens the album under sentence — the Veil, the sign stated and the verdict pronounced, with the first flash of "yet"; the lawsuit against the mother is the covenant grief, provision spurned; the allurement and the betrothal-for-ever and the striking of the "not" from the names is the Turn made mercy — the reversal that gives the album its title; and the buying-back lands it, a redeemer paying silver and barley for what was already his. The three chapters are already built as sentence-then-reversal, so the text's own order is the shape.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the marriage-sign and the three named children (1:10–11 reversal-flash absorbed) · 2:2–13 the lawsuit against the mother · 2:14–23 the allurement, the betrothal for ever, and the reversal of the names · 3 the buying-back for silver and barley. Four for four. *(Chapter 2 splits at 2:14, where the indictment turns to allurement — the register reverses from lawsuit to wooing.)*

# ALBUM II — MY HEART RECOILS WITHIN ME
### The Covenant Lawsuit and the Tender Heart · Hosea 4–14 · eleven songs

Once the sign is read, the book opens its long controversy: Yahuah has a lawsuit with a land that holds no truth, no mercy, no knowledge of Him — a people destroyed for the want of knowing, priests who forgot, a moth's slow decay and a lion's sudden tearing. The witnesses carry the whole indictment: a repentance that evaporates like the morning dew, a nation half-baked as a cake unturned and heartless as a silly dove flitting between empires, a wind sown and a whirlwind reaped, an empty vine, the days of reckoning come. And then, at the book's center, the thing that undoes every judgment justice demands — the Husband and Father whose heart recoils within Him, who taught the child to walk and cannot now let him go, who is El and not man. The album lands where the book lands: return, and "I will heal their backsliding, I will love them freely."

*Why this title:* the eleven oracles of lawsuit and love all bend toward 11:8 — "mine heart is turned within me, my repentings are kindled together" — the moment the wronged One recoils from the doom His own justice demands, and mercy overrules wrath "for I am El, and not man" (11:9); the title names the tender heart the whole record climbs toward.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the lawsuit opens the record — no truth, no mercy, no knowing — and the moth-and-lion judgment darkens it; the return-cry with its third-day ache is the false dawn, answered by the morning cloud; the dove, the whirlwind, and the days of reckoning run the indictment down to ruin; the fallow-ground call is the first true mercy inside the warning; and then the Turn that is not a fall but a recoil of love — "how shall I give thee up" — the album's summit and the book's heart. Yaakov's wrestling and the vow over death carry the ache forward, and "I will love them freely" lands the record — the wrath turned away, the same dew that vanished now the presence that stays.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 4 the controversy and the lost knowledge · 5 the moth, the lion, and the withdrawal · 6 the return-cry, the third day, and the morning-cloud reply · 7 the unturned cake and the silly dove · 8 the wind and the whirlwind · 9 the days of reckoning · 10 the empty vine and the fallow ground · 11 "out of Egypt I called my son" and "how shall I give thee up" · 12 Yaakov at the Yabboq and the east wind · 13 the vow over death and the ransom from Sheol · 14 the return and "I will love them freely." Eleven for eleven — one song per chapter, chapters 4 through 14, with chapter 6's return-cry and its divine reply held together as the single exchange the text makes them.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy. The children's names are the text's own Hebrew, rendered honestly — Yizre'el, Lo-Ruchamah, Lo-Ammi, and their reversals Ruchamah ("pitied") and Ammi ("my people"). The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **The restraint honored (the Song-of-Songs standard):** the marriage-to-an-unfaithful-wife allegory is handled at a grave, chaste, high altitude — family-friendly at every line, no sensual or graphic detail. Album I sings the covenant *grief* and the covenant *love* from heaven's side: the lawsuit against the mother hangs on spurned provision ("she did not know who gave her the corn and the wine and the oil"), not on the body; the allurement is the wilderness where He speaks to her heart; the buying-back is a redeemer paying for his own. Gomer and Hoshea are witnessed, never voiced, and never their intimacies.
- **The tender heart given full weight:** the book's center, 11:8 — "how shall I give thee up, Ephrayim?… mine heart is turned within me" — is the title of Album II and its own song ("How Can I Give You Up, Ephrayim"), sung in awe by the witnesses as Yahuah's own words: the wronged Husband and Father whose heart recoils from the judgment His justice demands, mercy overruling wrath "for I am El, and not man" (11:9). The reversed names are given their full weight in Album I, song 3 — the "not" struck clean off Lo-Ruchamah and Lo-Ammi (2:23).
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every song the messianic aches are carried within their own hour, never resolved forward — "out of Egypt I called my son" (11:1), "after two days… the third day he will raise us up" (6:2), and "O death, I will be thy plagues… I will ransom them from the power of Sheol" (13:14) are sung as mystery and longing the witnesses have just overheard. David is named because the text names him (3:5), his line's far end held as ache. No Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment language inside a song. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside Hosea's own hour, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Vav keyed to the binding "and" of the eternal betrothal (2:19–20), the Letters as the carriers of the knowledge the land has lost (4:6) and as the watchers of the "not" struck from the names. Hoshea, Gomer, the priests, and the princes are *witnessed*, never made the singer; Yahuah's first-person love-speech is testified to from heaven's side, never sung as the prophet's. The morning cloud and early dew, the unturned cake, the silly dove, the sown wind and the whirlwind, the empty vine, the moth and the lion, the east wind, and the dew and lily and roots like Levanon are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the fifteen pieces of silver and the homer and a half of barley Gomer is bought back for (3:2), the "two days… third day" of the return (6:2).
- **Craft:** no two titles share a formula or cadence — the fifteen open on *Three / She / The / Bought / No / Torn / After / A / They / The / Break / How / Their / O Death / I Will*, and their shapes run from the verdict-naming to the letter's confession to the witnessed vow; each is recognizable from its title and passage; paradox is prized where the text makes it true (a prophet told to love a wife who will betray him, a "not my people" renamed "my people," a Husband whose heart recoils from the judgment His justice demands, love bought back for the price of a slave, a repentance that burns off like dew, the same dew redeemed into His own faithful presence).
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — the covenant-love reading of the sign — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
