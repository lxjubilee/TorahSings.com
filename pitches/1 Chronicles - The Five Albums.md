# 1 CHRONICLES — THE FIVE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book Sung From the Choir Loft of Heaven

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the genealogical formulas ("the sons of…," "these are they whom David set…"), the regnal and battle markers, and the great organizing lists (the courses of the priests, the Levites, the singers, the gatekeepers) that Chronicles supplies in place of the Genesis *toledot*.

**The Chronicles angle:** Chronicles retells the David story from a **priestly, worship-centered** vantage — genealogy first, then the ark, the Levites, and above all the **appointing of the temple singers and musicians** (1 Chron 6; 15–16; 23; 25). This is where Scripture first organizes worship music: singers set apart "to prophesy with harps," 288 trained voices, four thousand musicians, gatekeepers and courses turning like the heavens themselves. It is the most on-theme book Torah Sings has yet reached — the same events as the book of Shmuel, but seen **from the choir loft of heaven**, not the battlefield. Where 1 Chronicles overlaps 2 Shmuel, these albums sing the Chronicler's distinct liturgical emphasis — the ark, the song, the pattern of the house — never a repeat of the Shmuel record. That is the feature, not a flaw: the same king, watched from where the singing is.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Son promised but not yet unveiled. The Levitical singers "prophesied with harps" (25:1); the Angels are the pattern of the heavenly worship those choirs were built to mirror, and they testify to it here. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own moment, with no later hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Chronicles-moment song the aches are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled: the Davidic covenant ("I will establish his throne forever," 17:14); the house David most wants and is forbidden to build (22:8); the singers appointed to a worship that echoes heaven's own (25); the throne traced past its own exile (3:17–24). **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not David, not Shlomo, not Asaf, not Sha'ul) or anything derived from one. David's psalm of thanks (16), his prayer "who am I" (17), his charge to Shlomo (22, 28), and his doxology "yours, O Yahuah, is the greatness" (29) are **witnessed and testified to by the Angels**, never sung as David. Impersonal creation the text supplies — the ark on the Levites' shoulders, the harps and lyres and cymbals appointed, the well of Beit-Lechem, the marching in the treetops, the threshing floor of Ornan, the crown of a talent of gold, the gold and silver laid up for a house David may not build — may be *invoked as image*, never voiced. Natural witness for the wars' end: **the Angel of Yahuah standing between earth and heaven, a drawn sword stretched out over Yerushalayim at Ornan's threshing floor** (21:16) — the future altar site. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Five albums. Thirty-two songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of 1 Chronicles is sung. All twenty-nine chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold — the nine chapters of genealogy become one coherent record, exactly as they did for Genesis. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE ROLL CALL BEFORE THE THRONE
### The Generations · 1 Chronicles 1–9 · ten songs

Chronicles opens the way no history dares: nine chapters of names before a single scene — the whole human race funneled from one word, *Adam*, down to a shepherd's tribe, and out again to the priests, the Levites, the singers, and the gatekeepers who will keep the house. To the reader these are the pages to skip. From heaven's side they are the foundation of everything that follows: a nation coming home from exile with almost nothing left, and finding the names were enough to rebuild on. The Letters never dropped one. This record is what their keeping sounds like — a roll call read aloud before the throne, every name still spoken, the whole worship of the house already latent in a list.

*Why this album exists:* per the methodology (§3.3), genealogy distributes onto one coherent record — the narrative albums stay tight, and the ledger becomes its own musical, exactly as *The Names Outlived the Graves* did for Genesis. Here the theology is sharper still: for the Chronicler the names are not filler but the proof that the returned remnant is still Israel, still the line, still the priesthood.

# ALBUM II — THE KINGDOM TURNED TO DAVID
### Sha'ul's Fall and David Enthroned · 1 Chronicles 10–12 · five songs

The Chronicler tells the death of one king in a single, devastating verdict — Sha'ul died because he asked a ghost instead of asking Yahuah — and then the kingdom turns, all in one motion, to a shepherd anointed by the whole nation. These five songs are the transfer of a throne seen from above: the fortress the Yevusi swore no shepherd could take, falling; three men breaking through a war camp for a drink of water their king refuses to swallow; the trickle of defectors who left the reigning king for the hunted one, until a whole host came to Chevron of a single, undivided heart.

*Why this title:* the Chronicler's own hinge — "he slew him, and turned the kingdom unto David the son of Yishai" (10:14) — the crown moving on a failure to inquire.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **He Sought a Ghost, and Lost the Throne** | 1 Chron 10 | The Angels, watching the crown leave Gilboa | Sha'ul falls on his own sword on the mountain, and the Chronicler gives the reason in one breath: he was unfaithful, "asked counsel of one that had a familiar spirit… and inquired not of Yahuah: therefore he slew him, and turned the kingdom unto David" — a throne lost over the direction of a single question |
| 2 | **The Shepherd Took the Stronghold** | 1 Chron 11:1–9 | The Angels, who knew the fortress could not keep him out | All Yisrael comes to Chevron with one voice — "thou shalt feed my people" — and the shepherd anointed there marches on Yevus and takes the stronghold of Tziyon that its people swore no such man could enter; the city of David built up from Millo round about |
| 3 | **The Water They Would Not Drink** | 1 Chron 11:10–47 | The Mem — the water itself — with the Angels naming the mighty men | The three who break through the Pelishtim camp to draw water from the well of Beit-Lechem for a longing spoken aloud — and the king pours it out on the ground to Yahuah, "shall I drink the blood of these men that have put their lives in jeopardy?"; a gift too costly to swallow, given back |
| 4 | **They Left the Crowned King for the Hunted** | 1 Chron 12:1–22 | The Angels, who watched a host gather to a fugitive | To David at Tsiqlag, while he was still hiding from Sha'ul, they came — even Binyamites of Sha'ul's own kin, and Gadite warriors "whose faces were like the faces of lions" — until the Ruach clothed Amasai and he cried "thine are we, David"; men coming day by day to the wrong king, until it was a great host like the host of Elohim |
| 5 | **Not One of Them Was Double-Hearted** | 1 Chron 12:23–40 | The Angels, who had never seen an army of one mind | The full muster at Chevron, thousands out of every tribe come to turn the kingdom, "expert in war… they could keep rank: they were not of double heart"; all the rest of Yisrael "of one heart to make David king," and three days of eating and drinking, for there was joy in Yisrael |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* Sha'ul's fall opens the album as the Turn already fallen — the crown adrift; the shepherd taking Tziyon is the Unveiling; the well of Beit-Lechem is the intimate Selah, valour answered by a king who will not drink it; the defectors at Tsiqlag build the wonder; and the whole-hearted host at Chevron lands the album — a nation of one heart, the kingdom fully turned and feasting.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 10 the death of Sha'ul and why · 11:1–9 David anointed and Tziyon taken · 11:10–47 the mighty men and the well of Beit-Lechem · 12:1–22 the warriors who came to Tsiqlag · 12:23–40 the muster of one heart at Chevron. Five for five.

# ALBUM III — THEY SANG THE ARK INTO THE CITY
### The Ark, the Song, and the Covenant · 1 Chronicles 13–17 · five songs

This is the worship heart of the book. David's first attempt to bring the ark home is done the world's way — a new cart — and a steadying hand falls dead against it; the ark rests three months in a stranger's house and blesses it while the king stands afraid. The second attempt is done Yahuah's way, on the Levites' shoulders, and this time the ark comes home not in silence but in a wall of song — the first singers Scripture ever appoints, a psalm of thanks left standing on duty before the ark day and night. And then the reversal that governs everything after: the man who wanted to build Yahuah a house is told the house will be built for *him*, a throne to stand forever.

*Why this title:* chapters 15–16 are the moment worship music is first organized in Scripture — the ark carried right and sung into the City of David; the album's spine is that song.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Hand That Reached to Steady It** | 1 Chron 13 | The Yod — the hand itself — with the Angels at the cart | The ark set on a new cart the way the Pelishtim once carried it; the oxen stumble at the threshing floor of Kidon, Uzza puts out his hand to hold it, and is struck dead — a right thing done the wrong way; David afraid, and the ark left in Oved-Edom's house, blessing all he has |
| 2 | **Marching in the Tops of the Trees** | 1 Chron 14 | The Angels — their own footsteps the sign | David established, a house built for him, the Pelishtim broken "like the breaking forth of waters" at Ba'al-Peratsim; and the second time, the sign to strike: wait for "the sound of going in the tops of the mulberry trees" — the host of heaven marching in the branches ahead of the army below |
| 3 | **Borne on Shoulders, Led by Song** | 1 Chron 15 | The Angels, watching the Levites lift what may not be touched | This time by the book: "none ought to carry the ark but the Levites," borne on their shoulders on staves — and David appoints the singers, Kenanyah "chief of the Levites for song," harps and lyres and cymbals sounding it home; and Mikhal at the window, despising the king who danced |
| 4 | **The Song Left on Duty Before the Ark** | 1 Chron 16 | The Angels, witnessing the first appointed thanks (David's psalm testified, never voiced as his) | The ark set in the tent, and a song delivered "to thank Yahuah" — "O give thanks unto Yahuah, call upon his name… sing unto him, sing psalms"; Asaf and his brethren appointed to minister before the ark continually, a song posted like a watchman, morning and evening, that would not fall silent |
| 5 | **He Came to Build and Was Given a Throne** | 1 Chron 17 | The Bet — the house, *bayit* — with the Angels witnessing the covenant | David means to build Yahuah a house of cedar, and the word comes back reversed: "thou shalt not build me an house… Yahuah will build thee an house," a seed after him, a throne "established for evermore"; and the king can only sit and ask, "who am I, O Yahuah Elohim?" — the promise of forever carried as ache, its far end unseen |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the struck hand opens the album in dread — reverence learned the hard way; the marching in the treetops is the Unveiling, heaven's own host going before; the ark borne on shoulders and led by song is the Declaration, the worship of the house first set in order; the appointed thanks is the still, continual center; and the covenant lands the album — the builder told to stand still and receive, a throne promised past where the witnesses can see, setting the ache that Album V will carry.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 13 the first attempt and Uzza · 14 David established and the Pelishtim broken · 15 the ark on the Levites' shoulders and the singers appointed · 16 the ark in the tent and the psalm of thanks · 17 the covenant and David's prayer. Five for five.

# ALBUM IV — THE WARS LED TO A THRESHING FLOOR
### The Campaigns and the Altar Site · 1 Chronicles 18–21 · four songs

David's wars are told here for a single destination. The plunder of every conquered nation is not spent — it is laid up, silver and gold and bronze, toward a house David already knows he will not build. An act of kindness answered with public shame drags a king into a war he did not seek; the giants who once made a nation tremble fall one after another to his servants. And it all funnels to a threshing floor: a census that should never have been counted brings a plague, and an Angel with a drawn sword stands between earth and heaven over Yerushalayim — and stops, at the exact patch of ground where the altar, and one day the whole house, will rise.

*Why this title:* the campaigns of chapters 18–20 run straight to chapter 21's threshing floor of Ornan — the sword sheathed where the temple will stand; war ending, of all places, at a place of worship.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Spoils Were Laid Up for a House** | 1 Chron 18 | The Angels, watching a king bank his victories | David subdues the Pelishtim, Moav, Hadad'ezer of Tsovah, Aram — "Yahuah preserved David whithersoever he went" — and the silver and gold and the great weight of bronze are not squandered but dedicated, laid up toward the house another hand will build; and he "executed judgment and justice among all his people" |
| 2 | **They Shamed the Men Who Came in Peace** | 1 Chron 19:1–20:3 | The Angels, who saw a kindness turned into a war | David sends comfort to Ammon, and Chanun shaves his envoys and cuts their garments to the hip; the Ammonites hire the Aramean chariots, Yoav sets the battle "be of good courage, and let us behave ourselves valiantly for our people" — and it ends at Rabbah, a crown of a talent of gold set on David's head |
| 3 | **Six on Each Hand, and He Still Fell** | 1 Chron 20:4–8 | The Yod — the hand, counting the fingers — with the Angels | The wars with the sons of the giant at Gat: Sippai, and Lachmi the brother of Golyat whose spear was a weaver's beam, and the man of great stature with six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot, four and twenty in all — every one of them fallen "by the hand of David, and by the hand of his servants" |
| 4 | **The Sword Stopped Where the House Would Stand** | 1 Chron 21 | The Angels, and the Angel of Yahuah between earth and heaven (invoked, witnessed) | The census that should not have been numbered, the plague, and the Angel of Yahuah standing between the earth and the heaven, a drawn sword stretched out over Yerushalayim — halted at the threshing floor of Ornan; David buys the floor, builds an altar, fire answers from heaven, and the sword is put up in its sheath at the very ground where the house will rise |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the banked spoils open the album already leaning toward the house; the shamed envoys and the crown of Rabbah are the campaign at full stretch; the giants of Gat are the last of the old terror falling; and the threshing floor lands the album — the sword stopped, the altar built, the temple site revealed at the place a plague was halted. The house itself is withheld; it rises on Album V, where it belongs — on the ground this album bought.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 18 the victories and the dedicated spoils · 19:1–20:3 the Ammonite-Aramean war and the crown of Rabbah · 20:4–8 the giants of Gat · 21 the census, the plague, and the Angel at Ornan's threshing floor. Four for four.

# ALBUM V — THE HOUSE HE COULD NOT BUILD
### The Preparation, the Orders, and the Last Charge · 1 Chronicles 22–29 · eight songs

Here is the paradox that carries the whole book: the man who wants nothing more than to build Yahuah a house is told his hands have shed too much blood to lay a stone of it. So he does the only thing left — he prepares everything, and ordains everyone, for a house he will die without seeing. Iron and bronze beyond weighing, cedar without number, gold and silver in talents; the Levites given new work now that the ark has rest; the priests set in four-and-twenty courses by lot; the gatekeepers, the treasurers, the army rotating month by month like a liturgy. And at its heart, the on-theme jewel of the entire book: the singers of Asaf and Heman and Yedutun **set apart to prophesy with harps** — 288 trained voices, four thousand instruments — the first choir Scripture ever ranks, built to answer the song the Angels have sung since the foundations. Then the last charge, the pattern handed over "in writing by his hand," and a dying king's doxology — *of thine own have we given thee* — before a single stone is laid.

*Why this title:* the album's engine is 22:8 — "thou hast shed blood abundantly… thou shalt not build an house unto my name" — the warrior-king forbidden the one thing he most wants, who spends his last strength preparing it for his son.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Too Much Blood to Build It** | 1 Chron 22 | The Bet — the house, *bayit*, that this hand may not raise — with the Angels | David told "thou hast shed blood abundantly… thou shalt not build"; so he heaps up iron, bronze, cedar, and a hundred thousand talents of gold against the day, and charges his son Shlomo, a man of rest, to build what he cannot — a builder pouring everything into a house he will never enter |
| 2 | **They Would Carry the Ark No More** | 1 Chron 23 | The Angels, watching the porters set down their poles | David old, the Levites numbered from thirty years up, eight and thirty thousand — and their work changed forever: "the Levites shall no more carry the tabernacle," for the ark has come to rest; the shoulders that bore it now stand to thank and praise Yahuah every morning and evening, and four thousand take up instruments |
| 3 | **The Lot Decided Who Would Serve** | 1 Chron 24 | The Angels, watching the holy turns fall by lot | The sons of Aharon set in four-and-twenty courses, "one principal household… divided by lot," Elazar and Itamar alike, so that no hand could claim the altar by rank — the order of the priesthood settled by a stone shaken out before Yahuah, each course its appointed week |
| 4 | **Set Apart to Prophesy on the Harp** | 1 Chron 25 | The Angels — the pattern of the heavenly worship the choir was built to mirror | The singers of Asaf, Heman, and Yedutun "separated to the service… who should prophesy with harps, with psalteries, and with cymbals"; two hundred fourscore and eight, "instructed in the songs of Yahuah," casting lots for their courses as the priests did — song made a ministry, the earthly choir tuned to the one heaven has never stopped singing |
| 5 | **The Strong Men Kept the Doors** | 1 Chron 26 | The Dalet — the door itself — with the Angels | The gatekeepers set by lot for every gate, east and north and south and the storehouse — the sons of Oved-Edom, "able men for strength for the service," mighty men of valour posted not to charge a wall but to guard a threshold; and the treasurers over the dedicated things, and the officers and judges of Yisrael |

| 7 | **The Pattern, Written by His Hand** | 1 Chron 28 | The Angels, witnessing the blueprint handed down | David rises before all the assembly and gives Shlomo the pattern of everything — the courts, the chambers, the weight of every vessel, the plan of the chariot of the cherubim — "all this… in writing by his hand upon me," the design of the house received from Yahuah; and the charge, "be strong and of good courage, and do it… Yahuah will not fail thee" |
| 8 | **Of Your Own We Gave You Back** | 1 Chron 29 | The Angels, witnessing the doxology (David's blessing testified, never voiced as his) | The leaders offer willingly, gold and silver and precious stones in talents beyond count, and the dying king blesses Yahuah before them all — "thine, O Yahuah, is the greatness, and the power… all things come of thee, and of thine own have we given thee"; Shlomo made king, and David dies "full of days, riches, and honour," the house still only a heap of gold and a written plan |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the forbidden builder and his heaped materials open the album in ache; the Levites who carry no more, the priests by lot, the singers set to prophesy, the gatekeepers, and the army in courses are the great ordering — the house given ranks before it has walls, with the choir of chapter 25 as the on-theme summit thundered in the middle; the pattern written by his hand is the Declaration, heaven's blueprint handed on; and the doxology lands not only the album but the whole book — a king who could not build giving everything and dying, blessing, before one stone is laid, the far promise of the house held as longing.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 22 the materials and the charge to Shlomo · 23 the Levites and their new service · 24 the courses of the priests · 25 the courses of the singers · 26 the gatekeepers, treasurers, and officers · 27 the army by month and the tribal stewards · 28 the public charge and the pattern · 29 the freewill offering, the doxology, and David's death. Eight for eight.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy — always Elohim / Yahuah. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). No anglicized divine names. Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing. No emoji.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Chronicles-moment song the aches are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled — the covenant throne "for evermore" (17:14); the house David is forbidden to build (22:8); the singers appointed to a worship that echoes heaven's own (25); the royal line traced past its own exile to Zerubbabel (3:17–24). The captivity and the return are sung because the text itself records them (9:1–2; 3:17), not as later hindsight. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Bet keyed to *bayit* (the house David meant to build and was given), the Yod to the hand (the steadying hand struck, the six-fingered giant, the hand of David's servants), the Mem to the water of the well, the Dalet to the guarded door. David, Sha'ul, Shlomo, Asaf, and the mighty men are *witnessed*, never made the singer; David's psalm of thanks (16), his prayer (17), his charge (22, 28), and his doxology (29) are testified to from heaven's side, never sung as his. Amasai's Spirit-clothed cry (12:18) is quoted as a quotation, never taken as the singers' own voice. The ark on the Levites' shoulders, the harps and cymbals appointed, the well of Beit-Lechem, the marching in the treetops, the crown of a talent, the threshing floor of Ornan, and the gold laid up are invoked as images, never voiced. The Angel of Yahuah with the drawn sword (21:16) is the natural witness at the album's altar-site, invoked and testified, never sung as a human.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used — chiefly as the ledger-keepers of the genealogies — and no total is ever stated, no gematria mechanism ever sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method: the two hundred fourscore and eight trained singers (25:7), the four thousand musicians (23:5), the eight and thirty thousand Levites (23:3), the four-and-twenty priestly courses (24), the twenty-four thousand a month in twelve courses (27), the crown of a talent of gold (20:2), the four-and-twenty fingers and toes (20:6), the talents of gold and silver laid up (22; 29).
- **The Chronicles angle in production:** these albums deliberately sing the Chronicler's liturgical emphasis rather than repeating the Shmuel material — the ark, the appointed song, the courses of worship, the pattern of the house. The worship-music thread that is this book's signature runs through the whole pitch by design: the singers named in the Levi genealogy (Album I, song 7), the first singers appointed as the ark comes home (Album III, songs 3–4), and the singers set apart to prophesy with harps (Album V, song 4, the on-theme jewel).
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5) — fitting for the one book of Scripture that first put worship music into ranks.
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
