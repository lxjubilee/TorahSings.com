# GENESIS — THE NINE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of Beginnings

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams (the *toledot* formulas of Genesis first among them).
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Son promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own place in Scripture, with no New-Testament hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing and in later-covenant material, but inside a Genesis-moment song the promise is carried as mystery and ache, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character or anything derived from one; impersonal creation (a well, a heap, a vine, the ground) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Nine albums. Sixty-nine songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Genesis is sung. Every chapter accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — WHEN THE MORNING STARS SANG
### Creation and the Break · Genesis 1–4 · six songs

Before anything broke, the witnesses sang it whole — seven days, seven declarations of *tov*, a light that needed no sun. Then, from inside the very good, a crack: a sword placed in the hands of the choir, a brother's blood learning to speak from the ground, heaven's own gift of music bent into a boast. These six songs follow the crack to its strangest end — the moment the dust He once knelt in said His Name back. Not canon; something overheard.

*Why this title:* nothing should crack in a creation pronounced *tov me'od* — and everything did.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order; matches the shipped album):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Light Before the Sun** | Genesis 1:1–2:3 | The Angels, and the Letters taking their places as each word is spoken | Three evenings and mornings shone before any sun existed — light with no lamp, poured straight from the speaking |
| 2 | **Our Maker Knelt in Dust** | Genesis 2:4–25 | The Angels | He who spoke galaxies stooped into mud He had made — the first thing heaven ever watched Him do slowly, with His hands |
| 3 | **The Sword We Held Was Mercy** | Genesis 3 | The cherubim at the gate — the singers of the foundations, now guarding what remained | The flaming blade bars the fallen from an endless death — the drawn weapon is the kindness, and it keeps the road home |
| 4 | **Dead, He Began to Speak** | Genesis 4:1–16 | The Angels — heaven's lament over the first death, watching from within the moment | The first blood cries up from the ground (4:10); the Letters Dalet (the door sin crouches at, 4:7) and Mem (the water) sound inside *dam* — and a heaven that had only ever sung creation learns to grieve |
| 5 | **We Never Taught Them That Song** | Genesis 4:17–24 | The Angels — the original singers, hearing humanity's first recorded song | In the unit where harp and pipe are invented, the first human song Scripture records is Lamech's murder boast — the one song the witnesses will not join |
| 6 | **Then Dust Called the Name** | Genesis 4:25–26 | The Letters of the Name themselves — Yod, Hey, Vav, Hey — hearing human breath shape them for the first time | The creature sentenced to return to dust calls, before returning, on the Name that formed it from dust |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order — NARRATIVE-ORDER RULE):* the album runs straight down Genesis — the seven-day light, the Maker's hands in the dust of Eden, the sword at the gate, the first death crying from the ground, the first human song bent into a boast, and the first time dust calls the Name. No cinematic reordering: the story is the spine, so the listener never loses the thread. The dust-frame still closes on itself — He knelt in the dust of Eden early; the dust calls His Name at the close.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1:1–2:3 creation · 2:4–25 Eden · 3 the Fall · 4:1–16 Cain and Abel · 4:17–24 the line of Cain · 4:25–26 Seth and the calling on the Name. One unit, one song, six for six — and the play order equals the text order.

# ALBUM II — GRIEF BUILT THE BASKET
### The Flood · Genesis 6–9 · seven songs

Before a drop of rain fell, there was grief in the heart of Yahuah — and the same sorrow that judged the world built a basket to carry it. The witnesses watched their own brothers go down, watched the second day come undone, and watched one tar-sealed *tevah* ride the verdict until smoke rose where the rain had fallen. When it was finished, heaven's war-bow hung in the cloud, undrawn — aimed away from the earth, at the Archer Himself.

*Why this title:* the *tevah* is literally a basket — Tet's own pictograph — sealed with *kopher*, the root of *kippur*. Sorrow as shipwright.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The First Broken Heart Was His** | Genesis 6:1–8 | The Angels — who stood beside the sons who went down, then heard grief inside the courts of the Most High | The first heartbreak recorded in Scripture belongs to the Maker: "it grieved Him at His heart" |
| 2 | **Atonement Smelled Like Tar** | Genesis 6:9–22 | The Kaph — first letter of *kopher*, the covering palm — while the Vav answers, redeeming itself hammering the planks | Mercy for a whole world sealed on with pitch from a workman's bucket — a basket made unsinkable by covering |
| 3 | **The Second Day Undone** | Genesis 7 | The Letters — the ones who sang the firmament into place, watching their own day-two song reversed | Waters above and waters below, separated at creation, reunite — every seam fails except the one door Yahuah shuts Himself |
| 4 | **The Wind Was His Remembering** | Genesis 8:1–19 | The Angels — who recognize the ruach of Genesis 1:2 hovering over the deep again | "And Elohim remembered Noah" — from the One who never forgot; in Hebrew, remembering is covenant set in motion, and the text makes the wind itself the act |
| 5 | **Smoke Rose Where the Rain Fell** | Genesis 8:20–22 | The Angels — who watched everything fall for a year, now watching the first thing ascend | After a world came down, the first motion upward from the washed mud is altar smoke |

Judgment is not the last word, and neither is triumph: human frailty is the honest one.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 6:1–8 the sons of Elohim · 6:9–22 the commission · 7 the flood · 8:1–19 the remembering · 8:20–22 the altar · 9:1–17 the bow · 9:18–29 the vineyard. Seven for seven.

# ALBUM III — THE NAMES OUTLIVED THE GRAVES
### The Generations · Genesis 5; 10; 11; 22:20–24; 25:12–18; 36; 46:8–27 · ten songs

These are the chapters readers skip — the begats — and from heaven's side of the veil they were singing the whole time. Eight times the ink tolls "and he died," yet every name it buried is still spoken here: seventy nations drawn as a family, one thread pulled through the scattering down to a single man in Ur, the cast-out and the crowned held in the same ledger, seventy souls counted at the border of the dark. The Letters never dropped a name — this record is what their keeping sounds like.

*Why this album exists:* per the methodology (§3.3), genealogy songs distribute onto one record — the narrative albums stay tight, and the ledger becomes its own musical.

# ALBUM IV — WE WATCHED HIM BIND HIMSELF
### Abraham I — The Call and the Cutting · Genesis 12–17 · eight songs

One man is called out of Ur with a single word, and heaven leans in to watch what no witness expected: Yahuah tying Himself, oath by oath, letter by letter, to a childless man's future. From the altars on a ridge he did not own, to the smoking furnace that walked between the pieces with no second walker, to a runaway slave who named the One who sees, to a breath from the Name itself set into two aged bodies — these songs are what the stars and the angels say they saw. Not canon — something to consider: the covenant was never a bargain between two. It was carried, alone, by One.

*Why this title:* both smiths converged on it independently — the witnesses' own confession: the Unbindable taking His own chains.

# ALBUM V — THE FIRE FELL, THE KNIFE DIDN'T
### Abraham II — Fire and the Knife · Genesis 18–23 · nine songs

The angels call this their heaviest watch: one family, one tent door, and everything heaven promised carried on a boy whose name means laughter. In these seven songs the witnesses remember what they were sent to do — eat under the tree at Mamre, drag four out of the burning by the wrists, open a well a weeping mother could not see, and cry out over Moriah one heartbeat before the knife came down. Fire fell on the plain; the knife did not fall on the boy — and the only ground faith ever owned in the land of promise was a grave.

*Why this title:* the album hangs between two verdicts from the same Elohim — the fire that fell, the blade that was stayed.

# ALBUM VI — THE LADDER STARTED UNDERGROUND

# ALBUM VII — THE BLESSING LIMPS HOME

Then one night at the Yabboq, one of their own held a man until daybreak and could not prevail, and the losing was the blessing. These are the songs they sang on the road home: seven wounds, seven renamings, and a limp the text calls Yisrael.

# ALBUM VIII — THE GARMENTS LIED, THE DREAMS DID NOT
### Joseph I · Genesis 37–43 · seven songs

Across these seven stories the witnesses watched a strange law hold: every garment lied — a coat soaked in goat's blood, a widow's veil, a cloak left behind in a fist — and every dream told the truth. They went down with the dreamer into the waterless pit, into the slave-house, into the cell he himself still called a pit, and they kept the ledger of every forgotten day until one morning undid thirteen years.
