**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`, and `pitches/Lamentations - The Album.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Obadiah is the shortest book in the Scriptures — a single chapter of twenty-one verses, the vision concerning Edom — and it holds one movement per seam, so it is one album of four songs. The seams are the text's own: the pride of Edom in the clefts of the rock and the coming fall (vv1–9); the violence Edom did to his brother Yaakov in the day of his calamity (vv10–14); the Day of Yahuah near upon all the nations, the cup on the holy mountain, and the fire of the house of Yaakov (vv15–18); and the deliverance on Mount Tzion, where the exiles come home and the kingdom returns to Yahuah (vv19–21). Twenty-one verses will not hold more without padding, and they are not padded.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* the hour of the vision, an aching hope held but never resolved. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside the vision's own moment, with no later hindsight — the book's closing line, "saviours shall come up on mount Tzion to judge the mount of Esav; and the kingdom shall be Yahuah's" (v21), is carried as longing held inside the oracle, never resolved forward. No Yeshua, no cross, no Messiah, no fulfillment inside any song; the deliverers are not yet come, and the album honors the vision's own unfinished reach. Yahuah's first-person oracle — "I have made thee small among the heathen" (v2), "thence will I bring thee down" (v4), "shall I not in that day destroy the wise men out of Edom?" (v8) — is **His word, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said,** never taken as their own claim. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human, not Ovadyah, not Edom, not Yaakov. Edom, Esav, Yaakov, and the children of Yehudah are *witnessed*, never voiced. Impersonal creation the text supplies (the eagle's high nest, the clefts of the rock, the stars among which Edom sets his nest, the cup drunk on the holy mountain, the fire of the house of Yaakov and the stubble of Esav) may be *invoked as image*, never voiced. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *One album. Four songs. No honest zeros — every movement of Obadiah is sung. All twenty-one verses accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes below it: the shortest book holds four movements, so it holds four songs — and four is what it gets. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE BROTHER BROUGHT DOWN FROM THE STARS
### The pride of Edom brought low, and the kingdom restored to Yahuah · Obadiah 1–21 · four songs

Edom sits in the clefts of the rock, his habitation high, and says in his heart, *who shall bring me down to the ground?* — a brother grown proud, nested as high as the eagle, as high as the stars themselves. The witnesses sing the whole vision against him: the pride that deceived him and the word that answers it — *thence will I bring thee down* — the wound of a brother who stood on the other side and posted himself at the fork of the road to cut off the fleeing in the day of Yaakov's calamity, the Day of Yahuah near upon all the nations where *as thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee,* and the deliverance on Mount Tzion where the exiles come home and the kingdom returns to Yahuah. They sing it from heaven's side — grave, restrained over the violence, unwilling to detail it — and they hold the last line open: the saviours not yet come up, the kingdom promised but not yet in hand.

*Why this title:* Edom is no stranger but a brother — Esav, whose striving with Yaakov runs all the way back to the womb in Genesis — and the whole vision is the raising of a proud brother's nest and the bringing of it down. "Though thou exalt thyself as the eagle, and though thou set thy nest among the stars, thence will I bring thee down, saith Yahuah" (v4). The pride reaches the stars; the fall comes from exactly that height; and the vision drives past the fall to Mount Tzion, where the kingdom is restored to Yahuah — the height that does not fall.

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the twenty-one verses march in their own order, and that order is already the album's shape. The pride in the rock and among the stars opens the record — the Veil, the proud heart simply stated and the word that answers it. The brother at the crossway is the Turn, the album's hardest beat and its deepest wound — a betrayal that runs back to Esav and Yaakov in Genesis. The Day of Yahuah is the Declaration, measure-for-measure thundered over all the nations: as thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee. And the saviours on Mount Tzion land the album as the Benediction — the kingdom restored to Yahuah, held open and unresolved, the deliverers still awaited. No reordering improves on the vision's own march; the running order is the order of the oracle.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1–9 the pride in the rock and among the stars, and the coming fall · 10–14 the violence against the brother Yaakov and the crossway of the fleeing · 15–18 the Day of Yahuah, the cup, "as thou hast done," and the fire that leaves no remnant · 19–21 the exiles restored, the saviours on Mount Tzion, and the kingdom to Yahuah. Four for four — one movement, one song, without exception.

## The Coverage Ledger — Every Verse Accounted For

Obadiah is a single chapter, so the ledger runs by verse-ranges across the one chapter.

| Obadiah | Disposition |
|---|---|
| 1:1–9 | Album I, song 1 — the vision announced, the pride in the clefts of the rock and the nest among the stars, "thence will I bring thee down," and the fall (the confederates' betrayal, the wise men of Edom and Teman cut off) |
| 1:10–14 | Album I, song 2 — the violence against the brother Yaakov, standing aloof as one of them, and the crossway where the fleeing were cut off (the chapter splits at the 9/10 seam — the fall of Edom, then the reason for it) |
| 1:15–18 | Album I, song 3 — the Day of Yahuah near upon all nations, the cup on the holy mountain, "as thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee," and the house of Yaakov a fire, the house of Esav stubble (the seam falls at 18/19 — "for Yahuah hath spoken it," then the concrete repossession) |
| 1:19–21 | Album I, song 4 — the exiles restored to their possessions, the saviours come up on Mount Tzion to judge the mount of Esav, and "the kingdom shall be Yahuah's," left unresolved |

**Four songs. One album. No honest zeros — every movement of the shortest book is sung. One song per narrative unit, without exception; the four movements sit on the text's own seams (the 9/10 turn from Edom's fall to Edom's crime; the 18/19 turn from the fire to the repossession), and the count follows the vision, not a ceiling. All twenty-one verses of the one chapter accounted for.**

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine); **Adonai Yahuah** used where the text opens the vision with it ("Thus saith Adonai Yahuah concerning Edom," v1). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy. Edom, Esav, Yaakov, Yehudah, Yoseph, Yerushalayim, Ephrayim, Shomron, Gilad, Teman, and Mount Tzion are given in Hebraic form. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** the book's closing line — "saviours shall come up on mount Tzion to judge the mount of Esav; and the kingdom shall be Yahuah's" (v21) — is carried as aching hope held *inside* the vision's own hour, never resolved forward. No Yeshua, no cross, no Messiah, no fulfillment inside any song; the saviours are not yet come up and the kingdom is not yet in hand. The Edom-as-brother wound is sung as the vision knows it — Esav and Yaakov, the striving that runs back to Genesis — never with hindsight. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside the vision, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Angels who dwell among the very stars where Edom set his proud nest (the ON-THEME hook of v4: "though thou set thy nest among the stars, thence will I bring thee down"); the Aleph that opens both *ach*, "brother," and Edom's own name; the Shin that opens *esh*, "fire," over the stubble of Esav; and the whole company of Letters as the ledger-keepers of the restored possessions. Yahuah's first-person oracle ("I have made thee small," "thence will I bring thee down," "shall I not in that day destroy the wise men out of Edom?") is testified to in awe by the witnesses, never claimed as their own word. Edom, Esav, Yaakov, and the children of Yehudah are *witnessed*, never voiced. The eagle's high nest, the clefts of the rock, the stars, the cup drunk on the holy mountain, and the fire and the stubble are invoked as images, never made the singer.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Aleph and the Shin are named as personae where they lead, and the whole company of Letters carries the closing ledger; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method.
- **Grave restraint:** the violence of the vision — the day of Yaakov's calamity, the fleeing cut off at the crossway, the fire that leaves no remnant of the house of Esav — is witnessed from heaven's side, family-friendly and undetailed; the weight comes from stillness and the witnesses' grief over a brother, never from gore.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5) — a dark cinematic oracle for the pride and the Day, lifting toward Mount Tzion at the close.
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
