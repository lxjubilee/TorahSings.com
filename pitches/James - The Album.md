# JAMES — THE ALBUM
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for Yaakov's Letter to the Twelve Tribes Scattered Abroad

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/John - The Six Albums.md`, `pitches/Colossians - The Album.md`, `pitches/1 Peter - The Album.md`, and the whole Old-Testament catalog — now carried into the **general epistles** and the wisdom-letter of **Yaakov**, in the flesh a brother of the Adon, yet naming himself only "a servant of Elohim and of the Adon Yeshua the Mashiach" (1:1), written to a scattered people.
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. James is a **letter** — and, more than that, a **wisdom-letter**: not narrative, not oracle, not law, but the plainest ethics in the New Covenant, addressed by Yaakov "to the twelve tribes which are scattered abroad" (1:1). Its "units" are the letter's own movements — the greeting and the counsel to count trials joy, ask for wisdom, and hold the crown of life (1:1–12); the good and perfect gift from the Father of lights, the word received and done, pure religion (1:13–27); the faith of the Adon of glory without respect of persons and the royal law (2:1–13); faith without works is dead — Avraham and Rachav (2:14–26); the untamable tongue and the wisdom from above (3:1–18); friendship with the world, resisting the adversary, humbling under the mighty hand, and the vanishing vapour (4:1–17); the woe to the rich, the withheld wages crying, and the patience unto the coming (5:1–12); and the prayer of faith, Eliyahu's prayer, and the turning of the wanderer (5:13–20). The seams are Yaakov's own: the "my brethren" address that reopens each movement, the "wherefore" and "therefore" hinges ("wherefore, my beloved brethren," 1:19; "be patient therefore," 5:7), the diatribe questions ("what doth it profit?" 2:14; "from whence come wars?" 4:1), the "go to now" woe-openings (4:13; 5:1), and the closing turn on prayer.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels), aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. **At the incarnation that changes.** The Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made is now made flesh; the aleph-bet lives in Him. The morning stars have gone quiet, gathered up into the Word made flesh — so through the New Covenant **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** And this letter carries the platform's warrant in its own bones: the whole record of Yaakov's ethics is nothing but "the wisdom that is from above" (3:15, 17) — the very Wisdom the Angels adored beside Yahuah before the deep (Mishlei 8:22–30). The Letters are the marvel the Angels *testify to,* not a voice of their own.

**Epistle, not narrative — the accomplished work and the wisdom of Yeshua proclaimed freely and fully.** In the Gospels the Angels were eye-witnesses, forward-locked to the story's order — never naming the cross before it came. **A letter has no biography of Yeshua to lock:** written *after* the resurrection, the finished work is proclaimed openly and in full, and the finished work stands *behind* every line of these ethics. Yaakov names Yeshua by name only twice — "a servant of Elohim and of the Adon Yeshua the Mashiach" (1:1) and "the faith of our Adon Yeshua the Mashiach, the Adon of glory" (2:1) — **but his whole letter is the teaching of Yeshua carried in a brother's hand.** The Angels who heard the Adon of glory teach on the mountain hear His every word again here: count it joy under trial ("rejoice, and be exceeding glad"); ask, and it shall be given; be a doer and build on the rock; blessed are the merciful; love thy neighbour; judge not; lay not up treasure the moth corrupts; take no thought for the morrow; swear not at all, but let your yea be yea. So the Angels sing every wisdom-word of this letter as **the wisdom of Yeshua the Adon of glory,** bearing witness to Him. The forward-lock does not vanish — it *relocates* to the one thing the letter itself still holds unresolved: **His coming.** "Be patient therefore, brethren, unto the coming of the Adon. Behold, the husbandman waiteth for the precious fruit of the earth… the coming of the Adon draweth nigh… behold, the judge standeth before the door" (5:7–9) — that patient, aching longing-for-the-coming is the album's single unresolved ache, exactly as the Old-Testament catalog held its ache.

**Yeshua witnessed and quoted, never voiced.** The singer is always the Angels; **Yeshua is testified to and His words are quoted in awe — never a first-person singer.** Every "the Adon of glory," every echo of the mountain, every "the coming of the Adon draweth nigh" is the Angels reporting and marveling at the wisdom Yaakov wrote and the Adon first spoke — never Yeshua's own voice, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old Covenant. **Christocentric — about "the who":** the humans of this letter — the poor man in vile raiment, the rich man with the gold ring, Avraham with the knife, Rachav with the scarlet line, the fainting husbandman, sick Iyov, praying Eliyahu — **never sing;** they are witnessed and quoted, always as testimony to the Adon of glory whose wisdom the whole letter carries, and every song lands on Him.

**The Angels have a stake this letter hands them — the hosts, the wisdom, and Iyov.** Yaakov names no angel outright, yet three times the letter reaches straight into the host that sang at the foundations. First, **"the Adon of sabaoth"** (5:4) — Adonai Tzevaot, the Adon of the *armies,* the hosts of heaven — is the very Commander of the Angels who sing this platform; when the withheld wages of the reapers cry, they cry "into the ears of the Adon of sabaoth," and the Angels are the hosts whose Adon bends to hear. Second, **"the wisdom that is from above"** (3:15, 17) is the Wisdom the Angels beheld beside Yahuah before the mountains were settled (Mishlei 8:22–30) — pure, peaceable, gentle, full of mercy — and they sing it as the wisdom they have adored since before the deep. Third, **the patience of Iyov** (5:11): the Angels are the sons of Elohim who shouted for joy over Iyov's own creation while the morning stars sang together (Iyov 38:7) — the platform's cornerstone verse — and now they sing the endurance of that same man, and "the end of the Adon; that the Adon is very pitiful, and of tender mercy." The Angels are not imposed on this book; its hosts, its wisdom, and its Iyov all belong to the singers.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels who witnessed all thirty-nine books freely tie every moment *back* into the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings: the twelve tribes scattered echoing the diaspora of Israel; wisdom asked of Elohim who giveth liberally (Mishlei 2:1–6; Melachim Aleph 3); "every good and perfect gift… from the Father of lights, with whom is no variableness" tied to the luminaries set in the firmament (Bereshit 1:14–18) and the Unchanging One who says "I change not" (Malachi 3:6; Tehillim 102:26–27); the firstfruits offered of the word of truth (Shemot 23:19; Vayikra 23:10); "swift to hear, slow to speak" (Qoheleth 5:2; Mishlei 17:27); "love thy neighbour as thyself" — the royal law (Vayikra 19:18) — beside "do not commit adultery… do not kill" (Shemot 20:13–14); Avraham who believed Elohim, counted for righteousness (Bereshit 15:6), and offered Yitzchak on the altar (Bereshit 22), called "the Friend of Elohim" (Divrei-HaYamim Bet 20:7; Yeshayahu 41:8); Rachav who received the messengers and sent them out another way (Yehoshua 2); the tongue no man can tame set against the mouth that both blesses and curses (Tehillim 34:13; Mishlei 18:21); the wisdom from above beside Wisdom before the deep (Mishlei 8; Iyov 28); "Elohim resisteth the proud, but giveth grace to the humble" (Mishlei 3:34); the friendship of the world as the old spiritual adultery of the prophets (Hoshea; Yirmeyahu 3); the adversary who flees, remembered from the heavenly court (Iyov 1–2; Zecharyah 3:1–2); the vapour "what is your life" (Tehillim 39:5–6; 102:3; 144:4); the moth-eaten garment and the cankered gold of the hoarding rich (Yeshayahu 51:8); the wages of the hired not kept overnight, lest he cry unto Yahuah (Vayikra 19:13; Devarim 24:14–15); the early and the latter rain the husbandman waits for (Devarim 11:14; Yoel 2:23); the prophets as the pattern of suffering and patience; the endurance of Iyov and the Adon "very pitiful, and of tender mercy" (Iyov 42; No forward, post-letter reference is used.

# ALBUM I — THE ADON OF GLORY
### The Wisdom of Yeshua, the Crown of Life, and the Ache of His Coming · Yaakov 1–5 · eight songs

The letter is short, plain, and hard as a whetstone — the most practical ethics in the New Covenant, written to a people scattered and poor and under trial. Yaakov opens not with doctrine but with a demand that turns the world over: *count it all joy* when trials fall, for the testing of faith works patience, and the man who endures wins the crown of life. He tells them to ask Elohim for wisdom — He gives it liberally, upbraiding not — and to know that every good and perfect gift falls down from the Father of lights, in whom is no shadow of turning. From there the whetstone bites: be *doers* of the word and not hearers only; keep the royal law, "love thy neighbour," without respect of persons, for the faith of the Adon of glory has no gold ring in its eye; a faith with no works is a corpse — Avraham's altar and Rachav's scarlet line prove it. He turns to the tongue no man can tame, and to the wisdom from above that is pure and peaceable; to the friendship of the world that is enmity with Elohim, and the summons to submit, resist the adversary, and humble under the mighty hand; to the vapour a life is, here and gone. And at the last he lifts the scattered, waiting poor toward the one thing still to come: the reapers' withheld wages already crying into the ears of the Adon of sabaoth, and the husbandman waiting for the rain — *be patient, for the coming of the Adon draweth nigh; behold, the judge standeth before the door.* He leaves them praying — the prayer of faith that raised the sick, Eliyahu's prayer that shut and opened heaven, and the mercy that turns a wanderer home. The Angels sing all of it as the wisdom of Yeshua the Adon of glory, whom Yaakov's brother-hand can only call *Master* — testifying to Him, and confessing the hosts, the wisdom, and the Iyov the letter hands them as their own.

*Why this title:* 2:1 is the letter's christological crown and the only place Yaakov names his brother in full — "the faith of our Adon Yeshua the Mashiach, the Adon of glory" — the one title from which the whole album of His wisdom hangs.

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books; in the New Covenant the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. James supplies the warrant in its own words: the whole letter is "the wisdom that is from above" (3:15, 17) — the Wisdom the Angels adored beside Yahuah before the deep (Mishlei 8), sung now as their own.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. Every "Who sings" cell is the Angels. Yaakov never sings; the Angels sing *about* what he wrote and freely *quote* him — "count it all joy," "the faith of the Adon of glory," "faith without works is dead," "be patient… unto the coming" — always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to Yeshua. A human-centered line (the poor man in vile raiment, the rich with the gold ring, Avraham, Rachav, the fainting husbandman, sick Iyov, praying Eliyahu, the wanderer) means the Angels bear witness to it *as testimony to the Adon of glory;* it never means a human sings.
- **The Angels' own witness-stake honored — the hosts, the wisdom, the Iyov:** where the letter reaches into heaven, the Angels answer from heaven's side. "The Adon of sabaoth" of 5:4 is Adonai Tzevaot, the Adon of the *hosts* — the Commander of the very Angels who sing — and the reapers' withheld wages cry "into the ears" of their Adon (song 7). "The wisdom that is from above" (3:15, 17) is the Wisdom the Angels beheld before the mountains were settled (song 5; Mishlei 8:22–30). And "the patience of Iyov" (5:11) is the endurance of the man over whose creation the sons of Elohim shouted for joy while the morning stars sang together (Iyov 38:7 — the platform's cornerstone verse), sung in song 7. The Angels are not imposed on this book; its hosts, its wisdom, and its Iyov are theirs.
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song lands on the person of Yeshua the Adon of glory. Yaakov names Him outright only twice (1:1; 2:1), yet his whole letter is the teaching of Yeshua carried in a brother's hand — the Sermon on the Mount echoing throughout (count it joy; ask and receive; doers on the rock; blessed are the merciful; love thy neighbour; judge not; the moth and treasure; take no thought for the morrow; swear not) — and the Angels sing every wisdom-word as the wisdom of the Adon of glory, bearing witness to Him. Even the plainest ethic testifies to Him: the one who grew up beside Yeshua names himself only His bondservant and calls his brother "the Adon of glory."
- **Yeshua witnessed and quoted, never first-person-voiced:** Yeshua is reported, quoted, and marveled at throughout — "the Adon of glory," the mountain's own words, "the coming of the Adon draweth nigh" — never given a first-person singer. His finished work and His teaching are testified to, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were sung by the witnesses across the Old Covenant.
- **The epistle forward-lock — relocated to the coming:** with no biography of Yeshua to keep in order, the finished work and His teaching stand *behind* the ethics, proclaimed freely and fully. The single thing held *unresolved* is His **coming** — "be patient… unto the coming of the Adon… the husbandman waiteth for the precious fruit… the coming of the Adon draweth nigh… behold, the judge standeth before the door" (5:7–9) — carried as the album's one aching, forward-looking longing (song 7), introduced under the scattered, waiting poor and never narrated as an event already come.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* — the giving Elohim of Mishlei 2 and Shelomoh's plea (Melachim Aleph 3); the luminaries of Bereshit 1 and the Unchanging One of Malachi 3:6; the firstfruits of Shemot 23:19; the royal law of Vayikra 19:18 and the commands of Shemot 20; Avraham of Bereshit 15:6 and 22, "the Friend of Elohim" of Divrei-HaYamim Bet 20:7 and Yeshayahu 41:8; Rachav of Yehoshua 2; the wisdom before the deep of Mishlei 8 and Iyov 28; "Elohim resisteth the proud" of Mishlei 3:34; the adversary of Iyov 1–2 and Zecharyah 3; the vapour of Tehillim 39 and 144; the moth and canker of Yeshayahu 51:8; the withheld wages of Vayikra 19:13 and Devarim 24:15; the latter rain of Yoel 2:23; Iyov 42 and the tender-mercy of Shemot 34:6 and Tehillim 103:8; Eliyahu of Melachim Aleph 17–18; the covered sins of Mishlei 10:12 and the watchman of Yechezkel 33. The echoes of the Sermon on the Mount are not forward references but the Adon of glory's own teaching, already spoken and witnessed, standing behind the letter. No forward, post-letter reference is used.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Abba** / the Father (the **Father of lights**, 1:17), and **the Adon / Adonai** (never "Lord"). Where the received text reads "the Lord of sabaoth" it is rendered **the Adon of sabaoth** (Adonai Tzevaot, 5:4); "if the Lord will" is "if the Adon will" (4:15); "in the name of the Lord" is "in the name of the Adon" (5:10, 5:14); "the Lord shall raise him up" is "the Adon shall raise him up" (5:15); "the Lord is very pitiful" is "the Adon is very pitiful" (5:11); "the coming of the Lord" is "the coming of the Adon" (5:7–8). "God" is rendered **Elohim** throughout ("the Friend of Elohim," 2:23; "pure religion… before Elohim and the Father," 1:27). The adversary of 4:7 is tied to the satan of the heavenly court (Iyov; Zecharyah 3); pagan powers are never called "gods." Hebraic names throughout — Yaakov, Yeshua, Avraham, Yitzchak, Rachav, Iyov, Eliyahu, Shelomoh. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint:** the letter's weight — the trials and the fire, the poor sent away naked, Avraham's knife over his son, the tongue's deadly poison, the reapers' withheld wages, the sick and the sinner — is handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the Old-Covenant ties and the Angels' awe carrying the weight, never gore. The crown of life, the peaceable wisdom, the coming of the Adon, and the wanderer turned home are the triumph, and triumph is triumph.
