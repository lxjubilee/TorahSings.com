# EZRA — THE TWO ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Return and the Ready Scribe

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Ezra is ten chapters and two movements decades apart — the return and the raising of the house (1–6), and the coming of the ready scribe and the painful reckoning (7–10). Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the royal decrees ("thus says Koresh king of Persia"), the registers of the returned, the ritual and building markers, and the letters that fly to and from the Persian throne in place of the Genesis *toledot*.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Redeemer promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside the days of the return, with no later hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside an Ezra-moment song the aches (a decree that sends the exiles home, a foundation greeted with a shout no one could tell from weeping, a house rebuilt smaller than the first, a Passover lamb kept in a lesser court, the holy seed mingled) are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. The returning high priest is named *Yeshua* (Yeshua son of Yotsadak) — inside the story he is a man, and the shared name is never leveraged for hindsight. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Koresh, not Zerubbavel, not Yeshua the priest, not Ezra) and nothing derived from one; the prophets Chaggai and Zekharyah stir the work but are witnessed, never voiced; Ezra's confession-prayer (ch. 9) is witnessed and testified to by the Angels, never sung as Ezra. Impersonal creation the text supplies (the returned vessels of the house, the rebuilt altar, the temple foundation, the trumpets and cymbals of the Levites, the winter rain) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Two albums. Fifteen songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Ezra is sung. All ten chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes below it: the return-and-house arc holds nine songs, the reckoning arc holds six, and that is what each gets — the count follows the stories, never a ceiling, and nothing is padded. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE HOUSE ROSE THROUGH WEEPING
### The Return and the House · Ezra 1–6 · nine songs

A captive people is sent home by the very throne that held them, and the word passes through a foreign mouth: Yahuah has charged him to build a house at Yerushalayim. The vessels carried off for exile are counted back into holy hands; forty-two thousand names climb the road home; an altar rises out of fear before there is a house to hold it; and when the foundation is laid, the shout of the young cannot be told from the weeping of the old who remember the first house. The witnesses sing the halted years, the eye of Elohim that kept the builders' hands moving, the second throne that funds and finishes what the first began, twelve he-goats offered for twelve tribes not all come home, and a Passover kept in a lesser court — joy and ache in one sound, the whole record long.

*Why this title:* the house of these six chapters does not rise in triumph; it rises *through weeping* — the old men who had seen the first house wept aloud at the new foundation (3:12), the work stood halted for years under the letters of its enemies, and the finished house was smaller than the one Babel had burned. It rose anyway.

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the decree opens the album on the impossible mercy — the Veil lifted by a pagan proclamation; the vessels and the register carry the return home; the altar built in fear and the foundation's mixed cry are the album's aching heart, the Selah that falls where the text's own still point falls; the halted work is the Turn; the eye upon the elders is the quiet hinge where the building resumes; and the dedication and the Passover land the record — the house finished and the feast kept, joy and ache held in one sound to the end. Ezra 1–6 is already built as an emptying that turns to filling; the text's own order is the shape.

# ALBUM II — HE CAME TO TEACH, AND WEPT
### Ezra and the Reckoning · Ezra 7–10 · six songs

Decades after the house is dedicated, a ready scribe comes up from Bavel with the Torah in his hand and his heart set to seek it, to do it, and to teach it — and the hand of his Elohim turns a Persian king to grant his every request and fund him. He leads a second company home carrying a fortune in holy vessels and no soldiers to guard them — ashamed to ask, because he had told the king the hand of Elohim is upon all who seek Him. And then the victory curdles: he learns the holy seed has mingled with the peoples of the lands, tears the hair from his own head and beard, and sits appalled until the evening sacrifice — and the book ends not in triumph but in a whole people trembling in the winter rain, cutting away what they loved to keep the seed unmixed.

*Why this title:* Ezra came to teach the Torah (7:10) and found his people had already broken it; the same scribe who blessed the king for beautifying the house wept on the ground before it (10:1). He came to teach, and wept.

| 4 | **Weighed Out to the Priests** | Ezra 8:24–36 | The Angels, who kept the count and the road (the holy vessels invoked as image, echoing the vessels of the first return) | Silver and gold and vessels weighed out to twelve chief priests — "you are holy to Yahuah, and the vessels are holy; watch and keep them until you weigh them" in the chambers of the house — carried through the ambush country by the hand of Elohim and weighed back in, by number and by weight, every gram delivered whole |
| 5 | **He Sat Appalled Till Evening** | Ezra 9 | The Angels, who kept the silence with him and testified to his prayer (the Vav — the nail in His holy place, 9:8 — invoked) | The princes bring word that the holy seed has mingled, "and the hand of the princes has been chief in this trespass"; the scribe rends his garment and pulls the hair from his head and his beard and sits appalled until the evening sacrifice — then falls on his knees, spreads his hands, and confesses a guilt heaped over their heads, ashamed to lift his face, for a remnant left by grace cannot stand before Him in this |
| 6 | **They Trembled in the Rain** | Ezra 10 | The Angels, who watched a people tremble in the winter rain and cut away what they loved | While he prays and weeps a great congregation gathers weeping, and one voice says "yet now there is hope in Israel concerning this"; they covenant to put away the foreign wives, and the men sit in the open street of the house, trembling for the matter and for the great rain, while it is examined house by house — the book closing not on a shout but on a bare list of the guilty and a seed kept whole at a terrible cost |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the ready scribe and the king's turned heart open the album in wonder; the register and the unguarded caravan carry the second return home; the vessels weighed out are the still, careful center — a holy trust borne through danger; the confession is the Turn and the Selah at once, grief held in silence until the evening sacrifice; and the trembling assembly lands the record, and the book, on an unresolved and sober note the text refuses to soften. Ezra 7–10 is one descent from devotion into reckoning; the text's own order is the arc.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 7 the ready scribe comes up and the king's letter · 8:1–14 the register of his company · 8:15–23 the fast at Ahava and the refused escort · 8:24–36 the vessels weighed out, carried, and weighed in · 9 the mingled seed and the confession · 10 the covenant to put away and the trembling assembly. Six for six.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy; the Persian kings' decrees, which invoke "the Elohim of heaven," are rendered as the text renders them — Elohim. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Ezra-moment song the aches — a decree that sends the exiles home, a foundation greeted with a shout no one could tell from weeping, a house rebuilt smaller than the first, the twelve tribes remembered though most are still scattered, a Passover lamb kept in a lesser court, the holy seed mingled — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. The high priest Yeshua son of Yotsadak is a man in the story; the shared name is never leveraged for later hindsight. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside the days of the return, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Kaph keyed to *keli* (the cupped palm that held the vessels home), the Ayin keyed to the eye of Elohim upon the elders (5:5), the Vav keyed to the nail in His holy place (9:8), the Letters as the ledger-keepers of the two registers and of Ezra's line to Aharon. Koresh, Daryavesh, Sheshbatsar, Zerubbavel, Yeshua the priest, Tatnai, and Ezra are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the prophets Chaggai and Zekharyah stir the work but are witnessed, never voiced; Ezra's confession-prayer is testified to from heaven's side, never sung as his. The returned vessels, the rebuilt altar, the temple foundation, the trumpets and cymbals, the Passover lamb, the river Ahava, and the winter rain are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the five thousand four hundred vessels, the forty-two thousand three hundred and sixty who returned, the twelve he-goats for the twelve tribes, the twelve chief priests, and the genealogies of chapters 2 and 8.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
