# 1 CORINTHIANS — THE FIVE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for Shaul's First Letter to the Assembly at Corinth

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/John - The Six Albums.md`, `pitches/Matthew - The Eleven Albums.md`, and the Gospel frontier of `pitches/_New Testament - The Gospels.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. 1 Corinthians is **Epistle** — a letter, not a life. There is no biography of Yeshua to lock here; instead the "units" are the letter's own movements — the seams Shaul himself cuts with his *"now concerning"* (peri de) headings, his *"know ye not"* refrains, his *"moreover, brethren, I declare unto you,"* and the great hinge *"and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way."* The book's own argument draws the boundaries.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fell silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Testament the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels), aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. At the incarnation that changed: the Word — the Davar by whom the very letters were made — came in the flesh in Yeshua, so **all the Picture Letters now live in Him and fall silent as separate singers.** Therefore in this letter, as in the Gospels, **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** And here the text itself hands the Angels their warrant three times over: the apostles are made "a spectacle unto the world, and to angels" (4:9); the woman is to keep her covering "because of the angels" (11:10); and love is weighed even against "the tongues of men and of angels" (13:1). The Angels of this platform are named, again and again, as the watching host — so they take up the song.

**The humans never sing — the Angels testify to what Shaul wrote.** Not Shaul, not Apollos, not Kepha, not the Corinthians. The Angels are the only voice. They sing *about* what Shaul unveiled and freely *quote* him — "we preach the Mashiach crucified," "the Mashiach our Pesach," "that Rock was the Mashiach," "this is my body," "O death, where is thy sting" — always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to Yeshua. A song that centers on the letter's argument bears witness *as testimony to Him;* it never means the human sings.

**Epistle chronology — the forward-lock adapts: the finished work proclaimed freely, the Return held.** In the Gospels the Angels were forward-locked to the narrative, never naming the cross before its hour. **A letter written after the resurrection is different.** The accomplished work of Yeshua — the cross, the resurrection on the third day, the ascension, the outpouring of the Ruach — is now history, and the Angels proclaim it **freely and fully:** "the Mashiach died for our sins according to the scriptures… and rose again the third day." The ONE thing still held as unresolved, aching longing is **His return, and the resurrection not yet consummated** — the body not yet raised imperishable, the last enemy not yet destroyed. The letter itself carries that ache: "waiting for the coming of our Adon Yeshua" (1:7); "till he come" (11:26); "then face to face" (13:12); "at the last trump… the dead shall be raised" (15:52); and the final cry, "Marana tha — our Adon, come" (16:22). The finished work is sung as triumph; only the Return is held open.

**Christocentric — about "the who."** Every song is ultimately about the person of Yeshua. Even where Shaul corrects a fracture, a lawsuit, a marriage, a table-disorder, the Angels turn every unit to Him — the crucified wisdom of Elohim, the Pesach lamb, the Rock in the wilderness, the broken bread, the risen firstfruits, the last Adam.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels, who witnessed all thirty-nine books, tie every movement back to the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings: "I will destroy the wisdom of the wise" (Yeshayahu 29:14); "let him that glories glory in Yahuah" (Yirmeyahu 9:24); "eye hath not seen" (Yeshayahu 64:4); the Pesach lamb and the unleavened bread (Shemot 12); the Shema, "one Elohim" (Devarim 6:4); the un-muzzled ox (Devarim 25:4); the cloud, the sea, and the Rock that followed (Shemot 17; Bemidbar 20); the first Adam of the dust (Bereshit 2:7); "death is swallowed up in victory" (Yeshayahu 25:8); "O death, where is thy sting" (Hoshea 13:14); "with men of other tongues" (Yeshayahu 28:11–12); and the resurrection "according to the scriptures" (Yeshayahu 53; Tehillim 16). No forward reference is used.

> *Five albums. Thirty-one songs. No honest zeros — every movement of Shaul's argument is sung. Every chapter 1–16 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its argument holds. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE MASHIACH CRUCIFIED
### The Cross, the Wisdom of Elohim, and the End of Boasting · 1 Corinthians 1–4 · nine songs

The letter opens with a longing planted in its very first lines — an assembly "waiting for the coming of our Adon Yeshua" — and then turns at once to the fracture: *I am of Shaul, I am of Apollos, I am of Kepha.* Against every faction Shaul lifts one thing only: the cross. To the perishing it is foolishness; to the called it is the power and the wisdom of Elohim. The Angels sing the great inversion — the wisdom of the wise brought to nothing, the weak and the base and the things that are not chosen to shame the mighty — and they tie it backward to the prophets who said it first. The album lands where Shaul lands it: the apostles a spectacle to the world and to angels, fools for the Mashiach's sake, and no glory left for any man.

*Why this title:* 1:23 and 2:2 are the album's spine — "we preach the Mashiach crucified… I determined not to know any thing among you, save Yeshua the Mashiach, and him crucified."

# ALBUM II — THE MASHIACH OUR PESACH
### The Leaven Purged, the Body the Ruach's Temple, and the Calling · 1 Corinthians 5–7 · five songs

From the cross Shaul turns to the house it purchased. A sin is tolerated that even the nations would not name, and the answer is the oldest festival image of all: purge the old leaven, *for the Mashiach our Pesach is sacrificed for us* — the lamb of Mitzrayim named at last. Then the disputes: brothers dragging brothers before the unjust, when such were washed and sanctified and justified; bodies treated as nothing, when the body is bought with a price and made the Ruach's own temple. And the calling — marriage and singleness held with open hands, because the time is short and the fashion of this world is passing away. The Angels tie the whole album backward to the Passover and forward, quietly, to the day that is coming.

*Why this title:* 5:7 is the album's spine and its sharpest backward tie — "purge out therefore the old leaven… for even the Mashiach our Pesach is sacrificed for us."

# ALBUM III — THE ROCK THAT FOLLOWED
### Freedom, Love, the Wilderness Rock, and the Table of the Adon · 1 Corinthians 8–11 · six songs

Now the long argument on freedom and love. May a believer eat what was offered to idols? Knowledge says yes; love asks after the weak brother and lays its own rights down. Shaul makes himself the example — an apostle who takes no wage, who becomes all things to all men, who runs as for an incorruptible crown. Then the album's summit and its warning: the fathers all passed through the sea and all drank of a spiritual Rock that followed them — *and that Rock was the Mashiach* — yet were overthrown in the wilderness. So flee idolatry, for the cup of blessing is the communion of His blood and the one bread makes one body. The album closes at the table itself — the head of the Mashiach is Elohim, the woman covered "because of the angels," and the bread broken with the words that show the Adon's death *till he come.*

*Why this title:* 10:4 is the album's summit and its deepest backward tie — "they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and that Rock was the Mashiach."

# ALBUM IV — THE MORE EXCELLENT WAY
### The Gifts, the One Body, and Love the Greatest · 1 Corinthians 12–14 · five songs

The gifts come next — and the one Ruach who deals them out. No one can even call Yeshua the Adon but by the Ruach Kodesh; and the many gifts, like the many members of a body, are one. The foot cannot leave because it is not the hand; the eye cannot dismiss the hand it needs. Then the hinge Shaul cuts himself — *and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way* — and the letter rises to its own summit: love. Though a man speak with the tongues of men and of angels and have not love, he is nothing. Faith, hope, love abide; the greatest is love. The album closes bringing the gifts to order — prophecy above tongues for the building up of all, and everything done decently and in peace, for Elohim is not the author of confusion.

*Why this title:* 12:31 is the album's hinge and 13:13 its summit — "yet shew I unto you a more excellent way… now abideth faith, hope, love, these three; but the greatest of these is love."

# ALBUM V — THE LAST TRUMPET
### The Resurrection, the Last Adam, and "Our Adon, Come" · 1 Corinthians 15–16 · six songs

The letter climbs to its great summit — the resurrection. Shaul delivers the gospel he received: the Mashiach died for our sins according to the scriptures, was buried, rose again the third day, and was seen. And he draws the line: if the Mashiach be not raised, all is vain — but now He is risen, the firstfruits of them that slept, and as in Adam all die, in the Mashiach shall all be made alive. The first Adam was a living soul; the last Adam, a quickening spirit. Then the trumpet no ear has yet heard: we shall all be changed, in a moment, at the last trump — the dead raised imperishable, death swallowed up in victory, the sting undone. The album — and the letter — closes on the ache made a prayer: the collection gathered, the great door opened, and the last cry, *Marana tha — our Adon, come.*

*Why this title:* 15:52 is the album's summit and the letter's held ache — "the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed" — the finished victory awaiting its last, unblown note.

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** through all thirty-nine Old-Testament books the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together;* here the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. This letter hands the Angels their warrant by name three times: the apostles "a spectacle… to angels" (4:9), the covering worn "because of the angels" (11:10), and love weighed against "the tongues… of angels" (13:1). The Angels of this platform are the letter's own named witnesses, and they take up the song.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by Shaul, Apollos, Kepha, or the Corinthians. Every "Who sings" cell in all five albums is the Angels. They sing *about* what Shaul wrote and freely *quote* him — "is the Mashiach divided?", "the Mashiach our Pesach", "that Rock was the Mashiach", "the greatest of these is love", "O death, where is thy sting", "Marana tha" — always framed from their own heavenly-witness perspective, testifying to Yeshua. A song that centers on the letter's argument means the Angels sing *about* it as witness to Him; it never means the human sings.
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song turns on the person of Yeshua — the crucified wisdom and power of Elohim, the Pesach lamb, the one foundation, the Rock in the wilderness, the broken bread and the covenant-cup, the risen firstfruits, the last Adam, the Adon who is to come. Even the songs that begin in a fracture, a lawsuit, a marriage, a table-disorder, or a disordered assembly are resolved by the Angels onto Him.
- **The epistle forward-lock — the finished work proclaimed freely, His return held unresolved:** because the letter is written after the resurrection, the accomplished work of Yeshua — the cross, the burial, the rising on the third day, the ascension, the gift of the Ruach — is proclaimed **fully and freely** ("He Rose the Third Day, According to the Scriptures"; "This Is My Body"; "That Rock Was the Mashiach"). The ONE thing held as unresolved, aching longing is **His return and the resurrection not yet consummated** — carried by the letter's own words: "waiting for the coming of our Adon" (1:7), "the fashion of this world passeth away" (7:31), "till he come" (11:26), "then face to face" (13:12), "at the last trump… the dead shall be raised" (15:52), and the closing cry "Marana tha" (16:22). The victory is sung as certain; only the trumpet, and the face-to-face day, are held open.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* into the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings — "the wisdom of the wise" of Yeshayahu 29:14; "glory in Yahuah" of Yirmeyahu 9:24; "eye hath not seen" of Yeshayahu 64:4; the Pesach lamb and unleavened bread of Shemot 12; the Shema of Devarim 6:4; the un-muzzled ox of Devarim 25:4; the cloud, the sea, and the smitten rock of Shemot 17 and Bemidbar 20; the dust-formed Adam of Bereshit 2:7; "death is swallowed up in victory" of Yeshayahu 25:8; "O death, where is thy sting" of Hoshea 13:14; "men of other tongues" of Yeshayahu 28:11–12; and the resurrection "according to the scriptures" of Yeshayahu 53 and Tehillim 16. No forward reference is used.
- **Restraint:** the cross, the leaven-sin, the profaned Supper, and the sting of death are all handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the Old-Covenant ties and the Angels' awe carrying the weight, never gore. Triumph is triumph — the risen firstfruits and the swallowed-up death are sung as victory.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **Ben-Elohim**, the **last Adam** / the **Adon from heaven**, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach HaKodesh** (feminine — written with no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, **Adon / Adonai**. Hebraic names and places throughout — Shaul, Apollos, Kepha, Timotheos, Stephanas, Yaakov, Ephesus, Yerushalayim, Mitzrayim, Moshe, Yisrael, the Yehudim; and "Marana tha" and "Anathema" carried in their own tongue. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
