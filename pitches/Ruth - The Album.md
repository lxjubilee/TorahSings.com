**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Ruth is four chapters and one continuous story, so it is one album. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the famine-and-death opening, the road home and the vow, the harvest field, the threshing floor, the redemption at the gate, and the birth that runs out into Ruth's own *toledot* formula ("these are the generations of Perets," 4:18) in place of the Genesis genealogies.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Redeemer promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside the days of the judges, with no later hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Ruth-moment song the *go'el* (the kinsman-redeemer, the near one who buys back the lost and covers the widow) and the genealogy that runs to David and past the song's own sight are carried as mystery and longing, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Ruth, not Naomi, not Boaz, not Orpah) or anything derived from one; impersonal creation the text supplies (the barley and the harvest, the gleaned sheaves, the threshing floor, the spread garment-wing — the *kanaf* — the drawn-off sandal, the gate) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *One album. Six songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Ruth is sung. All four chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes below it: this book holds six songs, and six is what it gets. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — A REDEEMER FOR THE EMPTY HOUSE
### Emptiness and the Kinsman-Redeemer · Ruth 1–4 · six songs

A famine empties the house of bread, and a family that fled to Moav to live comes back three graves lighter — one bitter widow who asks to be called Mara, "empty," because she went out full and Yahuah brought her home with nothing. But a foreign daughter-in-law will not let go, and heaven steers her gleaning feet into the one field that holds a *go'el* — a near kinsman with the right to buy back the lost. The witnesses sing the whole hidden rescue: the wing spread over a stranger, the plea at the midnight threshing floor, the sandal drawn off at the gate, the dead man's name purchased back into the living, and a son laid at last on an empty lap — the line that will run to David, and past where the song can see.

*Why this title:* Naomi's house is hollowed out by famine and death — "I went out full, and Yahuah has brought me home empty" (1:21) — and the whole book is the raising up of a *go'el* for that empty house: Boaz redeems the field, the widow, and the dead man's name, and the child restores Naomi's life and founds the line of David. The redeemer is the answer to the emptiness; the far end of the redeemer is held as longing.

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the emptying opens the album in grief — the Veil, the ache stated; the vow on the road is the first light, loyalty answering loss; the harvest field is the hidden providence, the "chance" that was a hand; the midnight wing is the intimate Selah; the sandal at the gate is the Declaration, redemption made law before ten witnesses; and the son laid on Naomi's lap is the Benediction — the empty house filled and the line begun. Ruth is already built as an emptying that turns to filling — a chiasm of empty and full — so the text's own order *is* the artistic shape; no reordering improves on it.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1:1–5 the famine and the deaths · 1:6–22 the road home, Orpah's kiss, Ruth's vow, and Mara at Beit-Lechem · 2 the gleaning and the wing of refuge · 3 the threshing floor and the plea to the *go'el* · 4:1–12 the redemption and the sandal at the gate · 4:13–22 the birth of Oved and the *toledot* to David. Six for six.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine); Shaddai used as the text names Him ("Shaddai has dealt bitterly," 1:20–21). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Ruth-moment song the messianic ache — the *go'el* who buys back the lost and covers the widow, the dead man's name raised into the living, the line running out toward an anointed one — is carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. David is named because the text names him (4:17, 22); the far end of his line is held as a longing the witnesses cannot yet see. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside the days of the judges, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Kaph keyed to *kanaf* (the wing/corner spread over her), the Gimel keyed to *go'el* (the near one whose foot walks up to buy back), the Letters as the ledger-keepers of the genealogy. Ruth, Naomi, Boaz, and Orpah are *witnessed*, never made the singer; Ruth's own vow and Boaz's blessing are testified to from heaven's side, never sung as hers or his. The barley and the harvest, the gleaned sheaves, the threshing floor, the spread garment-wing, the drawn-off sandal, and the gate are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the ten years in Moav, the six measures of barley, the ephah, the ten elders at the gate, the ten generations of the closing *toledot*, "better than seven sons."
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
