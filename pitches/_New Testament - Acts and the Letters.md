## Where this stands in the frontier

The Gospels sang the Word made flesh. **Acts** is what happens next: the risen, ascended Yeshua keeps working — now by the Ruach HaKodesh, through His emissaries — and the good news runs from Yerushalayim to Rome. Then the **letters** arrive, written back to the assemblies that mission planted, unfolding *what it all means*. This batch carries the reader from the ascension (Acts 1) through the parousia-longing of the Thessalonian letters — the first ten books past the four Gospels.

Two genres, one ruleset, one honest adaptation:

- **Acts is narrative** — and it obeys the Gospels' rule exactly: eye-witness chronology, forward-locked. The Angels unveil events in order (the ascension before Shavuot, the Ruach poured out before the mission, Shaul turned before his journeys). Here the Angels are often *literal actors* the text supplies — the two men in white, the prison-opening angel, the angels to Cornelius and Kepha and Herodus, the angel in the storm — and those ARE the Angels of this platform.
- **The letters are not narrative.** There is no biography of Yeshua to lock, so the forward-lock **relocates**: because each letter is written *after* the resurrection, the finished work — incarnation, cross, resurrection, ascension, the gift of the Ruach — is proclaimed *freely and fully*. The one thing still held as unresolved, aching longing is **His return** and the redemption not yet consummated. That single ache is the through-line of the whole batch, and it becomes the very heart of 1–2 Thessalonians.

Everything else holds from the Gospels: **only the Angels sing** (the Picture Letters live in the incarnate Word; the humans never sing — they are witnessed and quoted, never voiced); **christocentric** — every song is about the person of Yeshua; **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced**; **Old-Covenant cross-reference free and backward-only**; **OHI sacred names** throughout.

### The summits

- **Acts** opens on the promise fallen as fire (Shavuot) and ends with the good news in Rome, "no man forbidding him" — the ache of Yeshua's return (1:11) carried the whole way.
- **Romans** climbs to "there is no condemnation" (ch 8) — the Ruach, the cry of Abba, the groaning creation, and "nothing shall separate us."
- **1 Corinthians** lands the resurrection (ch 15: the firstfruits, the last Adam, the last trumpet) and love as the more excellent way (ch 13), closing on "Marana tha — our Adon, come."
- **2 Corinthians** sings the new-covenant glory and the strength made perfect in weakness — the thorn, and the treasure in jars of clay.
- **Galatians** turns on 2:20 — "I am crucified with the Mashiach: nevertheless I live" — and the fruit of the Ruach.
- **Ephesians** opens on the blessing-hymn (chosen before the foundation) and closes on the whole armor of Elohim.
- **Philippians** carries the Mashiach-hymn — He emptied Himself, and was exalted, that every knee should bow.
- **Colossians** sings the cosmic Christ-hymn — the image of the invisible Elohim, in whom all things hold together.
- **1 & 2 Thessalonians** are the parousia letters — caught up to meet the Adon in the air; and the day *not yet*, His revealing with the mighty angels in flaming fire.

### The angels' own warrant

This batch keeps handing the platform its own justification from the text itself — the Angels do not have to be imposed on these books; the books name them. "A spectacle unto angels" (1 Cor 4:9); "because of the angels" (1 Cor 11:10); "the tongues of angels" (1 Cor 13:1); the Torah "ordained by angels" (Gal 3:19); the assembly teaching the principalities "the manifold wisdom of Elohim" (Eph 3:10); "the voice of the archangel, and the trump of Elohim" (1 Thess 4:16); "his mighty angels, in flaming fire" (2 Thess 1:7) — and, all through Acts, the angels who open prisons and stand in storms.

## Compliance — the ruleset (applies to all ten)

1. **Only the Angels sing.** The Picture Letters are silent; the humans never sing. Every "Who sings" cell across all 206 songs is the Angels — including the text-supplied angels of Acts and the Thessalonian letters, who are the Angels of this platform.
2. **The Ruach witnessed, never voiced.** At Shavuot and the outpourings the Ruach HaKodesh (divine) is witnessed by the Angels, never made a first-person singer — the same reverence Yahuah and Yeshua are given.
3. **Christocentric** — every song about the person of Yeshua; human moments testify to Him.
4. **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced.**
5. **Chronology:** Acts is forward-locked eye-witness narrative; the letters proclaim the finished work freely and hold only His **return** as unresolved longing.
6. **Old-Covenant cross-reference free and backward-only.**
7. **Sacred names — OHI throughout:** Yeshua (never "Jesus"), the Mashiach (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), Yahuah, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine), Abba, Adon/Adonai. Hebraic names and places (Shaul, Kepha, Damaseq, Yerushalayim, Antiochia…). No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" in any title or copy. The "unknown God" altar of Acts 17 rendered "the Unknown El"; pagan idols never called "gods."
8. **Restraint** — the deaths, beatings, and shipwreck witnessed from heaven's side, grave and family-friendly. Each book's triumph is triumph.
9. **Hebraic Music Mandate** — the Hebraic music type primary or secondary in every track.
10. **Posture** — not offered as canon. A reading, held at arm's length and turned. Nothing publishes without approval per `J:\README-CONTRACT.md`.
