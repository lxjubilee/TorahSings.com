# GALATIANS — THE TWO ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for Shaul's Letter to the Assemblies of Galatia

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/John - The Six Albums.md` and the New-Testament ruleset in `pitches/_New Testament - The Gospels.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Galatians is **Epistle** — a letter, not a narrative. It has no biography of Yeshua to unveil in order; its "units" are the letter's own **movements of argument** — the greeting and the self-giving (1:1–5), the astonished "no other gospel" (1:6–10), the gospel received by revelation (1:11–24), the meeting at Yerushalayim (2:1–10), Kepha withstood at Antiochia (2:11–21), the foolish Galatians and the Ruach received by faith (3:1–5), Avraham's faith (3:6–9), the curse borne on the tree (3:10–14), the one seed and the unbreakable promise (3:15–18), the Torah as guardian until the Mashiach (3:19–25), all made sons and heirs (3:26–29), the fullness of time and the cry of Abba (4:1–7), the travail until the Mashiach be formed (4:8–20), Hagar and Sarah the two covenants (4:21–31), the freedom for which He set us free (5:1–6), love that fulfils the whole Torah (5:7–15), the fruit of the Ruach (5:16–26), the bearing of burdens and the harvest not yet reaped (6:1–10), and the boast in nothing but the cross, the new creation (6:11–18). The seams are the letter's own turns — the vocatives ("O foolish Galatians"), the rhetorical hinges ("wherefore then the Torah?"), the datelines ("fourteen years after"), and the "but when… came" formulas.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Covenant the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels) — aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. At the incarnation that changed: the Word — the Davar by whom the letters themselves were made — came in the flesh in Yeshua, and **the Picture Letters now live in Him and fall silent as separate singers.** Therefore in this letter, as in the Gospels, **the only singers are the Angels — the sons of Elohim — never a Picture-Letter persona.** And Galatians hands the Angels their own place in the story twice over: **the Torah "was ordained by angels in the hand of a mediator"** (3:19) — the very host that administered the Torah at Sinai now sings it laying down its guardianship before the Mashiach; and Shaul testifies the Galatians once **"received me… as an angel of Elohim, even as the Mashiach Yeshua"** (4:14) — the Angels sing over the messenger the assemblies once welcomed as one of them. These are the Angels of this platform, given their footing by the text itself.

**Epistle, not narrative — the forward-lock adapts.** In the Gospels the Angels unveiled Yeshua in narrative order, holding the cross forward as unspoken mystery. A letter has no such story to lock: Galatians is **written after the resurrection**, and Shaul proclaims the finished work *freely and fully* — "who gave Himself for our sins, that He might deliver us from this present evil age" (1:4); "I am crucified with the Mashiach, nevertheless I live" (2:20); "the Mashiach hath redeemed us from the curse… being made a curse for us" (3:13). So the Angels proclaim it fully too — the cross and the empty tomb sung as accomplished triumph, not withheld. **The one thing still held as aching, unresolved longing is the not-yet:** the harvest not yet reaped — "in due season we shall reap, if we faint not" (6:9) — and "we through the Ruach wait for the hope of righteousness by faith" (5:5). That waiting is the letter's Selah, and the Angels carry it as the last ache of the whole catalog.

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced — and Shaul freely quoted.** The singer is always the Angels. **Yeshua is witnessed and testified to in awe — never a first-person singer.** And the Angels sing *about* what Shaul wrote and freely *quote* him — "I am crucified with the Mashiach," "O foolish Galatians," "for freedom the Mashiach hath made us free" — always framed from their own heavenly witness, testifying. **Christocentric, not grammatically rigid:** a song may centre on a human moment — the persecutor turned preacher, Kepha rebuked at the table, the assemblies bewitched, Shaul in travail — *as testimony to Yeshua,* for the whole letter is written that the Mashiach be formed in His people (4:19) and that they stand fast in the freedom He bought (5:1). A human-centred song bears witness to Yeshua; it never means the human sings.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — always open, and backward only.** The Angels, who witnessed all thirty-nine books, freely tie every moment back to the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings: Avraham believed and it was reckoned to him for righteousness (Gen 15:6); "in thee shall all nations be blessed" (Gen 12:3); the separated one called from the womb (Yirmeyahu 1:5; Yeshayahu 49:1); "cursed is every one that continueth not… in the book of the Torah" (Devarim 27:26); "the just shall live by faith" (Havakuk 2:4); "cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree" (Devarim 21:23); the promise cut with Avraham and his seed (Gen 15; 17); the Torah given at Sinai by the hand of a mediator (Shemot 19–20; Devarim 5); Hagar and Sarah, the bondwoman and the free, the two covenants (Gen 16; 21); "rejoice, thou barren that bearest not" (Yeshayahu 54:1); "thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself" (Vayikra 19:18); and the new creation, witnessed by the same host that sang the first one (Bereshit 1; Iyov 38:7). No forward, post-letter reference is used.

> *Two albums. Nineteen songs. No honest zeros — every movement of Shaul's letter is sung. Every chapter 1–6 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as the argument holds. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — I AM CRUCIFIED WITH THE MASHIACH
### The Gospel Received and Defended · Galatians 1:1–3:18 · nine songs

The letter opens like a man on his feet before the words are even warm — no thanksgiving, only the cross: grace and peace *from the One who gave Himself for our sins.* Shaul is astonished that the assemblies are already turning to another gospel, and he will not have it — *though an angel from heaven preach it, let him be anathema.* He tells how the gospel came to him, not from men but by the unveiling of Yeshua Himself; how the hunter of the assembly became its herald; how the pillars at Yerushalayim gave him the right hand of fellowship; and how he withstood Kepha to his face at Antiochia to keep the truth of the gospel — *a man is not justified by the works of the Torah, but by the faith of Yeshua the Mashiach.* Then the argument turns to Avraham, who believed and was reckoned righteous; to the curse borne on a tree; and to the one seed, the promise no Torah could annul.

*Why this title:* 2:20 is the album's spine and the whole letter in one confession — the Angels testifying what Shaul wrote: "I am crucified with the Mashiach: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but the Mashiach liveth in me."

# ALBUM II — THE FULLNESS OF TIME
### Sons, Freedom, and the New Creation · Galatians 3:19–6:18 · ten songs

Now the letter climbs into its glory. If the promise stands, *wherefore then the Torah?* — and the Angels who once ordained it at Sinai sing it laying down its guardianship, a tutor that led the child by the hand until the Mashiach came. Then the great turn: no longer servants but sons — clothed with the Mashiach, neither Jew nor Greek, all one, heirs of the promise. *When the fullness of the time was come, Elohim sent forth His Son… crying, Abba, Father.* Shaul travails in birth again until the Mashiach be formed in them; he sets Hagar against Sarah, the bondwoman against the free, and cries with the prophet, *rejoice, thou barren.* And the letter lands where it has been driving: *for freedom the Mashiach hath set us free* — a freedom that is not the flesh let loose but the Ruach bearing its fruit, burdens carried, a harvest awaited, and a boast in nothing at all except the cross, where the world was crucified and a new creation began.

*Why this title:* 4:4 is the album's summit and the most Christocentric line in the letter — the sending of the Son, the whole gospel in a heartbeat: "when the fullness of the time was come, Elohim sent forth His Son."

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** in this letter, as in the Gospels, the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. Every "Who sings" cell in both albums is the Angels.
- **The platform's own footing in the text honored:** Galatians seats the Angels twice — **"the Torah… was ordained by angels in the hand of a mediator"** (3:19), sung by that very host in "The Guardian We Delivered Steps Aside," and **"ye received me… as an angel of Elohim"** (4:14), sung over the welcomed messenger in "Until the Mashiach Be Formed in You." Both are the Angels of this platform, never a human's voice — the letter's counterparts to John's ladder-angels (1:51) and tomb-angels (20:12). And **"though… an angel from heaven preach any other gospel… let him be accursed"** (1:8) is sung by the Angels as their own binding — even the host of heaven stands under the one gospel.
- **Epistle genre — the forward-lock adapted, not abandoned:** Galatians is written after the resurrection, so the accomplished work is proclaimed *fully* — the self-giving (1:4), "I am crucified with the Mashiach" (2:20), the curse borne and redemption won (3:13) — sung by the Angels as finished triumph, never withheld as mystery. **The one thing held unresolved is the not-yet:** "we through the Ruach wait for the hope of righteousness" (5:5) and "in due season we shall reap, if we faint not" (6:9). These are the letter's aching Selah — the last longing of the catalog — carried in "For Freedom the Mashiach Set Us Free" and "Bear the Burden; Faint Not."
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song is ultimately about the person of Yeshua — who gave Himself (1:4), who lives in His people (2:20), the one Seed (3:16), sent in the fullness of time (4:4), in whom there is a new creation (6:15). Human-centred songs — the turned persecutor, Kepha rebuked, the bewitched assemblies, Shaul in travail — testify to Him.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. The Angels sing *about* Shaul and freely *quote* him — "I am crucified with the Mashiach," "O foolish Galatians," "for freedom the Mashiach hath made us free," "Abba, Father," "far be it from me that I should boast, save in the cross" — always framed from the Angels' own witness, testifying. A human-centred song means the Angels sing about that human as a witness to Yeshua; it never means the human sings.
- **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced:** He is reported and marvelled at by the Angels throughout — the self-given One, the crucified and living Mashiach, the Son sent forth, never given a first-person singer.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* — Avraham's faith and the counted stars (Bereshit 15:6), "in thee shall all nations be blessed" (Bereshit 12:3), the prophet called from the womb (Yirmeyahu 1:5; Yeshayahu 49:1), the curse of Devarim 27:26 and 21:23, "the just shall live by faith" (Havakuk 2:4), the covenant and its four hundred and thirty years (Shemot 12:40), the Torah given at Sinai (Shemot 19–20; Devarim 5), Hagar and Sarah (Bereshit 16; 21), "rejoice, thou barren" (Yeshayahu 54:1), "love thy neighbour" (Vayikra 19:18), and the new creation set beside the first (Bereshit 1; Iyov 38:7). No forward, post-letter reference is used.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** / **HaMashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms), **Ben-Elohim**, **Yahuah**, **Elohim**, **Ruach HaKodesh** / the **Ruach** (feminine — no stacked English "the"), **Abba** / the Father, **Adon** / **our Adon Yeshua the Mashiach**, and the **Torah** (never "the law" as a title). Hebraic names and places throughout — Shaul, Kepha, Yaakov, Bar-Naba, Titos, Yochanan, Avraham, Sarah, Hagar, Yerushalayim, Antiochia; the prophets named Yirmeyahu, Yeshayahu, Havakuk. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **No textually-disputed passages:** Galatians carries none of the manuscript cruxes that flagged John (7:53–8:11) or Mark (16:9–20); the letter is textually secure, so no scholarly-caution flag is needed here beyond the standing posture.
- **Restraint:** the sharp moments — the anathema, the rebuke of Kepha at Antiochia, the curse borne on the tree, the marks in Shaul's body — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the Old-Covenant ties and the Angels' awe carrying the weight, never gore. Triumph is triumph — the freedom bought and the new creation.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
