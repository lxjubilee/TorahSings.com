# 2 KINGS — THE NINE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Whirlwind and the Fall

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, and the pitches through `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. 2 Kings has no single formula like the Genesis *toledot*; its own seams are the regnal formulas ("in the Nth year of… began to reign"), the prophet-cycle openings ("and it came to pass"), the miracle notices of the Elisha cycle, and the accession-and-death frames of each king. Every song below is one narrative unit drawn at those seams.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Son promised but not yet unveiled. 2 Kings hands the song some of the richest angelic-witness scenes in all Scripture: the **chariot of fire and horses of fire that took Eliyahu up in the whirlwind** (2), the **mountain full of horses and chariots of fire around Elisha** (6), the **Angel of Yahuah who struck a hundred fourscore and five thousand in the Assyrian camp in one night** (19) — and the messenger of Yahuah who sends Eliyahu down to a doomed king (1). **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own hour, with no New-Testament hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a 2-Kings-moment song the aches (a prophet taken up without dying; a leper made clean by sevenfold washing; a dead man raised by a prophet's bones; a lost scroll found in the rubble; the exile as covenant-curse come due) are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled; no cross, no Messiah, no fulfillment language enters a song. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Eliyahu, not Elisha, not Na'aman, not Chizqiyahu, not Yoshiyahu) or anything derived from one; impersonal creation the text supplies (the mantle that struck the water, the jar of oil that kept pouring, the floating axe-head, the Jordan Na'aman dipped in seven times, the sundial's shadow, the found scroll of the law) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Nine albums. Fifty-three songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of 2 Kings is sung. All twenty-five chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — A CHARIOT INSTEAD OF A GRAVE
### Eliyahu: Fire and the Whirlwind · 2 Kings 1–2 · four songs

A king who fell through a lattice sends past the Elohim of Israel to a foreign god to ask whether he will live, and fire answers the question with death; then the prophet who called that fire down is taken by fire himself — a whirlwind and a chariot where every other prophet gets a grave. The witnesses sing the going-up they came down to carry, the mantle that fell back to earth and struck the Jordan open, and the first quiet signs of the double portion at work. This is the one man the ground never buried.

*Why this title:* Eliyahu is the exception to every burial in Scripture — where each prophet is laid in the earth, he is lifted alive in a whirlwind by a chariot of fire (2:11); and the fire that consumed the captains in chapter 1 is the same element that carries him up in chapter 2.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Is There No Elohim in Israel** | 2 Kings 1 | The Angels — one of whom, the messenger of Yahuah, sends Eliyahu down (1:3, 15) | A king fallen through his lattice sending to Baal-Zevuv of Ekron to ask if he will live, past the Elohim of Israel; fire falls on two captains and their fifties, and the third kneels and is spared |
| 2 | **We Split the Sky to Take Him** | 2 Kings 2:1–18 | The Angels — the horses and chariots of fire that parted the two and bore Eliyahu up in the whirlwind (2:11) | A hard thing asked — a double portion, granted only if the going-up is seen; a chariot of fire, horses of fire, and the whirlwind; and the mantle falling back to the earth to strike the Jordan into two dry halves |
| 3 | **Salt Healed the Poisoned Spring** | 2 Kings 2:19–22 | The Angels (the new bowl of salt and the spring invoked as image) | Bad water and a barren land; salt cast from a new bowl into the source, and the spring made whole "unto this day" — the thing that ruins ground made the thing that heals it |
| 4 | **They Jeered the Going-Up** | 2 Kings 2:23–25 | The Angels, grave witnesses (the two bears from the wood invoked as image) | A crowd of young ones on the Bethel road turning the prophet's very ascent to scorn — "go up, go up" — and the wood answering; the number forty-two carried gravely, never gloried in |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the doomed king and the fire open the record with the question the whole book keeps asking — is there no Elohim in Israel; the whirlwind is the Unveiling, the going-up the witnesses came down to carry; the healed spring is the still Selah between the blaze and the dark; and the jeered ascent lands the album gravely — even the going-up can be mocked.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 Achazyah and the fire on the captains · 2:1–18 the whirlwind, the mantle, the double portion · 2:19–22 the spring healed at Jericho · 2:23–25 the youths and the bears. Four for four.

# ALBUM II — EVEN THE GRAVE GAVE BACK A SON
### Elisha: Provision and the Raised Son · 2 Kings 3–5 · seven songs

Heaven answers every emptiness the prophet meets — a dry valley, an indebted widow's cupboard, a poisoned pot, too little bread, a leper's ruined skin — but the deepest reversal is a mother's dead boy breathing again on the prophet's bed. The witnesses sing provision drawn from what has nothing to give: water where no rain fell, oil that will not stop, meal that draws the death out of a stew, a muddy river that heals the flesh of a foreign commander. And they sing the one who chased that grace for silver and carried away the leprosy instead.

*Why this title:* across these chapters provision runs all the way to resurrection — the Shunammite's son, dead by noon, sneezes seven times and opens his eyes (4:35); the album's provision-signs orbit that single impossible return.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Water Red as Blood at Dawn** | 2 Kings 3 | The Angels (the minstrel's music invoked — the hand of Yahuah came as it played, 3:15) | No wind and no rain, yet by morning the valley runs full; Moav sees the water red as blood in the dawn and rushes to spoil, and is broken; and a firstborn burned on the wall, carried gravely |
| 2 | **It Poured Until the Jars Ran Out** | 2 Kings 4:1–7 | The Angels (the widow's jar of oil invoked as image) | A widow's last pot of oil poured behind a shut door into every borrowed vessel her sons can find — and it does not cease until there is not one empty jar left to fill; the limit was never the oil |
| 3 | **Seven Sneezes, and the Boy Woke** | 2 Kings 4:8–37 | The Zayin — value seven — with the Angels | A son promised to a barren house and given, then dead on his mother's knees by noon; laid on the prophet's bed; and warmth returning to the flesh until the child sneezes seven times and opens his eyes |
| 4 | **There Was Death in the Pot** | 2 Kings 4:38–41 | The Angels (the handful of meal invoked as image) | A famine stew poisoned by wild gourds, the cry "there is death in the pot," and a little meal thrown in that makes it safe to eat |
| 5 | **Twenty Loaves, a Hundred Men** | 2 Kings 4:42–44 | The Angels (the first-fruits barley loaves invoked as image) | Twenty barley loaves set before a hundred, a servant's "how?"; they eat and leave some over, as the word said |
| 6 | **He Went Down Seven Times, and Was Clean** | 2 Kings 5:1–19 | The Mem — the water — with the Angels (the Jordan invoked as image) | A great commander's chariot at the prophet's door, and an errand of dipping in a muddy river beneath his dignity; seven times down, and flesh come back like a little child's |
| 7 | **What Left the Stranger Clung to Him** | 2 Kings 5:20–27 | The Angels (Gechazi witnessed, never voiced) | A servant chasing a foreigner's silver down the road, lying for two talents and two garments — and the whiteness that left Na'aman's skin settling onto his own |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the blood-red water opens under the threat of thirst and war; the oil and the raised son are the tender, staggering center — the Selah where death itself gives back a boy; the poisoned pot and the loaves keep the provision running; Na'aman's sevenfold Jordan is the Declaration — the mighty made clean by stooping; and the leprosy changing hands lands the album as a warning at the very door of grace.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 3 the war with Moav and the blood-water · 4:1–7 the widow's oil · 4:8–37 the Shunammite's son given and raised · 4:38–41 death in the pot · 4:42–44 the hundred fed · 5:1–19 Na'aman's sevenfold Jordan · 5:20–27 Gechazi's greed. Seven for seven.

# ALBUM III — MORE WITH US THAN WITH THEM
### Elisha: The Unseen Armies and the Kings · 2 Kings 6–8 · seven songs

When the servant sees only the ring of enemy chariots, his eyes are opened to a mountain crowded with horses and chariots of fire — "they that are with us are more than they that are with them." The witnesses sing the unseen host that decides sieges: iron made to float, an enemy army blinded and led to a feast, a besieged city saved by four lepers walking into a camp the enemy fled from a sound of chariots that was not there. And they sing the turning of thrones — a prophet's tears over a murderer-to-be, a king's story told at the very hour its subject walks in, and the lamp of David that will not be put out.

*Why this title:* Elisha's word to his terrified servant (6:16) is the album's spine — "fear not, for they that be with us are more than they that be with them" — and the mountain full of fire (6:17) is heaven's own confirmation of it.

Seven for seven.

# ALBUM IV — THE SWORD, AND THE HIDDEN BOY-KING
### Yehu's Sword and the Rescued Heir · 2 Kings 9–12 · seven songs

A young prophet empties a flask of oil on a captain's head behind a shut door, and a sword is loosed on the house of Achav that will not sheathe until Baal is a latrine and a queen is a skull. But the same purge that cleanses the north cannot cleanse the man who wields it — Yehu breaks Baal and keeps the calves. And in the south the sword nearly ends everything: a queen destroys all the royal seed but one hidden infant, carried into the temple for six years, crowned at seven, grown to mend the very house that saved him.

*Why this title:* the record is a sword and a survivor — Yehu's blade ends a dynasty (9–10), and one hidden boy-king, all but the last of David's line, is kept alive in the house of Yahuah and set on the throne (11).

# ALBUM V — LIFE LEAPT FROM A DEAD MAN'S BONES
### The Brief Reprieve and Elisha's Death · 2 Kings 13–14 · five songs

Both kingdoms get a reprieve none of them earned — a nameless deliverer sent to a thankless north, three victories loosed from three arrows, a proud win over Edom, the old borders restored coast to coast through a wicked king's hand. And in the middle of the borrowed grace, the prophet dies; yet a corpse flung into his grave touches his bones and stands alive. The witnesses sing life that is lent, not owned — a kingdom saved by pity, and a dead man raised by a dead man.

*Why this title:* the album's most staggering image is a corpse thrown into Elisha's tomb reviving the instant it touches his bones (13:21) — and around that miracle everything else is borrowed life: reprieve for a people who kept their idols, victory measured out to a timid hand.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **A Deliverer for a Thankless House** | 2 Kings 13:1–13 | The Angels, who watched mercy given to the undeserving | A northern king's army ground down to ten chariots, a whispered entreaty, and a nameless deliverer sent so the people could dwell in their tents again — grace to a house that kept its groves standing |
| 2 | **Three Strokes, and No More** | 2 Kings 13:14–19, 22–25 | The Angels, who counted the strokes (the arrows invoked as image) | A dying prophet's hands on the king's hands upon the bow, an arrow loosed eastward as "the arrow of deliverance," and a command to strike the ground — three blows and no more, so three victories and no more |
| 3 | **Alive Where They Threw Him Down** | 2 Kings 13:20–21 | The Angels, who saw life leap from the grave (Elisha's bones invoked as image) | A hasty burial interrupted by raiders, a body flung into a prophet's tomb, and the moment it touches the old bones the dead man's feet find the ground — life given by one already gone |
| 4 | **The Thistle Sent to the Cedar** | 2 Kings 14:1–22 | The Angels, who watched pride pick the fight | A king fresh from breaking Edom demanding to face Israel, answered by a fable — a thistle sending to a cedar, trodden down by a passing beast — and a wall of the holy city broken four hundred cubits for the pride that would not stay home |
| 5 | **He Saved Them by an Evil King** | 2 Kings 14:23–29 | The Angels, who saw the pity behind the reprieve | An evil king restoring the old borders coast to coast — not for his righteousness but because heaven saw an affliction very bitter and no one to help; a kingdom saved by the last hand that deserved to save it |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the thankless north opens under undeserved mercy; the arrows meter that mercy out to a hand that stopped too soon; the bones in the grave are the Selah, the wonder at the album's still center; Amatzyah's pride darkens the record; and Yarov'am's borrowed victory lands it — a reprieve lent, not earned, running out toward the fall.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 13:1–13 Yeho'achaz and Yeho'ash of Israel · 13:14–19, 22–25 the arrows and the three victories · 13:20–21 the bones that raised a man · 14:1–22 Amatzyah and the thistle · 14:23–29 Yarov'am II. Five for five.

# ALBUM VI — THEY HIRED THE HAND THAT TOOK THEM
### The Assyrian Shadow and the Fall of the North · 2 Kings 15–17 · five songs

Stroke by stroke both kingdoms buy off Assyria — a leper-king's stable south, a northern throne changing hands by murder every few months, a Davidic king who writes "I am your servant and your son" to the emperor and empties the temple to pay him. And the empire they paid to protect them carries the north away: a three-year siege, a capital taken, ten tribes marched east, and heaven's long reckoning of why. The witnesses sing a kingdom that threw itself away, and the strangers moved into its emptied land who feared Yahuah and kept their idols in the same breath.

*Why this title:* the tribute hired its own conqueror — Menachem's silver to Pul (15:19–20), Achaz's temple-silver to Tiglath-Pileser (16:7–8) — until Assyria, so often paid, carries the north into exile (17).

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **A Leper's House Apart** | 2 Kings 15:1–7, 32–38 | The Angels, grave witnesses of the shut-away king | A long and mostly faithful reign ended in a leper's separate house, his son judging the people at the gate; and the high places still smoking on the hills while Aram and Israel begin to press the borders |
| 2 | **The Throne Changed Hands in Blood** | 2 Kings 15:8–31 | The Angels, counting the short reigns | A throne passing by murder — six months, one month, ten years, two years — captains killing kings and taking the crown still warm; and the first companies of the north already marched east to Asshur |
| 3 | **Your Servant and Your Son** | 2 Kings 16 | The Angels, who read the letter and grieve the son passed through fire | A king of David's house passing his own son through the fire, then writing to Asshur "I am your servant and your son," emptying the temple to pay him, and raising in Yahuah's court a copy of a Damascus altar |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the shadowed south opens with a king shut away and the high places never torn down; the northern throne spirals through blood; Achaz's letter is the Turn, David's heir enslaving himself to the empire; the fall of Shomron and heaven's verdict are the Declaration; and the imported nations land the record as a grim benediction over a kingdom removed from His sight.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 15:1–7, 32–38 Azaryah and Yotam of Yehudah · 15:8–31 the northern kings and the four murders · 16 Achaz and the Assyrian altar · 17:1–23 the fall of Shomron and the indictment · 17:24–41 the imported nations and the lions. Five for five.

# ALBUM VII — THE ANGEL WENT OUT AT NIGHT
### Chizqiyahu and the Angel · 2 Kings 18–20 · six songs

Yehudah's great reformer grinds even the bronze serpent to powder, and then the empire that swallowed the north stands at his wall and a pagan mouth names Yahuah among the gods that could not save their lands. The king carries the blasphemer's letter into the house of Yahuah and spreads it open before Him — and in one night a single messenger goes out and a hundred fourscore and five thousand do not wake. The witnesses sing heaven answering blasphemy with one Angel, the day itself made to walk backward for a sign, and the fatal vanity that shows Babylon everything.

*Why this title:* besieged and blasphemed, Chizqiyahu spreads the enemy's letter before Yahuah, and the Angel of Yahuah goes out that night and by morning the whole Assyrian camp is dead (19:35) — the album is heaven's single messenger against an empire.

# ALBUM VIII — THE SCROLL WAS FOUND TOO LATE
### Menasheh's Blood and the Found Scroll · 2 Kings 21–23:30 · six songs

The worst king Yehudah ever knew fills Yerushalayim with innocent blood from wall to wall until the doom is sealed; a generation later the lost Book of the Law is uncovered in the very house of the Law, and the best king who ever turned tears his robe and then the whole land clean. He keeps the greatest Passover since the days of the judges — and heaven's wrath, kindled for Menasheh's blood, does not turn back. The witnesses sing a reform that could not undo the blood, and the last light of David's house cut down at Megiddo.

*Why this title:* Chilkiyahu finds the scroll in the temple rubble and Yoshiyahu reforms with all his heart, yet "Yahuah turned not from the fierceness of His great wrath" kindled by Menasheh (23:26) — the scroll was found, and it was found too late.

# ALBUM IX — THE HOUSE THAT FIRE TOOK DOWN
### The Fall of Yerushalayim · 2 Kings 23:31–25 · six songs

The last kings fall between two empires — crowns made and unmade by Pharaoh and by Babylon — and the deportations begin: the golden vessels cut up, ten thousand marched east, a young king surrendering with his mother. Then the wall is breached, a king watches his sons die and is blinded, and the house of Yahuah goes up in fire, its bronze pillars broken up and hauled to Babylon. The remnant murders its own governor and flees back down to Egypt, the road out of bondage walked backward — and the book of judgment ends, against all expectation, on a captive king's head lifted at a foreign table.

*Why this title:* the record's catastrophe is the burning of the house of Yahuah and the breaking-up of its pillars (25:9, 13); and at its very end, a lifted head at a foreign king's table — the lamp of David guttering low, but not gone out.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy — always Elohim / Yahuah. Pagan deities are rendered by the text's own names (Baal, Baal-Zevuv, Molech, Nisroch, and the imported gods of 2 Kings 17). The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every 2-Kings-moment song the aches — the prophet taken up without dying, the leper made clean by sevenfold washing, the dead man raised by a prophet's bones, the lost scroll found in the rubble, the lamp of David kept alive in exile, the exile itself as covenant-curse come due — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. David is named because the text names him (8:19; 19:34; 20:6); the far end of his lamp is held as a longing the witnesses cannot yet see. No cross, no Messiah, no fulfillment language enters a song. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour, do not.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Zayin keyed to the sevenfold (the seven sneezes, 4:35), the Mem to the Jordan Na'aman dipped in, the Dalet to the temple door that hid the heir, the Yod (value ten) to the shadow drawn back ten steps, the Bet to the house of Yahuah as it burned. Eliyahu, Elisha, Na'aman, Gechazi, Yehu, Chizqiyahu, Yoshiyahu, and every king are *witnessed*, never made the singer. The rich angelic witnesses the text itself supplies are used as the mandate directs: the chariot and horses of fire that took Eliyahu up (2), the mountain full of horses and chariots of fire around Elisha (6), the Angel of Yahuah who struck the Assyrian camp (19), the messenger who sent Eliyahu down (1). Impersonal creation — the mantle, the jar of oil, the floating axe-head, the Jordan, the wet cloth, the sundial's shadow, the found scroll, the two bears, the lions — is invoked as image, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the fire on two captains' fifties and the third spared, the double portion, the forty-two youths, the seven sneezes, Na'aman's seven dips, the twenty loaves before a hundred, the three strokes, the seventy heads in two heaps, the six years hidden, the four hundred cubits of broken wall, the hundred fourscore and five thousand slain, the fifteen years added and the shadow's ten steps, the ten thousand deported, the thirty-seven years to a lifted head.
- **Grave restraint on the horrors:** the siege-cannibalism of Shomron (6:24–33), the slaughter of the royal seed and the seventy sons, the children passed through fire, Izevel and the dogs, and Tzidkiyahu's blinded sons are all handled with grave, family-friendly restraint — the weight kept, the graphic detail kept off-stage.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
