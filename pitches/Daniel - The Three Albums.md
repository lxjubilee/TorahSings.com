# DANIEL — THE THREE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of Daniyel

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Job - The Seven Albums.md`, and `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Daniyel is two books in one: the **court tales** (1–6) and the **apocalyptic visions** (7–12). Its "units" are the dated episodes and the numbered visions, and the book draws its own seams — the change of king, the dream given and read, the vision received and sealed, the confession prayed, the angel sent to explain. One episode, one song.

**The named angels — Daniyel's distinctive, and this record's second cornerstone.** No book in the Scriptures names the Angels so plainly. **Gavriel** is sent to make the visions plain (8:16; 9:21); **Mikha'el, "one of the chief princes," "the great prince who stands for your people,"** fights the twenty-one-day battle and stands at the end of days (10:13, 10:21; 12:1). The **watchers, the holy ones** come down and decree (4:13, 17); a **fourth man "like a son of the gods"** walks the furnace unbound (3:25); an **angel shuts the lions' mouths** in the den (6:22); a **man clothed in linen** stands above the great river (10:5; 12:6–7). Every one of these is **the Angel of this platform, doing his own scriptural work** — Gavriel explaining, Mikha'el standing, the watchers decreeing, the mouth-shutting angel keeping the den. These are the messengers of the text, sung and witnessed as the text sends them, and they are held distinct from any platform persona; they are never conflated with one.

**The messianic ache — held, never resolved.** Daniyel carries some of the weightiest messianic material in the Scriptures, and all of it is sung as **unresolved longing — never resolved forward, no New-Testament hindsight, no Yeshua-unveiled inside a song.** The **stone cut without hands** that grinds the image to chaff and becomes a mountain filling the earth, "a kingdom that shall never be destroyed" (2:34–35, 44); **one like a son of man coming with the clouds of heaven,** given a dominion that shall not pass away (7:13–14); the **seventy weeks and the Anointed (Mashiach) cut off** (9:24–26), sung as the text speaks it and never carried past the wound; and the **resurrection** — "many that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life" (12:2), and "they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament, and as the stars for ever" (12:3), a line that rings straight back to Job 38:7, the morning stars who are the Picture Letters, the platform's own foundation. Every one is held as the ache the Angels witness and cannot yet name.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels; Gavriel and Mikha'el are the Angels by name. They sing praise to the Creator — **Yahuah, the Most High, the Elohim of heaven** — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the everlasting kingdom and the Anointed longed for but not yet unveiled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or a personified Letter — never a human (not Daniyel, not Nevukhadnetzar, not Chananyah, Misha'el, or Azaryah, not Belshatzar, not Daryavesh). Daniyel's prayers — the great confession, "we have sinned, we have done wickedly" — and his visions are **witnessed and testified to, never voiced as Daniyel;** his own first person is quoted only when clearly framed as quotation. Yahuah's decrees and the heavenly-court scene — the thrones set, the Ancient of Days seated, the fiery stream, the books opened (7:9–10) — are **sung in awe by the witnesses who stood before them.** Impersonal creation and signs the text supplies — the great image of gold, the fiery furnace, the fingers writing MENE MENE TEKEL UPHARSIN on the plaster, the stone cut without hands, the four beasts from the sea, the ram and the goat, the sealed book — are **invoked as image, never voiced.**

> *Three albums. Thirteen songs. No honest zeros — every episode and every vision of Daniyel is sung. Every chapter 1–12 accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE MOST HIGH RULES THE KINGDOM OF MEN
### The Court Tales · Daniel 1–6 · six songs

A people carried into exile, and a line of foreign kings who each learn the same lesson the hard way. Four youths who will not defile themselves at the king's table; a dream of a golden colossus toppled by a stone no hand ever cut; a furnace heated sevenfold with a fourth man walking loose inside it; a proud king driven out to grass until he lifts his eyes; a hand that writes doom across a feast; a den of lions with their mouths held shut till dawn. The witnesses sing the sentence that runs beneath every tale — the watchers' own decree that the Most High rules the kingdom of men, and gives it to whomever He will.

*Why this title:* 4:17 is the verdict the whole album teaches — "this matter is by the decree of the watchers… to the intent that the living may know that the Most High ruleth in the kingdom of men" — the sentence sung by the very Angels the text calls watchers, and confirmed by every king who tries to rule without Him.

|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Ten Days on Pulse and Water** | Daniel 1 | The Angels, watching four youths refuse a king's bounty | Chananyah, Misha'el, Azaryah, and Daniyel set to eat the king's meat and drink his wine, and asking instead for pulse and water ten days — and at the end their faces are fairer and their flesh fuller than all who ate the royal food; whole in a foreign court because they would not be defiled |
| 2 | **A Stone Cut Without Hands** | Daniel 2 | The Angels; the great image and the stone invoked as image | A dream of a colossus — head of gold, breast of silver, belly of bronze, legs of iron, feet of iron and clay — struck by a stone cut out without hands that grinds the whole to chaff the wind carries off, then swells to a mountain filling the earth: a kingdom that shall never be destroyed, held as longing, its coming not yet shown |
| 3 | **Four Walked Where Three Were Bound** | Daniel 3 | The Angels — the fourth in the fire is one of them | A furnace heated seven times hotter, three men bound and cast in, and Nevukhadnetzar rising astonished — "did we not cast three bound into the midst? — lo, I see four loose, walking in the fire, and they have no hurt, and the fourth is like a son of the gods"; the only thing the flame burned was the ropes |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the exile and the table open and set the stake — faithfulness in a foreign court; the dream of the image plants the stone that will outlast every kingdom in the book; the furnace and the felled tree are the deliverance and the humbling at the heart of the court; the writing on the wall ends one empire in a single night; and the lions' den lands the album — a man kept whole under yet another king, the last court tale before the visions break open.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the youths and the king's table · 2 the great image and the stone cut without hands · 3 the furnace and the fourth man · 4 the madness and the tree · 5 the feast and the writing on the wall · 6 the lions' den and the mouth-shutting angel. Six for six.

# ALBUM II — THE ANCIENT OF DAYS TOOK HIS SEAT
### The Thrones, the Beasts, and the Seventy Weeks · Daniel 7–9 · four songs

The visions begin, and the man who read every other king's dream now sees his own and cannot bear them. Four beasts rise from a churning sea, and above them thrones are set: the Ancient of Days takes His seat, a fiery stream issuing, thousand thousands ministering, the books opened — and one like a son of man comes with the clouds to receive a kingdom that shall not pass away. A ram and a goat clash and Gavriel is sent to make the vision plain; a blameless man confesses a whole nation's sin; and Gavriel comes again to number seventy weeks and speak of an Anointed cut off. The witnesses sing the heavenly court in awe, and hold every promise open.

*Why this title:* 7:9 — "the Ancient of Days did sit; his garment was white as snow… his throne was like the fiery flame" — the throne from which every verdict in these visions flows, sung in awe by the Angels who stood before it.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **One Like a Son of Man** | Daniel 7 | The Angels, witnessing the heavenly court and longing for the kingdom they see given | Four beasts out of the churning sea, and thrones set in place: the Ancient of Days seated, white as snow, a fiery stream before Him, thousand thousands ministering, the books opened; and one like a son of man coming with the clouds of heaven to be given dominion and glory and a kingdom that shall not pass away — held as ache, its end not yet shown to the singers |
| 2 | **Gavriel, Make Him Understand** | Daniel 8 | Gavriel — an Angel of this platform — sent to explain, and the Angels who heard the command | A ram with two horns, and a goat with one great horn out of the west that tramples it; the great horn broken into four, and a little horn magnifying itself against the host and the sanctuary — and a voice between the banks of the Ulai: "Gavriel, make this man to understand the vision"; the numbered evenings and mornings sealed for many days |
| 3 | **He Confessed a Sin Not His Own** | Daniel 9:1–19 | The Angels, witnessing a blameless man confess for a whole people | Yerushalayim seventy years desolate, and Daniyel turning his face to Yahuah with fasting, sackcloth, and ashes to pray "we have sinned, we have done wickedly" — the most upright man in Bavel owning a guilt the text never lays on him, pleading not his own righteousness but the great mercies of Elohim |
| 4 | **Seventy Weeks, and the Anointed Cut Off** | Daniel 9:20–27 | Gavriel again — the Angel caused to fly swiftly — and the Angels who hold the promise unresolved | While Daniyel is yet speaking, Gavriel touches him about the evening oblation and numbers seventy weeks decreed upon the city — to finish transgression, to seal up vision, to anoint the most holy; and after threescore and two weeks the Anointed cut off, and none for Him — sung exactly as the text speaks it, carried no further than the wound |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the four beasts and the Ancient of Days open the visions on the throne itself, the Son of Man given a kingdom the witnesses can only long for; the ram and the goat bring Gavriel down to explain the sealed times; the great confession is the album's still, low place, a nation's guilt carried by one clean mouth; and Gavriel's seventy weeks land it, the Anointed cut off held open like a wound the singers cannot yet close.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 7 the four beasts, the Ancient of Days, and the Son of Man · 8 the ram, the goat, and Gavriel · 9:1–19 the great confession-prayer · 9:20–27 the seventy weeks and the Anointed cut off. Four for four. *(Chapter 9 splits at 9:20, where Gavriel arrives to answer the prayer — a penitential prayer and an apocalyptic revelation, two movements, two songs.)*

# ALBUM III — THE MAN CLOTHED IN LINEN
### The Sealed Vision and the Waking Dust · Daniel 10–12 · three songs

One last vision, and the greatest of the Angels are in it by name. A man clothed in linen, his body like beryl and his face like lightning, stands above the great river; a messenger tells how the prince of Persia withstood him twenty-one days until Mikha'el, one of the chief princes, came to help. Then the future of kingdoms is poured out like water — the long war of the king of the north and the king of the south — and, at the end of days, Mikha'el standing up for his people, a time of trouble such as never was, and the dust of the earth waking: some to everlasting life, and the wise shining like the stars for ever. The witnesses hold the resurrection as the longing it is, and marvel that the morning stars are promised company.

*Why this title:* the man clothed in linen (10:5; 12:6–7) frames the whole final vision — first beside the river, and last above its waters, swearing by Him that lives for ever how long till the end of these wonders; the figure the Angels attend and the reader cannot name.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Twenty-One Days He Withstood Me** | Daniel 10 | The messenger-angel in his own voice, and Mikha'el named his helper | By the great river, the Hiddekel, a man clothed in linen, his body like beryl, his face as lightning, his words as the voice of a multitude; and the messenger's own testimony — "the prince of the kingdom of Persia withstood me twenty-one days, until Mikha'el, one of the chief princes, came to help me" — a war in heaven standing behind a delayed answer, and Mikha'el "your prince" holding the field |
| 2 | **The Kings of the North and South** | Daniel 11 | The man clothed in linen, unrolling the times; the kings witnessed | A mighty king whose realm is broken toward the four winds, and then the long grinding war of the king of the north and the king of the south — leagues and betrayals, a raiser of taxes, a vile person who takes the kingdom by flatteries, arms that profane the sanctuary — the future of empires sung before it is fought, down to the one who comes to his end with none to help him |
| 3 | **The Dust Shall Wake, the Wise Shall Shine** | Daniel 12 | The Angels; Mikha'el standing; the man in linen swearing by Him that lives for ever | Mikha'el the great prince stands up for his people, a time of trouble such as never was since there was a nation; and many that sleep in the dust of the earth awake, some to everlasting life — the wise shining as the brightness of the firmament, and as the stars for ever, the morning stars promised company at last; the book shut and sealed till the time of the end, the numbered days, a time, times, and half a time — all of it held as the longing it is |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the man clothed in linen opens the last vision by the river, and the withstood angel's twenty-one days reveal the war standing behind every answer; the kings of the north and south are the long, unrolling middle, the future of empires poured out to a mourning man; and Mikha'el standing at the end of days lands the whole book — the dust waking, the wise shining like the very stars this platform is built upon, and the sealed book left sealed till the time of the end.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 10 the man in linen and the twenty-one-day battle · 11 the kings of the north and the south · 12 Mikha'el stands, the resurrection, and the sealed book. Three for three.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Elohim, and the book's own honest titles as it uses them: **the Most High** (the Aramaic *Illa'a*) and **the Elohim of heaven**, both rendered as the Most High / Elohim. **No English "God" or "Lord" appears anywhere in any title or copy** — Elohim and Yahuah stand in their place. Human names in Hebraic form — Daniyel, Nevukhadnetzar, Belshatzar, Daryavesh, Chananyah, Misha'el, Azaryah, Bavel, Yerushalayim — never anglicized. Yeshua and Ruach HaKodesh (feminine) are reserved to the platform framing. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms); no internal labels; no emoji.
- **The named angels honored:** **Gavriel** (8; 9:21) and **Mikha'el** (10:13, 21; 12:1) are the Angels of this platform doing their own scriptural work — Gavriel making the vision plain, Mikha'el standing in the twenty-one-day battle and at the end of days. The **watchers and the holy ones** (4), the **fourth man "like a son of the gods"** in the furnace (3), the **mouth-shutting angel** in the den (6), and the **man clothed in linen** above the river (10; 12) are the Angels the text supplies — witnessed and sung as the text sends them, and held distinct from any platform persona, never conflated with one.
- **The Son of Man and the messianic ache honored:** the stone cut without hands and the kingdom that shall never be destroyed (2), one like a son of man given an everlasting dominion (7), the seventy weeks and the Anointed cut off (9:24–26), and the resurrection with the wise shining as stars (12) are all sung as unresolved longing — no New-Testament hindsight, no cross, no fulfillment language, no Yeshua-unveiled. The **fourth man "like a son of the gods"** (3:25) is sung as one of the Angels come down into the fire, exactly as Nevukhadnetzar names it, and is not resolved forward. The framing names Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour, never do.
- **The morning-stars resonance:** the wise who "shine… as the stars for ever" (12:3) are sung with the Job 38:7 resonance the whole platform rests on — the morning stars who are the Picture Letters — the book's own promise that the risen wise will join the singers who sang at the foundations.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a personified Letter. Daniyel, the three companions, Nevukhadnetzar, Belshatzar, and Daryavesh are *witnessed*, never made the singer; Daniyel's confession is testified to and quoted only as clearly framed quotation, never taken as his own first person. Yahuah's decrees and the heavenly-court scene — the thrones set, the Ancient of Days seated, the fiery stream, the books opened — are His and the court's, sung in awe by the witnesses who stood before them. The great image, the fiery furnace, the fingers and the writing on the wall, the stone cut without hands, the four beasts, the ram and the goat, and the sealed book are invoked as image, never voiced.
- **Text-numbers as content; the Letters never a count:** the three companions, the furnace heated sevenfold, the seventy years and the seventy weeks, the twenty-one days of the withstood angel, the 1,290 and 1,335 days, and the "time, times, and half a time" are sung as the text's own numbers; **MENE, MENE, TEKEL, UPHARSIN** are rendered as the text's own words — numbered, weighed, divided — never as a gematria mechanism. The Picture Letters are named as personae, never totaled, never reduced to a count.
- **Restraint:** the furnace and the lions' den are deliverances, sung reverent and family-friendly; the judgments — the felled tree, the hand at the feast, the beasts from the sea, the wars of the kings — are handled with grave restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, never rendered for shock.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
