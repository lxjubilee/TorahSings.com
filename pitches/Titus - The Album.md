**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced the four Gospels (`pitches/John - The Six Albums.md` and its companions, under `pitches/_New Testament - The Gospels.md`) and the letters already brought in (`pitches/Philippians - The Album.md`, `pitches/Colossians - The Album.md`). Titus is Book 56 — one of Shaul's three pastoral letters, written to his own son in the faith left behind to set the assemblies of Crete in order.
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Titus is a **letter** — not narrative, not oracle, not law: Shaul's pastoral charge to Titus, whom he left in Crete "to set in order the things that are wanting, and ordain elders in every city" (1:5). Its "units" are the letter's own movements — the greeting and the hope promised before the world began (1:1–4), the appointing of elders and the sound doctrine that convinces the deceivers (1:5–16), the household order and the adorning of the doctrine (2:1–10), the grace that bringeth salvation and the ache of His appearing (2:11–15), and the kindness that appeared in the washing of regeneration, closing on the final charge and the benediction (3:1–15). The seams are Shaul's own: the greeting-and-hope, the "for this cause left I thee in Crete" of the elder-charge, the "but speak thou the things which become sound doctrine" of chapter 2, the "for the grace of Elohim... hath appeared" hinge, and the "put them in mind" of the closing chapter. Three chapters; the count follows the material — one album, five songs.

**THE NEW-TESTAMENT SHIFT — the Letters fall silent; only the Angels sing.** Through all thirty-nine books of the Old Covenant the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* — the morning stars (the Picture Letters) and the sons of Elohim (the Angels), aching toward a promised One they could not yet name. **At the incarnation that changed.** The Word (the Davar) by whom every letter was made is now made flesh in Yeshua; the aleph-bet lives in Him and has gone quiet as a separate voice. Therefore in this letter, as in the Gospels and the letters already sung, **the only singers are the Angels** — never a Picture-Letter persona. This letter names the reason in its own way: the grace and the kindness of Elohim, once promised, have **appeared** — the Word is here, and the Letters that carried Him now rest in Him. The Letters are the marvel the Angels testify *to,* not a voice of their own.

**Epistle, not narrative — the accomplished work proclaimed freely; one ache still held.** In the Gospels the forward-lock held the cross unspoken until its place. A letter has no biography of Yeshua to walk in order, and this one is written *after* the resurrection — so the finished work is proclaimed **freely and fully:** "the grace of Elohim that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men" (2:11); "who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works" (2:14); "the kindness and love of Elohim our Saviour toward man appeared... by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Ruach Kodesh... that being justified by his grace, we should be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life" (3:5–7). All of it accomplished, all of it sung openly. **The one thing still held as aching, unresolved longing is His APPEARING** — "looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great Elohim and our Saviour Yeshua the Mashiach" (2:13). This letter has two appearings: the one that **has** come — grace and kindness in the flesh — and the one still **awaited** — the glorious appearing. That second appearing is the album's aching hinge, held exactly as the Old-Covenant catalog held its ache, alongside the hope of eternal life "promised before the world began" (1:2) and awaited still (3:7). The Angels sing the truths Shaul unveils, quoting him, and bear witness to Yeshua whom the letter proclaims.

**Yeshua witnessed, never voiced.** The singer is always the Angels; **Yeshua is testified to in awe — never a first-person singer.** "Who gave himself for us," "our great Elohim and Saviour," "the washing of regeneration... through Yeshua the Mashiach our Saviour" are the Angels reporting and marveling at what Shaul wrote of Him — never Yeshua's own voice, exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old Covenant. And Shaul never sings, nor does Titus: their charge — "set in order the things that are wanting," "speak thou the things which become sound doctrine," "these things speak, and exhort, and rebuke with all authority" — is **witnessed and freely quoted** by the Angels, framed from their heavenly vantage as testimony to Yeshua, never voiced as the men's own first person. **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song lands on the person of Yeshua the Saviour, whose grace appeared, whose self-gift redeemed, and whose glorious appearing is awaited.

**Old-Covenant cross-reference — free, and backward only.** The Angels, who witnessed all thirty-nine books, tie the letter *back* into the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings — but never forward: "Elohim, that cannot lie" (1:2) tied to "Elohim is not a man, that he should lie" (Bamidbar 23:19); "a peculiar people" (2:14) tied to the peculiar treasure of Shemot 19:5 and the peculiar people of Devarim 14:2; "redeem us from all iniquity" (2:14) tied to "he shall redeem Israel from all his iniquities" (Tehillim 130:8); "the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Ruach Kodesh" (3:5) tied to the clean water sprinkled and the new heart and new spirit of Yechezkel 36:25–27; "that the word of Elohim be not blasphemed" (2:5) tied to the Name profaned among the nations and then sanctified (Yechezkel 36:20–23; Yeshayahu 52:5); "heirs according to the hope of eternal life" (3:7) tied to the inheritance and the portion of Yahuah's people (Bamidbar 18:20; Tehillim 16:5). No forward, post-letter reference is used.

> *One album. Five songs. No honest zeros — every movement of Shaul's letter is sung. All three chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes below it: this short letter holds five songs, and five is what it gets — neither padded up toward a quota nor cut below its seams. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — THE BLESSED HOPE AND THE GLORIOUS APPEARING
### Sound Doctrine, Saving Grace, and the Appearing Still Awaited · Crete 1–3 · five songs

Shaul writes to his own son in the faith, left behind on a hard island to build order where there was little. The letter is short, and its spine is grace that *appeared.* It opens on a hope older than the world — eternal life promised before time began by an Elohim who cannot lie — then turns to the work at hand: faithful elders set in every city to hold fast the sound word against the deceivers of Crete, whose own prophet witnessed against them; a whole household — the older and the younger, and the servants — taught to make the doctrine beautiful in ordinary life. From that quiet ground the letter lifts, without warning, into its summit: the grace of Elohim that bringeth salvation *hath appeared,* teaching a people to look for the one appearing still to come — that blessed hope, the glorious appearing of the great Elohim and our Saviour — Yeshua, who gave Himself to redeem and to purify a peculiar people zealous of good works. And it closes where grace began: the kindness and love of our Saviour appeared, not by works we have done but by His mercy, in the washing of regeneration and the renewing of the Ruach Kodesh, that we should be made heirs of the hope of eternal life. The Angels sing the whole arc — quoting Shaul, testifying to Yeshua whom the letter proclaims — the grace that has come, and the glory that is coming.

*Why this title:* 2:13 is the letter's aching hinge and the one thing it holds unresolved — "looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great Elohim and our Saviour Yeshua the Mashiach." Between the grace that *has* appeared and the glory that *will,* the whole letter leans forward; the title is that forward lean, the album's single unresolved longing.

## Compliance Notes

- **The New-Testament shift honored — the Letters fall silent, only the Angels sing:** the two voices of Job 38:7 sang *together* through all thirty-nine Old-Covenant books; in this letter the morning stars (the Picture Letters) have gone quiet and now live in the Word made flesh, so **every song is sung by the Angels alone** — never a Picture-Letter persona. The letter's own word for it is *appeared:* the grace and kindness of Elohim, once promised, have appeared in the flesh, and the Letters that carried Him rest in Him. No gematria mechanism and no letter-count is ever stated or sung.
- **The humans never sing — the Angels are the only voice:** not one song is voiced by a human. Every "Who sings" cell is the Angels. Shaul does not sing, nor does Titus; their charge — "set in order the things that are wanting," "speak thou the things which become sound doctrine," "these things speak, and exhort, and rebuke with all authority" — is **witnessed and freely quoted** by the Angels, always framed from their heavenly vantage as testimony to Yeshua. A song built on Shaul's words means the Angels sing *about* what he wrote; it never means the man sings. The men of Crete, the deceivers, the household, and the co-workers (Artemas, Tychikos, Zenas, Apollos) are likewise witnessed, never voiced.
- **Christocentric — about "the who":** every song is ultimately about the person of Yeshua the Saviour — the hope promised in Him before the world began, the sound word that is His, the doctrine of "Elohim our Saviour" the household adorns, the grace that appeared and the self-gift that redeemed, and the mercy poured out "through Yeshua the Mashiach our Saviour." The hinge song is the album's summit; even the household exhortations are sung as the outworking of the grace that *appeared.*
- **Yeshua witnessed, never first-person-voiced:** "who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us" (2:14), "the great Elohim and our Saviour Yeshua the Mashiach" (2:13), and "which he shed on us abundantly through Yeshua the Mashiach our Saviour" (3:6) are all sung *in awe by the Angels who testify to what He is and did,* never given a first-person singer — exactly as Yahuah's oracles were handled across the Old-Covenant catalog. The high witness of 2:13 — our great Elohim and Saviour — is testified, not voiced.
- **The epistle adaptation — the finished work proclaimed freely, one ache held:** written after the resurrection, the letter lets the accomplished work of Yeshua be proclaimed fully — the grace that *hath appeared* (2:11), the self-gift that redeemed and purified a people (2:14), the washing of regeneration already poured out (3:5–6), the justification by grace already granted (3:7). The forward-lock is *relocated,* not removed. The single thing held as aching, unresolved longing is **His appearing** — "looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great Elohim and our Saviour Yeshua the Mashiach" (2:13) — carried alongside the "hope of eternal life" promised before the world began (1:2) and awaited still (3:7). This letter's two appearings — the one come, the one coming — are the album's whole tension.
- **Old-Covenant cross-reference present and backward-only:** every tie reaches *back* — "Elohim is not a man, that he should lie" (Bamidbar 23:19) under "Elohim, that cannot lie"; the peculiar treasure of Shemot 19:5 and the peculiar people of Devarim 14:2 under "a peculiar people"; "he shall redeem Israel from all his iniquities" (Tehillim 130:8) under "redeem us from all iniquity"; the clean water and new heart and new spirit of Yechezkel 36:25–27 under "the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Ruach Kodesh"; the Name profaned then sanctified (Yechezkel 36:20–23; Yeshayahu 52:5) under "that the word of Elohim be not blasphemed"; the false shepherds of Yechezkel 34 and Yirmeyahu 23 behind the deceivers of Crete; the inheritance and portion of Bamidbar 18:20 and Tehillim 16:5 under "heirs according to the hope of eternal life." No forward, post-letter reference is used.
- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — **Yeshua** (never "Jesus"), the **Mashiach** (never "Christ"; no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms — always "the Mashiach," never "the HaMashiach"), **Ben-Elohim** / the Son, **Yahuah**, **Elohim** (rendered for every "God" — "the great Elohim and our Saviour," "Elohim our Saviour," "that the word of Elohim be not blasphemed"), **Ruach HaKodesh** / **the Ruach Kodesh** (feminine — never stacked with an English "the"; rendered "the Ruach Kodesh" at 3:5 where the received text reads "the Holy Ghost"), **Abba** / the Father, and **the Adon / Adonai** rendered for every "Lord." "Saviour" is retained where the text reads it (Elohim our Saviour; Yeshua the Mashiach our Saviour). Hebraic and Grecized names and places throughout — Shaul, Titus, Crete, Artemas, Tychikos, Zenas, Apollos, Nikopolis; the Old-Covenant ties keyed to Bamidbar, Shemot, Devarim, Yechezkel, Yeshayahu, Yirmeyahu, and the Tehillim. **No English "God," "Lord," "Jesus," or "Christ" appears anywhere in any title or copy;** the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note; no internal labels; no emoji.
- **Restraint — triumph is triumph:** the letter's sharp edges — the deceivers whose mouths must be stopped, Crete's own prophet's witness against his people, the heretic rejected — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the sound doctrine and the Angels' awe carrying it, never made a spectacle. The grace that appeared, the washing of regeneration, and the blessed hope of His glorious appearing are the record's triumph, sung plainly as triumph.
