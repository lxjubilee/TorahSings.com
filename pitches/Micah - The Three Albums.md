# MICAH — THE THREE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of Mikhah of Moresheth

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Isaiah - The Ten Albums.md`, and `pitches/Hosea - The Two Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Mikhah is seven chapters, one of the Twelve, and a contemporary of Yeshayahu (1:1 — the days of Yotham, Achaz, and Chizqiyahu). It is a book of judgment and hope in alternating waves, and its units sit on the text's own seams: above all the three **"hear ye" oracle-openings** — *shim'u* — that begin chapters 1, 3, and 6 ("Hear, all ye people"; "Hear, O heads of Yaakov"; "Hear ye now what Yahuah saith"); then the naming-and-wordplay dirge, the woe-cries ("woe to them that devise iniquity"; "woe is me"), the covenant lawsuit-opening (*rib* — "Yahuah hath a controversy"), and the shifts between doom and deliverance that are the book's signature. **The three "hear ye" summons are the book's own outline, and they draw three oracles, each running from judgment toward promise — so this pitch ships three albums, at 1–2, 3–5, and 6–7, a defended alternative to a two-album cut that honors the book's own structural seams and keeps each record within the shipped album-size norm.**

**The three jewels, and the restraint.** Mikhah carries three passages of unusual weight, and each is given its own song and its full room. **(1) The mountain of Yahuah's house and the swords into plowshares** (4:1–5), parallel to Yeshayahu 2 — the nations flowing uphill to be taught, "they shall beat their swords into plowshares… and none shall make them afraid" under vine and fig — **held as unresolved longing.** **(2) Beit-Lechem Ephratah** (5:2) — "though thou be little among the thousands of Yehudah, yet out of thee shall he come forth… that is to be ruler in Israel; whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting" — one of the heaviest messianic aches in the Scriptures, **held UNRESOLVED, sung as the mystery the Angels carry toward the One promised and never named forward.** **(3) "What doth Yahuah require of thee"** (6:8) — "to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy Elohim" — the book's ethical summit; and the closing **mi-chamokha** (7:18–19), "who is a El like unto thee, that pardoneth iniquity… he will subdue our iniquities; and thou wilt cast all their sins into the depths of the sea" — a play on the prophet's own name (Mikhah = "who is like Yah"), echoing the Song of the Sea's *mi-chamokha* (Exodus 15:11). The judgment oracles — the melting mountains, the cannibal metaphor for the devouring leaders, the town-by-town dirge — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, never rendered for shock.

**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator — **Yahuah** — and, from *within* each moment of the book, toward the ruler and the deliverance longed for but not yet unveiled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or a personified Letter — never a human (not Mikhah, not the heads, not the false prophets). The prophet's laments ("therefore I will wail and howl," 1:8) and his confession ("I am full of power by the Ruach of Yahuah," 3:8) are **witnessed and testified to** — the Angels sing *about* them, never voice the prophet. Yahuah's own first-person oracles and the courtroom "controversy" — "O my people, what have I done unto thee?" (6:3); "I will surely assemble" (2:12); "I will cut off thy horses" (5:10) — are **His own words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said** (the Deuteronomy / Job / Isaiah model). Impersonal creation the text supplies (the mountains molten like wax, the plowshares and pruninghooks, the vine and fig tree under which none is afraid, the tower of the flock, the ruler from Beit-Lechem, the dew and the lion, the sins cast into the depths of the sea) is **invoked as image, never voiced.**

**Narrative-time — the messianic ache held, never resolved forward.** No New-Testament hindsight anywhere. The book's two great unresolved aches are carried within their own hour: **the mountain of the House and the swords to plowshares** (4:1–5), and above all **the ruler from Beit-Lechem Ephratah whose goings forth are from everlasting** (5:2) with the shepherd who feeds the flock (5:4) — the sharpest messianic ache in the book, sung as the mystery the witnesses carry toward the One promised, never named forward. No Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment language inside a song. The **mi-chamokha** (7:18–19) is the book's own landing and a present-tense pardon, not a forward resolution — so it is **sung in full**: the pardon and the sins drowned in the sea, "who is a El like unto thee."

> *Three albums. Twenty-one songs. No honest zeros — every oracle of Mikhah is sung. All seven chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its oracles hold, one per unit and never a split to pad. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — WITNESS FROM HIS HOLY TEMPLE
### The First Oracle · "Hear, All Ye People" · Mikhah 1–2 · five songs

The book opens as a summons — *hear, all ye people; earth, give ear* — and Yahuah takes the stand against His own from His holy temple. He comes forth treading the high places, and the everlasting hills run down like wax beneath His feet; the wound of Shomron festers incurable and climbs to the very gate of Yerushalayim. The Angels witness the whole deposition: a dirge chanted town by town in bitter wordplay, the schemers who covet fields from their beds, the drunkards' prophet who promises wine — and, at the last, the breaker who goes up before the gathered remnant, the first flash of hope in the dark.

*Why this title:* 1:2 — the book opens by calling Yahuah as witness against the peoples "from his holy temple," the celestial courtroom the Angels attend; the whole first oracle is a deposition heard from heaven's side.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Hills Melted Under His Feet** | Mikhah 1:2–9 | The Angels, who felt the earth soften as He came down | Yahuah forth from His place, treading the high places of the earth; the mountains molten like wax, the valleys cleft like water poured down a steep — all for the transgression of Yaakov; and the wound of Shomron incurable, come up to the gate of His people, even to Yerushalayim (the prophet's barefoot wail, 1:8, witnessed, never voiced) |
| 2 | **Roll in the Dust, House of Dust** | Mikhah 1:10–16 | The Angels, keeping the roll of the mourning towns | The dirge sung town by town in bitter wordplay — tell it not in Gath; in Beit-le-Aphrah, the house of dust, roll thyself in the dust; Shaphir passing away in shame, Zaanan not daring to come forth, Lakhish binding the chariot, Achziv proving a lie — ending "make thee bald… for thy delicate children; they are gone into captivity" |

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1:2–9 the coming down, the wax mountains, the incurable wound · 1:10–16 the dirge of the towns · 2:1–5 the coveters on their beds · 2:6–11 the false prophets and the prophet of wine · 2:12–13 the remnant gathered and the breaker. Five for five. *(1:1 is the superscription; its date and provenance are carried into song 1, not set alone.)*

# ALBUM II — OUT OF THEE SHALL THE RULER COME
### The Second Oracle · "Hear, O Heads of Yaakov" · Mikhah 3–5 · nine songs

A second summons falls, this time on the leaders: *hear, O heads of Yaakov, is it not for you to know judgment?* The Angels witness the shepherds who devoured their own flock and the seers who sold peace by the mouthful, until the temple-mount itself is sentenced to be plowed as a field. And then the great reversal the same oracle carries: that very mountain established above the hills, swords hammered into plowshares, every man unafraid under his vine — and out of little Beit-Lechem Ephratah a ruler whose goings forth are from everlasting, held as the sharpest ache in the book, carried toward the One promised and never named forward.

*Why this title:* 5:2 — the album climbs from the mountain of the House sentenced to be plowed (3:12), through the mountain of the House established in the top of the mountains (4:1), to Beit-Lechem, "out of thee shall he come forth… whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting"; the ruler the whole oracle reaches toward, held unresolved.

|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **The Heads Who Hated the Good** | Mikhah 3:1–4 | The Angels, who saw the shepherds turn on the flock | The heads of Yaakov who ought to know judgment and instead hate the good and love the evil — who devour their own people as flesh into a pot (sung with grave restraint, the metaphor witnessed, not rendered); and when the ruin comes they cry to Yahuah, and He will not hear, because they made their doings evil |
| 2 | **Peace for Those Who Fed Them** | Mikhah 3:5–8 | The Angels, who watched the seers go blind | The prophets who make the people err — who bite with their teeth and cry "Peace," and sanctify war against any who fill not their mouths; the sun going down over the seers, no vision, no answer from Elohim, their faces covered for shame — and, witnessed against them, the true word "full of power by the Ruach of Yahuah" that stood alone (3:8) |

Nine for nine — the density the shipped Yeshayahu record set, one recognizable oracle-image per unit, the three sub-oracles of chapter 3 and the several turns of chapters 4–5 each given their own movement.

# ALBUM III — WHO IS A EL LIKE THEE
### The Third Oracle · "Hear Ye What Yahuah Saith" · Mikhah 6–7 · seven songs

The last summons calls the mountains themselves as jury: *hear ye what Yahuah saith.* He pleads His own case — *O my people, what have I done unto thee?* — and the answer to all the ritual bargaining, the rams and the rivers of oil and the offered firstborn, is three plain things: to do justly, to love mercy, to walk humbly. The Angels witness the crooked scales that curse the bread, the land where not one upright man is left, the daughter who falls in the dark and swears she will rise — and the book's landing, the pardon that drowns iniquity in the sea: *who is a El like unto Thee*, a play on the prophet's own name, sung in full.

*Why this title:* 7:18 — the book lands on the *mi-chamokha*, "who is a El like unto thee, that pardoneth iniquity," a play on the prophet's name (Mikhah, "who is like Yah") and an echo of the Song of the Sea (Exodus 15:11); the album climbs the controversy and the requirement to this pardon and the sins cast into the depths of the sea.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **What Have I Done to You?** | Mikhah 6:1–5 | The Angels, singing in awe of Yahuah's own plea | "Arise, contend thou before the mountains"; Yahuah's controversy with His people, the hills called to hear — "O my people, what have I done unto thee? and wherein have I wearied thee? testify against me"; I brought thee up out of Mitzrayim, sent Mosheh, Aharon, and Miryam; remember Balak and Bilaam, from Shittim unto Gilgal — that ye may know the righteousness of Yahuah |
| 2 | **Do Justly, Love Mercy, Walk Humbly** | Mikhah 6:6–8 | The Angels, who heard the whole weight of worship set on three plain things | "Wherewith shall I come before Yahuah, and bow myself before the high Elohim? with burnt offerings, with thousands of rams, with ten thousands of rivers of oil? shall I give my firstborn for my transgression?" — no; "he hath shewed thee, O man, what is good: to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy Elohim" |
| 3 | **Eat, and Never Be Filled** | Mikhah 6:9–16 | The Angels, who watched the crooked scales curse the bread | The city and the rod, the scant measure that is abominable, the wicked balances and the bag of deceitful weights, the rich men full of violence — "thou shalt eat, but not be satisfied"; thou shalt sow but not reap, tread the olives but have no oil; the statutes of Omri and the works of the house of Achav kept, a desolation for it |
| 4 | **Not One Upright Man Left** | Mikhah 7:1–6 | The Angels, who searched the land for one upright man | "Woe is me!… the good man is perished out of the earth, and there is none upright among men; they hunt every man his brother with a net"; the best of them a brier; "trust ye not in a friend, put ye not confidence in a guide" — for a man's enemies are the men of his own house (the prophet's lament witnessed, never voiced) |
| 5 | **Fallen, She Will Rise** | Mikhah 7:7–13 | The Angels, who watched the daughter of Tzion rise from the dark | Her turn from despair — "I will look unto Yahuah; I will wait for the Elohim of my salvation"; "rejoice not against me, O mine enemy: when I fall, I shall arise; when I sit in darkness, Yahuah shall be a light unto me" (her words, witnessed); she bears His indignation because she sinned, until He plead her cause; the day her walls are to be built |

| 7 | **Cast Into the Depths of the Sea** | Mikhah 7:18–20 | The Angels, singing the pardon in awe — who is a El like Yahuah | The book's landing, sung in full — "who is a El like unto thee, that pardoneth iniquity and passeth by the transgression of the remnant of his heritage? he retaineth not his anger for ever, because he delighteth in mercy; he will subdue our iniquities; and thou wilt cast all their sins into the depths of the sea" — the pardon that drowns sin like the host in the sea (the Song of the Sea's own *mi-chamokha*, Exodus 15:11), the play on the prophet's name; "thou wilt perform the truth to Yaakov, the mercy to Avraham" |

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 6:1–5 the controversy, "what have I done unto thee" · 6:6–8 "do justly, love mercy, walk humbly" · 6:9–16 the scant measure and the curse on the bread · 7:1–6 no upright man, trust no friend · 7:7–13 "I will look… when I fall, I shall arise," the walls to be built · 7:14–17 "feed thy flock with thy rod," the nations ashamed · 7:18–20 the *mi-chamokha* and the sins cast into the sea. Seven for seven.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy — Elohim, El, and Yahuah stand in their place ("who is a El like unto thee," 7:18; "the high Elohim," 6:6). Hebraic forms are used honestly throughout — Mikhah, Beit-Lechem Ephratah, Yehudah, Shomron, Tzion, Yerushalayim, Yaakov, Botzrah, Bavel, Mitzrayim, Bashan, Gilad, Mosheh, Aharon, Miryam, Balak, Bilaam, Shittim, Gilgal, Omri, Achav. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled English article on *Ha-* forms — Ruach HaKodesh named only in this framing). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **The three jewels honored:** the mountain of the House and the swords into plowshares (4:1–5, "Under the Vine, None Afraid") and Beit-Lechem Ephratah with the ruler from everlasting (5:1–6, "Little Among the Thousands") are each given their own song and sung as **unresolved longing** — the mountain raised and the weapons undone that the Angels long to see, and the ruler-mystery the Angels carry toward the One promised, never named forward. "What doth Yahuah require" (6:6–8, "Do Justly, Love Mercy, Walk Humbly") is the ethical summit, its own song; and the closing **mi-chamokha** (7:18–20, "Cast Into the Depths of the Sea") is the book's landing — sung in full, the pardon and the sins drowned in the sea, the play on the prophet's name, echoing the Song of the Sea.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every song the messianic aches are carried within their own hour, never resolved forward — the mountain of the House and the plowshares (4:1–5), and above all the ruler from Beit-Lechem whose goings forth are from everlasting and the shepherd who feeds the flock (5:1–6), are sung as mystery and longing the witnesses have just overheard. No Yeshua, no cross, no fulfillment language inside a song. The framing names and adores Yeshua; the songs, standing inside Mikhah's own hour, do not. The *mi-chamokha* (7:18–19) is a present-tense pardon, the book's own landing, and is therefore sung in full.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter. Mikhah, the heads of Yaakov, the false prophets, and the daughter of Tzion are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the prophet's wail ("I will wail and howl," 1:8) and his confession ("I am full of power by the Ruach of Yahuah," 3:8) are testified to, never voiced as his own first person. Yahuah's first-person oracles — the controversy "what have I done unto thee?" (6:3), "I will surely assemble" (2:12), "I will cut off thy horses" (5:10) — are His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said (the Deuteronomy / Job / Isaiah model). The wax mountains, the plowshares and pruninghooks, the vine and fig tree, the tower of the flock, the ruler from Beit-Lechem, the dew and the lion, and the sins cast into the depths of the sea are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Restraint:** the judgment oracles — the melting mountains, the devouring metaphor for the corrupt heads (3:1–4), the town-dirge, the scant measure — are handled with grave, family-friendly restraint, witnessed from heaven's side, the harshest metaphors named but never rendered for shock.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the "seven shepherds and eight principal men" raised against the Assyrian (5:5), the ten thousands of rivers of oil (6:7).
