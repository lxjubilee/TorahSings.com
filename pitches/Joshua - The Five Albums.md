# JOSHUA — THE FIVE ALBUMS
## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Inheritance

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Leviticus - The Seven Albums.md`, `pitches/Numbers - The Eight Albums.md`, and `pitches/Deuteronomy - The Nine Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the campaign and battle narratives, the tribal-allotment formulas ("this was the lot of the tribe of…"), the speech openings ("and Yahuah spoke unto Yehoshua"), and the "unto this day" memorial markers Joshua supplies in place of the Genesis *toledot*. The dense allotment lists (roughly 13–21) are gathered into coherent distribution units per §3.3 rather than split parcel by parcel.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Son promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside its own place in Scripture, with no New-Testament hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Joshua-moment song the scarlet cord on the doomed wall, the memorial stones set "for when your children ask," the Commander who is for neither side, the manslayer set free at the high priest's death, the rest that is given yet not fully entered are carried as mystery and ache, never named as accomplished fulfillment. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Yehoshua, not Rachav, not Kalev, not Achan) or anything derived from one. Joshua supplies its own angelic witnesses above all others — the **Commander of the army of Yahuah** with the drawn sword (5:13–15), and the host He commands; the impersonal creation the text supplies (the heaped-up Jordan, the twelve memorial stones, the scarlet cord, the fallen walls, the sun and moon that stood still, the great stone under the oak at Shechem, the bones of Yoseph) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *Five albums. Twenty-six songs. No honest zeros — every narrative unit of Joshua is sung. Every chapter accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap: each album carries as many songs as its stories hold. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — AT FLOOD STAGE THE RIVER WENT DRY
### The Crossing In · Joshua 1–5 · six songs

The book opens on a death — Mosheh is gone — and a command repeated until it stops sounding like encouragement and starts sounding like a man's fear being answered out loud. Before a single wall falls, a nation crosses a river at flood stage on dry ground, piles twelve stones so its children will one day ask what they mean, and is halted at the border to be circumcised, to keep Passover, and to watch the free bread of forty years end the morning it eats the produce of the land. And at the very threshold of the war, the Commander of Yahuah's own army draws His sword — and refuses to take Israel's side. The witnesses sing the crossing-in: the consecration that comes before the conquest, the ground made holy before it is ever taken.

*Why this title:* 3:15 notes that the Jordan was overflowing all its banks — it was harvest, the river at full flood — and on that very flood the people crossed on dry ground; at its fullest the river went dry to let them through.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Moses My Servant Is Dead** | Joshua 1 | The Angels, who saw Mosheh buried in the valley and now watch the charge pass on | The book's first spoken words (1:2) — a death, and a "now therefore arise, go over this Jordan"; the command to be strong and courageous said again and again over a man whose knees the Angels can see shaking, sealed with "I will not fail thee" |
| 2 | **The Scarlet Cord in the Doomed Wall** | Joshua 2 | The Angels, who heard the least likely mouth confess Yahuah | A harlot of a condemned city says "Yahuah your Elohim, He is Elohim in heaven above and on earth beneath" (2:11); flax on the roof, spies let down by a rope, a scarlet cord bound in the window of a house built into a wall that will fall — a mark on a doomed house, carried as ache, not named |
| 3 | **The Priests Stood Where the River Was** | Joshua 3 | The Mem — the water, heaped up and standing — with the Angels | The moment the ark-bearers' feet touch the brim, the waters far upstream rise in a heap and the flood is cut off; the priests stand firm on dry ground in the midst of Jordan while a whole nation passes over between the walls of the halted river |
| 4 | **Stones for When the Children Ask** | Joshua 4 | The Angels; the twelve stones invoked as image | Twelve stones lifted from the dry riverbed and twelve set in the midst of it, piled at Gilgal against forgetting — "when your children ask in time to come, what mean these stones?" — a memorial built so a miracle survives its own generation |
| 5 | **The Reproach Rolled Off at Gilgal** | Joshua 5:1–12 | The Gimel — *galal*, to roll; the foot that has finished the journey in — with the Angels | Flint knives at Gibeath-haaraloth, an uncircumcised wilderness generation given the covenant flesh; "this day have I rolled away the reproach of Egypt," so the place is called Gilgal; Passover kept in the land, and the next morning the manna ceases — heaven's bread stops the day the land's bread begins |
| 6 | **Neither For You, Nor For Them** | Joshua 5:13–15 | The Angels — the host of the army of Yahuah, whose Commander drew His sword | A man with a drawn sword before Yericho; "art thou for us, or for our adversaries?" and the answer is *No* — "as Commander of the army of Yahuah am I now come"; Yehoshua falls on his face, and the word is "loose thy sandal, the place is holy" — a captain who belongs to His own side alone |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the death of Mosheh and the trembling commission open the album in hush; the scarlet cord and the crossing carry the approach and the first wonder; the twelve stones are the still point, the memorial that turns to the children; the consecration at Gilgal weans the nation off the wilderness and readies it; and the Commander with the drawn sword lands the album on the threshold of the war — the sword unsheathed, and Israel told first to take off its shoes.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1 the commission · 2 Rachav and the scarlet cord · 3 the Jordan heaped up · 4 the twelve stones · 5:1–12 circumcision, Passover, the manna ceasing · 5:13–15 the Commander of Yahuah's army. Six for six.

# ALBUM II — THE WALLS OBEYED, ONE MAN DID NOT
### The Central Campaign · Joshua 6–9 · five songs

The land is not taken by arms but by obedience to a word — and it is lost the same way. Yericho falls without a blow, marched down in six days of silence and one shout; then thirty-six men die at little Ai because a single man buried devoted silver under his tent, and a whole nation learns that one hidden sin can empty its ranks. Ai is taken on the second attempt by a staged retreat, the very rout that undid them turned into a trap; the whole law is read aloud on the mountain to every soul, women and little ones and strangers alike; and a ragged embassy in mouldy shoes tricks Israel into an oath it will keep because it was sworn in the Name. The witnesses sing the first battles in the heart of the land, where victory and defeat both hang on the covenant, not the sword.

*Why this title:* chapters 6 and 7 set the album's whole argument side by side — a city's walls come down in perfect obedience, and one man's disobedience brings an army down at the very next city.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **They Marched the Wall Down** | Joshua 6 | The Zayin — value seven — tolling the sevens; the Angels | Seven priests, seven trumpets, six days of ordered silence and a seventh day of seven circuits, then one shout and the wall falls flat under itself; no ram, no ladder — a city marched down; and one house on the wall, marked by a cord, is carried out alive |
| 2 | **The Valley They Named Trouble** | Joshua 7 | The Angels, who watched the lot narrow tribe to clan to man | A whole army routed by the men of little Ai because one man took of the devoted things — a goodly Babylonian garment, silver, a wedge of gold hidden in the earth under his tent; thirty-six dead, a leader on his face, and a valley named Achor, Trouble, over a heap of stones |
| 3 | **He Would Not Draw the Spear Back** | Joshua 8:1–29 | The Angels, who had seen those same hands held up over Amalek | An ambush set behind the city and a feigned flight before it — the rout pretended this time, and the trap sprung; and the javelin stretched out toward Ai and not drawn back until every soul is destroyed, the outstretched arm that would not fall, and the king of Ai hanged on a tree until evening |
| 4 | **Every Word, Read to Every Soul** | Joshua 8:30–35 | The Letters — the very words being read aloud from the stones | An altar of stones no iron tool had touched on Mount Ebal, the law written on plastered stones, half the tribes toward Gerizim and half toward Ebal, the ark in the midst — and not a word of all that Mosheh commanded left unread before the whole assembly, the women, the little ones, and the strangers among them |
| 5 | **Stale Bread Bought Their Lives** | Joshua 9 | The Angels, who saw the oath sworn before the mouth of Yahuah was asked | Mouldy bread, cracked wineskins, patched sandals, "we are come from a very far country" — a lie that saves its tellers; the princes swear by Yahuah and ask no counsel, and because the oath was sworn in the Name it stands, and the Gibeonites become hewers of wood and drawers of water |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* Yericho's obedient fall opens the album in wonder; the valley of Achor is the Turn, the album's catastrophe of a single hidden sin; the retaking of Ai answers it with a trap and an unfallen arm; the law read on the mountain is the Selah, the covenant word spoken over the whole nation so none can plead ignorance; and the Gibeonite oath lands the album on a paradox — a fraud that binds, because Israel swore by the Name.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 6 Yericho · 7 Achan, the defeat at Ai, the valley of Achor · 8:1–29 the taking of Ai · 8:30–35 the altar and the law read on Ebal · 9 the Gibeonite deception. Five for five.

# ALBUM III — NOT ONE KING LEFT STANDING
### The Coalition Kings · Joshua 10–12 · four songs

When the kings of Canaan finally band together, the campaigns run south and then north, and nothing stands before them. Five kings besiege Gibeon for making peace, and Yehoshua marches all night to break them — and the day itself is stretched long, the sun halted over Gibeon and the moon over Aijalon, hailstones killing more than the sword, because Yahuah hearkened to the voice of a man as never before or since. The five kings are dragged from the cave they hid in and the captains set their feet on their necks; the northern coalition, numberless as the sand with horses and chariots, is broken at the waters of Merom and its chariots burned; and the record closes with a roster of thirty-one thrones and not one left standing. The witnesses keep the tally of every fallen king.

*Why this title:* 10:8, 11:23, and 21:44 together — not a man of all their enemies could stand before them; the campaigns end with the land at rest from war and every king of Canaan brought down.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Sun, Stand Still Over Gibeon** | Joshua 10:1–15 | The Angels; the sun and moon invoked as image | Five kings against Gibeon, an all-night march from Gilgal, great hailstones from heaven that slay more than the sword; and "Sun, stand thou still upon Gibeon, and Moon, in the valley of Aijalon" — the sun stayed in the midst of heaven a whole day, the one day Yahuah hearkened to the voice of a man |
| 2 | **Feet Upon the Necks of Kings** | Joshua 10:16–43 | The Angels, who watched five thrones dragged into the light | Five kings hidden in the cave at Makkedah, sealed under great stones, then brought out; the captains called to set their feet upon their necks; the kings hanged on five trees until evening and cast back into the cave — and city after southern city taken, because Yahuah fought for Israel |
| 3 | **They Burned the Chariots They Won** | Joshua 11 | The Shin — the fire — with the Angels | A northern host by the waters of Merom, many as the sand, with horses and chariots past counting; and the strange command — hamstring the horses, burn the chariots with fire; Hazor, the head of those kingdoms, burned alone; the victory that refuses to keep the very weapons it captured |
| 4 | **We Wrote Down Every Fallen King** | Joshua 12 | The Letters — the ledger-keepers who never drop a name | The roll of the conquered — two kings east of Jordan, Sihon and Og, brought down under Mosheh, and thirty-one kings west of it under Yehoshua, each named and each entered in the column; a whole land's thrones set down in a single accounting |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the standing sun opens the album at its highest pitch of wonder; the five kings under the feet is the Unveiling of total victory; the burned chariots at Merom carry the northern campaign and the refusal of the horse and chariot; and the ledger of thirty-one kings lands the album as a benediction rendered in a roll-call — every throne of Canaan named, and none left standing.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 10:1–15 the sun stands still at Gibeon · 10:16–43 the five kings in the cave and the southern sweep · 11 the northern campaign and Hazor · 12 the roster of the kings. Four for four.

# ALBUM IV — NOT ONE WORD FAILED
### The Land Divided · Joshua 13–21 · seven songs

Now the sword is sheathed and the land is parceled out — and the long, patient lists of borders and cities turn out to be the very proof that Yahuah keeps every promise. The tribe given no land inherits the Giver Himself; an eighty-five-year-old asks for the mountain still full of giants; a daughter climbs down from her donkey to ask for springs, and her father gives the upper and the lower; five sisters keep their dead father's name alive by claiming his portion among their uncles; and the man who divided the whole land takes his own share dead last. The cities of refuge open their gates to the unwitting killer, the landless Levites are scattered as cities through every tribe's territory, and the record closes with the sentence the whole book was written to earn — not one good word failed. The witnesses sing the inheritance handed down, allotment by allotment, as the promise comes true on the ground.

*Why this title:* 21:45 — "there failed not aught of any good thing which Yahuah had spoken unto the house of Israel; all came to pass"; every border, lot, and city in these chapters exists to demonstrate that one line.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Levi's Only Portion Was Yahuah** | Joshua 13 | The Angels | "Thou art old, and there remaineth yet very much land to be possessed"; the eastern tribes' inheritance recounted — and Levi given no share of the ground, because the offerings of Yahuah, Yahuah Himself, is their inheritance; the one tribe handed no land is handed the Giver |
| 2 | **Give Me the Mountain of Giants** | Joshua 14 | The Angels, who remembered the man from the twelve | Kalev at eighty-five, as strong for war as the day Mosheh sent him, who wholly followed Yahuah; and rather than easy ground he asks for the hill country of Hebron — the fenced cities and the Anakim still on it — "give me this mountain," the old man choosing the hardest land there is |
| 3 | **The Daughter Asked for Springs** | Joshua 15 | The Angels; the upper and lower springs invoked as image | Yehudah's borders drawn, Kalev driving the three sons of Anak from Hebron, Othniel taking Kiriath-sepher; and Achsah lighting down from her donkey to ask a blessing — "thou hast given me a south land, give me also springs of water" — and she is given the upper springs and the lower |
| 4 | **A Name Kept Through Its Daughters** | Joshua 16–17 | The Letters — the record-keepers who let no name fall out of the roll | Ephraim and Manasseh's portion, the Canaanites put to tribute but not driven out; and the daughters of Tzelophechad — Machlah, Noah, Choglah, Milcah, Tirtsah — standing before Eleazar and Yehoshua to claim the inheritance Yahuah commanded, a father's vanished name held open in the ledger through five daughters |
| 5 | **He Took His Portion Last** | Joshua 18–19 | The Angels | The tent of meeting pitched at Shiloh, seven tribes still slack to possess their land; the country surveyed in a book and the lot cast before Yahuah; and when all of it is divided, the one who divided it — Yehoshua — receives Timnath-serah, his own portion taken dead last of all Israel, and builds the city and dwells there |
| 6 | **Refuge Until the High Priest Died** | Joshua 20 | The Angels; the six cities invoked as image | Six cities set apart, roads kept open, where the one who kills unawares outruns the avenger of blood and is taken in at the gate; and he must stay until the death of the high priest before he may go home — a shelter for the guilty, and a strange release bought by a death, carried as ache and not named |
| 7 | **The Landless Dwelt in Every Land** | Joshua 21 | The Angels | Forty-eight cities with their pasture lands given to the Levites out of every tribe — the tribe that owns no territory of its own present in all of them, dwelling everywhere; and over the finished dividing, the great word: Yahuah gave them all the land He sware, and there failed not one good thing of all He had spoken |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the album opens on the unfinished land and Levi's paradox of a portion; Kalev's mountain and Achsah's springs and the five daughters are its human heart, faith and daring and a name held open; the leader's last portion is the quiet turn, greatness taking its share last; the cities of refuge are the Selah, mercy built into the map; and the scattered Levites and the closing declaration land the album — the whole tedious survey revealed, at the end, as the proof that every promise came true.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 13 the land that remains and Levi's portion · 14 Kalev's mountain · 15 Yehudah's lot and Achsah's springs · 16–17 Yoseph's allotment and the daughters of Tzelophechad · 18–19 the lot at Shiloh and Yehoshua's own portion · 20 the cities of refuge · 21 the Levitical cities and "not one word failed." Seven for seven. *(The seven-tribe allotment of 18–19 is treated as one distribution unit per §3.3, not split parcel by parcel.)*

# ALBUM V — CHOOSE THIS DAY WHOM YOU SERVE
### The Last Words and the Covenant at Shechem · Joshua 22–24 · four songs

The book of conquest ends not with a throne but with witnesses and graves. The eastern tribes go home and build a great altar by the Jordan, and the western tribes nearly march against them before they learn it is only a witness — Ed — that Yahuah is Elohim. Then the old leader gathers the nation twice: once to warn that the very nations left in the land will become snares and traps if Israel clings to them, and once at Shechem to rehearse the whole story from Abraham beyond the River and force the question that will not be softened — choose this day whom you will serve. A great stone is set up under the oak as a witness that has heard every word; and the book closes with three graves, and the bones of Yoseph, carried up out of Egypt, laid to rest at last in the land the oath had promised.

*Why this title:* 24:15 — "choose you this day whom ye will serve… but as for me and my house, we will serve Yahuah"; the covenant at Shechem is the book's final and central demand, and the thread that binds these last three chapters.

**Tracklist (play order):**

| # | Song | Passage | Who sings | The image it hangs on |
|---|------|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **They Nearly Warred Over an Altar** | Joshua 22 | The Angels; the altar named Ed invoked as image | The eastern tribes sent home, building a great altar by the Jordan; the whole west gathered at Shiloh to make war on them; Phinehas and ten princes crossing over — and the answer that stops the sword: it is no altar for sacrifice but a witness between us and you, and they name it Ed, that Yahuah is Elohim |
| 2 | **One of You Will Chase a Thousand** | Joshua 23 | The Angels, who reported the old leader's charge | Yehoshua, old and stricken in years, gathering the elders: cleave to Yahuah, for one man of you has chased a thousand because He fights for you — and the warning under the promise, that the nations left among them will be snares and traps and scourges, and that not one good thing has yet failed of all He spoke |
| 3 | **The Stone That Heard the Covenant** | Joshua 24:1–28 | The Angels; the great stone under the oak invoked as image | The whole story rehearsed at Shechem — "I took your father Abraham from the other side of the flood," a land they did not labor for; "choose this day," and "you cannot serve Yahuah, for He is a holy Elohim"; the people covenanting, and a great stone set up under the oak by the sanctuary as a witness, for it has heard all the words of Yahuah |
| 4 | **Yoseph's Bones Came Home** | Joshua 24:29–33 | The Angels, who kept the oath from the end of Genesis | Yehoshua dead at a hundred and ten, buried in his own Timnath-serah; the bones of Yoseph, carried up out of Egypt, laid at last in the parcel of ground at Shechem that Yaakov bought; and Eleazar buried in the hill of Phinehas — the book of the conquest closing on three graves and an oath finally paid |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the altar of witness opens the album on a near-war averted by a word; the farewell to the elders is the warning and the promise held together; the covenant at Shechem is the Declaration, the book's whole story recited and its choice forced under a stone that hears; and the three graves land the album and the book — the conquest ending not in triumph but in burial, and the bones that waited from Egypt come to rest in the land.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 22 the altar of witness and the near-war · 23 Yehoshua's farewell to the elders · 24:1–28 the covenant at Shechem and the great stone · 24:29–33 the three graves and Yoseph's bones. Four for four.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine); the Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere; internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Joshua-moment song the aches — the scarlet cord on the doomed wall, the memorial stones set for the children's future question, the Commander who is for neither side, the manslayer released at the high priest's death, the rest given yet not fully entered — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. The framing names Yeshua; the songs, standing inside their own hour, do not. (Yehoshua and Yeshua share the name; he is kept the man Joshua within the story, never leveraged for hindsight.)
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter. Yehoshua, Rachav, Kalev, Achsah, the daughters of Tzelophechad, Achan, and the Gibeonites are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the heaped Jordan, the twelve stones, the scarlet cord, the fallen walls, the sun and moon, the burned chariots, the six cities, the altar Ed, the great stone at Shechem, and Yoseph's bones are invoked as images, never voiced. The Commander of the army of Yahuah is witnessed and adored by the host He commands; the host — the Angels — are the singers there.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters appear as personae keyed to word or value where the text supports it — the Mem for the heaped water, the Gimel for *galal*/Gilgal (the reproach rolled away), the Zayin (seven) tolling Yericho's sevens, the Shin for the fire at Merom, the Letters as ledger-keepers for the roster of kings and the daughters' claimed name — and their total number is never stated. The text's own numbers are content: the twelve stones, the seven priests and trumpets and days and circuits, the thirty-one kings, the whole day the sun delayed, the forty-eight Levitical cities, the six cities of refuge.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
