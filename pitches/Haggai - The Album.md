## The Torah Sings Catalog Pitch for the Book of the Rebuilt House

**Platform:** Torah Sings (TorahSings.com) · Zev Inspire, project lead · all twelve Inspire Family members sing where suitable
**Governed by:** `_METHODOLOGY - Album & Song Creation.md` (signed standard) and `_GROUNDING - Torah Sings Project.md`
**Built by:** the process in `.prompts/music_torahSings - pitch process.md` — the same one that produced `pitches/Genesis - The Nine Albums.md`, `pitches/Exodus - The Ten Albums.md`, `pitches/Ruth - The Album.md`, and `pitches/Ezra - The Two Albums.md`
**Scope:** High-level album and song titles only — no lyrics yet. Chaggai is two chapters built as **four dated oracles** delivered in the second year of Daryavesh the king to Zerubbavel the governor, to Yehoshua son of Yehotzadak the high priest, and to the remnant returned from Bavel — so it is one album of four songs, one per dated word. Every song below is one narrative unit; every unit boundary sits on the text's own seams — the four date-headings that open each oracle ("in the sixth month, in the first day"; "in the seventh month, in the one and twentieth day"; "in the four and twentieth day of the ninth month"; "and again… in the four and twentieth day of the month") in place of the Genesis *toledot*.
**The voices:** Job 38:7, followed precisely — the morning stars are the Picture Letters; the sons of Elohim are the Angels. They sing praise to the Creator, Yahuah — and, from *within* each moment of the story, toward the Redeemer promised but not yet unveiled. **Narrative-time rule:** every song is sung from inside the days of the return, with no later hindsight — Yeshua is named and adored in the platform's framing, but inside a Chaggai-moment song the aches (a House lying waste while its people dwell in paneled rooms, a latter house of less gold promised a greater glory, "the desire of all nations shall come… and I will fill this house with glory," and a signet ring torn off in the older word now promised back onto the hand) are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. **Voice rule:** the singer is always the Angels and/or the personified Letters — never a human character (not Chaggai, not Zerubbavel, not Yehoshua) and nothing derived from one; the prophet's word to the leaders and "the people did fear before Yahuah" (1:12) are witnessed, never voiced, and Yahuah's own first-person oracles — "consider your ways"; "I am with you, saith Yahuah"; "the silver is mine, and the gold is mine"; "I will fill this house with glory"; "I will shake the heavens" — are His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who testify to what He said. Impersonal creation the text supplies (the ceiled houses and the waste House, the sown much and brought-in little, the bag with holes, the heaven staying its dew, the shaken nations, the signet ring on the hand) may be *invoked as image*, never made the singer. We overhear — and join in, or stand in shock and awe.

> *One album. Four songs. No honest zeros — every dated oracle of Chaggai is sung. Both chapters accounted for.*
> *Seven is the aim, not a cap, and the floor flexes far below it: this book holds four dated words, so it holds four songs, and four is what it gets — the count follows the oracles, never a ceiling, and nothing is padded past four. Not canon — something to consider.*

# ALBUM I — A RUINED HOUSE, A GREATER GLORY
### Consider Your Ways, and Build · Chaggai 1–2 · four songs

A people has paneled its own roofs while the House of Yahuah lies waste, and the drought and the empty purse follow it home — they sow much and bring in little, they earn wages only to pour them into a bag with holes, and the heaven over them is stayed from its dew. Then a word falls, dated to the day: *consider your ways* — go up, bring wood, build the House — and *I am with you, saith Yahuah*. The witnesses sing the four dated oracles that raise the House out of its ruin: the greater glory promised to a poorer house and the desire of all nations that shall come to fill it, the priestly ruling that holiness would not spread by touch though defilement would — yet "from this day will I bless you" — and the last word of all, the throne of kingdoms shaken and a signet set back on the hand: "for I have chosen thee." Joy held out over an empty barn; a ring torn off in the older word, and promised back.

*Why this title:* in the first oracle the House lies waste while the people dwell in ceiled houses (1:4, 9); by the second, Yahuah promises "the glory of this latter house shall be greater than of the former… and in this place will I give peace" (2:9). The album is that reversal — a ruined house given a glory greater than the first ever held — with "consider your ways" and "I am with you" as the whole mechanism between the ruin and the glory.

**Tracklist (play order — biblical narrative order):**

| 1 | **Consider Your Ways** | Chaggai 1:1–15 | The Angels, who heard a word dated to the day fall on a people rich in cedar and poor in everything | In the sixth month, first day, the word comes: is it time for you to dwell in your ceiled houses while this House lies waste? *Consider your ways* — you have sown much and brought in little, and earned wages to put in a bag with holes, for the heaven over you is stayed from dew and I called a drought upon the land because of mine House that is waste; go up, bring wood, build — and *I am with you, saith Yahuah*; and the remnant feared before Him and rose and worked on the House in the four and twentieth day |

*Sequencing (biblical narrative order):* the four dated oracles run in the order the text dates them, and the dates themselves ascend — sixth month, seventh month, ninth month, and the ninth month again. *Consider Your Ways* opens the album in the Veil, the ache stated in a ruined House and a stayed heaven; *The Desire of All Nations Shall Come* is the Unveiling, the highest promise sung over a house that looks like nothing; *Holiness Would Not Spread* is the Turn and the Selah at once — the sober priestly reckoning that bends, at the last verse, into blessing; and *Set as a Signet on His Hand* lands the record as the Benediction, thrones shaken and one man chosen, the torn-off ring promised back. Chaggai is already built as an emptying that turns to filling — a waste House raised to a greater glory — so the text's own dated order *is* the artistic shape; no reordering improves on it.

**Narrative-unit receipt (text order):** 1:1–15 the first oracle — the ceiled houses, the waste House, the drought and the bag with holes, "consider your ways," "I am with you," and the remnant who rose and built · 2:1–9 the second oracle — the house that is as nothing, "yet be strong," the shaking, the desire of all nations, and the latter glory greater than the former with peace in this place · 2:10–19 the third oracle — the priestly ruling on holy and unclean, "so is this people," and "from this day will I bless you" · 2:20–23 the fourth oracle — the heavens shaken, the throne of kingdoms overthrown, and Zerubbavel made a signet, "for I have chosen thee." Four for four.

## Compliance Notes

- **Sacred names:** OHI mode throughout — Yahuah, Yeshua, Elohim, Ruach HaKodesh (feminine); Yahuah Tzeva'ot rendered as the text names Him ("saith Yahuah Tzeva'ot," 2:6–9, 23). The English words "God" and "Lord" appear nowhere in any title or copy. The Hebrew article rule holds in every title and note (no doubled article on *Ha-* forms). Internal labels appear nowhere listener-facing. The prophet is Chaggai, the governor Zerubbavel son of She'altiel, the high priest Yehoshua son of Yehotzadak, the exile-land Bavel and the king Daryavesh — the name Yeshua is reserved for the Son and is not used of the high priest here.
- **Narrative-time rule honored:** inside every Chaggai-moment song the aches — a House lying waste while its people are paneled and warm, a latter house of less gold promised a greater glory (2:9), *the desire of all nations shall come and I will fill this house with glory* (2:7), and the signet ring torn off in the older word (Yirmeyahu 22:24) promised back onto the hand (2:23) — are carried as mystery and longing, never named as fulfilled. The signet is held as royal hope, not messianic revelation: the reversal is invoked backward, to a prior word the witnesses already know, never forward to any accomplished fulfillment; no Yeshua, no cross, no Messiah is named inside a song.
- **Voice rule honored:** every singer is the Angels and/or a named Letter — the Tet keyed to *tamei* (the basket that surrounds, first letter of the uncleanness that spreads where holiness would not), the Chet keyed to *chotam* (the fence that seals, first letter of the signet set back on the hand). Chaggai, Zerubbavel, and Yehoshua are *witnessed*, never made the singer; the prophet's word to the leaders and "the people did fear before Yahuah" (1:12) are testified to from heaven's side, never sung as theirs; and Yahuah's first-person oracles are His words, sung in awe by the witnesses who report what He said. The ceiled houses and the waste House, the sown much and the brought-in little, the bag with holes, the stayed dew and the drought, the shaken nations, and the signet ring are invoked as images, never voiced.
- **Picture Letters, never a count:** the Letters are named as personae where used; no total is ever stated and no gematria mechanism is sung. The text's own numbers are content, not method — the sixth month and the first day, the four and twentieth day, the seventh month and the one and twentieth day, the ninth month, "yet once, it is a little while," and the twenty-and-more years the House lay waste.
- **Hebraic Music Mandate:** when these songs enter production, the Hebraic music type will be primary or secondary in every track, per the grounding (§5).
- **Posture:** none of this is offered as canon. It is a reading — held at arm's length and turned. Something to consider.
