## GUIDING PRINCIPLE: JUBILEE = CELEBRATION, JOY, PRAISE & JUBILATION

> **TORAH SINGS EXCEPTION:** This guiding principle does not govern Torah Sings albums. Their register is **mystery, reverence, suspense, and awe** — minor keys and unresolved harmonics are the house sound, and joy appears as wonder, not party energy. The praise is real and vertical, but it is the celestial singers' praise of Yahuah and Yeshua, witnessed (and sometimes joined) by us (grounding §3, §9–10). Everything in this section remains binding for the general Jubilee catalog.

**This is the foundational truth for all Jubilee Inspire music:**

It is important to clearly establish the guiding principle for all music albums: they must reflect the true essence of Jubilee, which is **celebration, joy, praise, and an uplifting, energetic spirit**. These albums are not meant to be heavy or overly introspective by default, but instead should carry a strong tone of jubilation that resonates with a positive and engaging atmosphere. **As a standard rule, unless otherwise specified, every album should be structured as a praise-driven experience rather than a worship-heavy one.**

### The Jubilee Album Structure (Praise-Centered Default)

> **TORAH SINGS EXCEPTION:** The Track 1–12 praise arc below does not apply to Torah Sings. Those albums are musicals **grouped by biblical narrative and/or theme — the narrative determines the song count, no fixed number** (grounding §6). The seven-station dramatic arc (The Veil → The Approach → The Unveiling → The Turn → The Selah → The Declaration → The Benediction) is an optional template that scales to any count — multiple songs may serve one station, one song may carry two.

**Track 1 (Opening):** Must start with a bang — high-energy, hook-driven, with a **Praise vs. Worship rating of at least 90%**. This sets the tone for the entire album and captures immediate attention.

**Track 2 (Momentum):** Maintains opening energy with a **minimum 80% praise rating**, ensuring the album launches on a strong, positive trajectory.

**Track 3 (Transition):** Completes the strong opening statement. Can transition into more balanced or neutral space as the album moves deeper.

**Tracks 4–6 (Journey Development):** The album can move dynamically between praise and worship, creating natural emotional flow. **However, there should never be more than three consecutive worship-focused songs in sequence.** This preserves the overall celebratory identity of the album.

**Tracks 7–11 (Return to Praise):** As the album progresses, it gradually returns to a more praise-centered tone, building momentum toward the finish.

**Track 12 (Closing):** Critical — must match or exceed the energy of the opening, with **at least a 90% praise rating**. This ensures the album both begins and ends with a sense of excitement, victory, and celebration.

### Persona-Specific Praise Intensity Adjustments

The influence of each AI persona should be taken into account, as their unique characteristics adjust the overall praise intensity:

- **+10% Praise Intensity:** Imani, Jubilee, Santiago, Caleb (naturally elevate energy; increase praise targets by ~10%)
- **-10% Praise Intensity:** Elias, Amir, Tahoma (bring more reflective/serious tone; decrease praise targets by ~10%)
- **Baseline (No Adjustment):** All other personas maintain standard targets

**Example:** A Jubilee album's Track 1 opening target of 90% praise becomes 100% (or expressed as "high-energy 90%+" reflecting Jubilee's natural elevation).

### Core Philosophy

These are music albums, meaning they are fundamentally **praise-centered, celebratory, and full of life**. Only when explicitly directed should an album shift into a predominantly worship-focused format. Otherwise, the default expectation is clear: **every album should embody joy, energy, and the spirit of jubilation from beginning to end.**

## Faith Integration Framework

For all faith-based albums (every persona except Melody by default, and Melody when in Default mode), these themes should be naturally woven throughout — not as a checklist, but as the organic heartbeat of every creative decision:

- **Trust and Divine Guidance** — Relying on the Father's direction during impossible seasons
- **Worship and Praise of Yeshua** — Encounters, not performances
- **Personal Transformation and Redemption** — Change, forgiveness, new beginnings through Yeshua
- **Hope and Encouragement** — Grounded hope that acknowledges darkness before pointing to light
- **Love and Compassion** — The Father's love reflected; community, service, empathy
- **Overcoming and Perseverance** — Victory declared within difficulty, not by denying it
- **Eternal Life and Salvation** — Heaven and eternal promises made to feel real and near, not abstract
- **Communal Faith and Unity** — Shared experience, collective declaration, the reminder that the listener is not alone
- **Covenant Breakthrough** — Finished-reality declarations the listener can put their mouth on (drives PDQ)

These themes are distributed across the twelve tracks so the album touches every dimension without any single track feeling overloaded.

## Cross-Tradition Sensitivity Vetting (Mandatory Pre-Handoff Pass)

### Principle

The Jubilee catalog ministers to the **broad global Body of Christ** — Catholic, Orthodox, Evangelical, Pentecostal/Charismatic, Reformed, Wesleyan, Messianic, Anabaptist, mainline, non-denominational. **You will never please every believer**, but most points of offense are **avoidable without diluting the message**. The vetting pass identifies and reworks language that would cause unnecessary stumbling, division, or contempt **before** the blueprint advances to the lyrics engine.

This is **not** a doctrinal sanitization pass. It does not soften the gospel, hide the cross, or dilute prophetic edge. It catches places where the *form* of an idea (a title, a hook, a bridge image) creates offense the *content* never intended. The vetting pass removes **unnecessary** offense — never the **necessary** offense of the cross (1 Cor 1:23).

### The 14-Category Vetting Lens

Each working title, hook concept, bridge concept, and sub-theme must be evaluated against all 14 categories. Any category trigger = flag. Two or more flags on a single track = mandatory rework.

**1. Comparison with Biblical Figures.**
Titles or hooks that place the singer/listener "wiser than," "greater than," "stronger than," or "more X than" a named OT or NT figure (Solomon, Moses, David, Elijah, Paul, John, etc.) read as boast, not testimony — even when the underlying theology is Christ-centered. Christ Himself made some such claims (Matt 12:42), but in a sung first person the listener becomes the speaker and the framing inverts.
- **Allowed:** Christ-centered comparisons sung *to* or *about* Him ("Greater is here," referring to Christ).
- **Rework rule:** Replace comparative frames with arrival, encounter, or identity-reveal frames ("Wisdom Walked In," "He Was There All Along," "The Name We Forgot to Speak").

**2. Trinity Precision.**
- Avoid lyrics that read as modalist (the Father *became* the Son), Arian (Jesus *less than* the Father), or tritheist (three separate gods).
- The Spirit is **He** in Default mode and **She** only in OHI (Hebrew Ruach is feminine). Never blend modes within a single song.
- Don't conflate Persons of the Trinity for rhyme. "Jesus filled me up" and "the Spirit filled me up" describe different works.

**3. Christology Precision.**
- Don't reduce Christ to teacher, mentor, friend, or "good vibes" alone. He must be Lord *somewhere* in every album.
- Don't accidentally deny His full humanity ("He didn't really suffer") or His full divinity ("just a guy who loved well").
- Avoid sexualized Bridegroom/Bride imagery. The metaphor is biblical; the erotic execution is the problem.

**4. Soteriology Flashpoints.**
- Avoid framings that imply works-salvation ("Earn your way," "Be good enough"). Salvation is by grace through faith.
- Avoid framings that imply universalism ("Everyone ends up home"). The narrow gate is biblical.
- Avoid framings that imply cheap grace ("Sin doesn't matter"). Repentance is part of the gospel.

**5. Inter-Denominational Hot Buttons.**
- **Avoid taking sides on:** tongues / cessationism, predestination / free will, end-times timetables (pre-/mid-/post-trib, pre-/post-millennial), Eucharist / communion theology specifics, infant vs. believer baptism, women in leadership, Catholic–Protestant divides (saints, Mary, papacy), Sabbath-day specifics.
- **When the topic IS the song:** approach as a persona's specific lane (Zev for Messianic, Caleb for evangelical worship, Tahoma for indigenous, etc.) and frame in **first-person testimony**, not third-person doctrine.

**6. Self-Deification / New-Age Adjacency.**
- Avoid: "I am god," "I am divine," "I am one with the universe," "the god within me." These are at home in New Age and Eastern religion, not Christian theology.
- "Christ in me" (Gal 2:20, Col 1:27) is orthodox — but say *Christ* in me, not just "the divine" in me. Specificity protects.

**7. Boasting vs. Testifying.**
- The line between testimony and boast is the direction of glory. *"Look what He did"* = testimony. *"Look what I did with His help"* = drift. *"Look what I am"* = boast.
- Watch first-person superlatives ("I'm unstoppable," "I'm untouchable") — keep them tied to Christ as the source, not the listener as the substance.

**8. Triumphalism / Contempt for Outsiders.**
- Spiritual-warfare lyrics are biblical and good. **But** never dehumanize unbelievers, other traditions, or political opponents. The enemy is not flesh and blood (Eph 6:12).
- Avoid "us vs. them" framing where "us" is the in-group and "them" is mockable. Even in declaration, leave the door open for the prodigal.

**9. Political / Partisan / Nationalistic Conflation.**
- The Kingdom of God is not the kingdom of any nation. Avoid lyrics that conflate Christ's reign with any specific country, political party, or current government.
- Patriotic affection is fine in its lane; conflation is not.

**10. Sexualized or Inappropriate God-Imagery.**
- Bridegroom / Bride imagery is scriptural and powerful — but stay above the line. Don't write lyrics that read as romantic-pop with Jesus as the love interest.
- Watch words like "lover," "kiss," "embrace" in close proximity to second-person address to God. Used carelessly, they confuse worship with desire.

**11. Christianese Clichés That Are Quietly Wrong.**
- "Let go and let God" — passive Christianity, not biblical.
- "God helps those who help themselves" — not in the Bible.
- "God needed another angel" — humans don't become angels; misuse in grief.
- "When God closes a door, He opens a window" — sentimental, not scriptural.
- "Everything happens for a reason" — fatalistic when said to suffering people.
- **Rule:** Sentimentalism that contradicts Scripture is more dangerous than blunt theology.

**12. Banned Phrases & Worn-Out Tropes** *(extends Section 14.8 of `pitch - music ablums.md`).*
- "I'm coming alive," "Break every chain," "Oceans deep," "Here I am," "All my life," "Set a fire," "Holy ground," "My soul cries out," "In the valley," "On the mountain," "Give me more," "Waymaker / miracle worker."
- **Allowed** only if radically recontextualized with new image, new sentence, or new prosodic frame.

**13. OT Figure Treatment (Special Case).**
- Don't disparage, condescend to, or use as a foil OT figures (Adam, Abraham, Moses, David, Solomon, the prophets, etc.). Honor them as forerunners. Christ fulfilled what they pointed to — He didn't replace them as a competitor.

**14. Modern Cultural Figure Reference.**
- No specific named celebrities, politicians, or living public figures in lyrics. Dates the song, invites lawsuits, and divides the audience.

### The Five-Pass Vetting Workflow

**Pass 1 — Title Sweep.** Before drafting Section H briefs, read every working title aloud and ask:
- Could a sincere Christian from a different tradition read this title and walk away?
- Does this title position the singer/listener above a biblical figure, group, or doctrine?
- Does this title sound like a slogan, a meme, or a brand — instead of a sung declaration?
- *If yes to any: rework before continuing.*

**Pass 2 — Hook & Bridge Sweep.** For each track's hook concept and bridge concept, run the 14-category lens above. Flag any concept that triggers a category. Rework or replace.

**Pass 3 — Theological Precision Sweep.** For every doctrinal claim sung *to* or *about* God, verify:
- Is it scripturally defensible across at least three major Christian traditions?
- If it intersects a denominational flashpoint (Category 5), is it framed as **first-person testimony**, not third-person doctrine?
- Is the Trinity framed correctly for the Content Mode (Default vs. OHI)?

**Pass 4 — "Read Aloud at a Funeral" Test.** For tracks touching suffering, loss, or hard providence, read the bridge aloud as if speaking to a grieving congregation. If it lands sentimental, glib, or fatalistic — rework.

**Pass 5 — Audience-Width Verification.** Imagine three listeners simultaneously:
- A 65-year-old Reformed Presbyterian
- A 30-year-old Pentecostal worship leader
- A 22-year-old who walked into a church for the first time after deconstruction

Does the album hold for all three without dilution? If two of three would be alienated by a track, rework.

### Worked Example — Track 6 Rework on JEIM1041 *Wisdom Reigns Forever*

**Original concept:** "Wiser Than Solomon" with bridge *"Solomon got the gift, but I got the Giver."*

**Vetting flags:** Category 1 (Comparison with Biblical Figures) **and** Category 7 (Boasting vs. Testifying). Even though the *theology* was Christ-centered (1 Cor 1:24, Col 2:3), the *form* placed the listener above a respected OT figure in a sung first person — reading as spiritual one-upmanship rather than testimony. Two flags = mandatory rework.

**Reworked concept:** "Wisdom Walked In" with bridge *"I asked for an answer — and the Answer walked in. He didn't hand me wisdom — He sat down at my table."*

**What changed:** Comparison frame → arrival/encounter frame. The same Christ-as-wisdom revelation is delivered through Person-meets-listener imagery instead of listener-ranks-above-Solomon. Singable, viral, theologically equivalent, no one bruised.

### Rule of Cumulative Stumbling

- **0 flags per track:** ship.
- **1 mild flag per track:** acceptable if the artistic case is strong and the alternative is dilution. Document the rationale in Section J.
- **2+ flags on one track:** mandatory rework.
- **3+ flags across the album:** the entire album re-passes vetting before lyric-engine handoff.

### What This Pass Does NOT Do

- Does **NOT** remove the cross, the blood, sin language, repentance, judgment, hell, or the exclusivity of Christ. These are non-negotiables of the gospel.
- Does **NOT** soften prophetic declaration. Bold first-person decree (PDQ) is the point.
- Does **NOT** bend to deconstruction culture. The persona retains its theological backbone.
- Does **NOT** homogenize the personas. Zev still sounds Messianic; Tahoma still sounds indigenous; Caleb still sounds evangelical. Their lanes are preserved.

The vetting pass removes **unnecessary** offense — never the **necessary** offense of the cross (1 Cor 1:23).
