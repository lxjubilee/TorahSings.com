# Torah Sings — Angelic Image Generation Guide

**Purpose:** Convert song lyrics into photorealistic, emotionally riveting marketing artwork depicting angelic presence and celestial worship.
**Platform:** TorahSings.com
**Prepared for:** Gabriel Ungureanu
**Status:** Art Direction & Prompt System — v2.0 (Advanced Techniques integrated)

> **How this ties into production (owner directive, 2026-07-08):** every song block in each album lyrics file ends with an **IMAGE PROMPT** field, generated from that song's lyrics by this guide. Each IMAGE PROMPT is **≤ 1500 characters, with the first 250 characters weighted the heaviest** (image models weight early tokens most) — so the compact realism anchor + vantage + primary physical subject + angelic register must live in that first 250. The **standard negative prompt (§7) is not repeated per song** — it is documented here once and applies to every generation (like Suno's separate Style field). **T7 (the hidden glyph) appears in every image, always.**

---

## 1. The Governing Principle

Every image must answer one question:

> **What were the angels doing while this song was being sung?**

Not "draw an angel." Not "illustrate the lyric." The camera is placed inside a real moment of celestial worship that is happening *whether or not anyone is watching.* The viewer has stumbled into it. They were not invited. They are seeing something they were never meant to see, and it is still happening.

**The three rules that flow from that:**

1. **The angels are not posing.** They are mid-act. Mid-song. Mid-flight. Mid-collapse in worship. Never centered, never smiling at the lens, never aware of us.
2. **The angelic is often peripheral.** The most powerful images place the angelic activity in the *background, the periphery, the reflection, the atmosphere* — hidden in plain sight, exactly as it is in the Scriptures. The viewer's eye finds it a half-second after the image loads. That delay is the entire emotional payload.
3. **It must look like it was photographed, not painted.** Real optics. Real grain. Real atmosphere. If it looks like fantasy illustration, it reads as fiction. If it looks photographed, it reads as evidence.

---

## 2. Visual Theology (Non-Negotiable)

**Scriptural anchor:** Job 38:7 — the morning stars sang together and the sons of Elohim shouted for joy at creation.

**What this means visually:**

- **The stars are participants, not scenery.** Starlight is *voice*. In every night-sky composition, the stars should read as active, resonating, alive — not decorative background.
- **The angels sing; they do not perform.** Their worship is directed away from the viewer, upward and outward toward Yahuah. Their attention never lands on us.
- **The Inspire Family are vessels, never originators.** No persona is depicted as the source of the song. Where human figures appear, they are small, receiving, overwhelmed, or turned away.
- **Yahuah is never depicted.** Never a face, never a form, never a figure. Only light, only direction, only the response of everything else to what is off-frame. The most powerful images show creation *reacting* to a presence that is never shown.
- **Yeshua is never depicted as a rendered face.** Where His presence is implied, use light, silhouette from behind, or absence.

**Reverence guardrail:** These images accompany Scripture. Nothing sensual, nothing gratuitous, nothing that trivializes. Awe, not spectacle. Terror, not gore. Weight, not kitsch.

## 3. Angel Design Doctrine

The single fastest way to destroy this project's credibility is a greeting-card angel. Kill it on sight.

### Forbidden (the "Christmas card" failure mode)
- Chubby cherubs, babies with wings, Renaissance putti
- Blonde women in white nightgowns with feathered swan wings
- Halos as flat golden rings
- Harps, clouds as furniture, pearly gates
- Anything smiling gently at the camera
- Wings that look glued on

### Required (the biblical failure mode — awe and dread)
Angels in Scripture cause men to fall on their faces. Build them accordingly. Choose one register per image:

**Register A — The Terrifying Herald (Seraphic)**
Towering. Multi-winged, with wings that *cover* — one pair over the face, one over the feet, one pair for flight. Faces obscured, always. Bodies of burning metal, molten bronze, living coal. Heat distortion in the air around them. The ground beneath them scorched or vitrified.

**Register B — The Living Light (Ophanic / Wheel-like)**
Not humanoid. Concentric rings of light, wheels within wheels, rimmed with eyes rendered as points of unbearable brightness rather than literal eyeballs. Slow rotation implied by motion blur. Utterly alien, utterly holy.

**Register C — The Watcher (The Man Who Is Not a Man)**
Human-shaped but *wrong*. Too tall by a head and a half. Too still. Standing in the background of an ordinary scene, feet not quite touching the ground, face turned three-quarters away or lost in bloom. This is the register for "hidden in plain sight." Devastatingly effective. Use it often.

**Register D — The Choir (Massed, Distant)**
Thousands. Rendered as a shifting constellation of light in the upper atmosphere, or a horizon-wide band of luminous forms too far away to resolve. Individual figures barely legible. Scale is the emotion here.

**Universal angel rules:**
- **Faces are always obscured** — by wings, by light bloom, by turn of the head, by distance, by motion blur. Never a rendered angelic face. This is both theologically safer and infinitely more frightening.
- **Wings are never bird-decorative.** They are architecture. Vast, structural, casting real shadow, disturbing real air. Dust and particulate move in their wake.
- **They displace the world.** Grass flattens. Water ripples. Dust lifts. Light bends. An angel is a physical event.
- **Light originates from them or through them** — they are never lit by the scene. They are the light source, or they are backlit by something greater and unseen.

## 4. The Ten Techniques (Advanced)

These are the moves that separate *striking* from *unforgettable*. Every album should deploy at least three, and no album should deploy the same three as the album before it.

- **T1 — Photograph the Song (Star Trails as Sustained Notes).** Long-exposure star trails turn points of light into lines — sustained notes arcing across the sky. In a few frames, let a trail curve into the shape of a Paleo-Hebrew letter; never mention it. *Vocab: long-exposure astrophotography, 4-hour star trail, concentric arcs around the celestial pole, varying trail lengths, one trail curving impossibly against the rotation, ultra-wide, Kodak Vision3 500T.*
- **T2 — Let the Camera Fail.** Equipment that could not survive the light. Blistered emulsion, one edge blown to pure white bleeding into sprocket holes, chromatic aberration going violently wrong near the wing, a light leak, a burn. *Vocab: damaged film emulsion, heat-blistered negative, light leak across sprocket holes, severe halation past the frame edge, chromatic aberration fringing near the light source, physical film artifact.* **Restraint: one or two per album.**
- **T3 — Turn the Camera Around (You Are the Angel).** A vast out-of-focus wing enters the lower frame, unmistakably attached to the viewer; below, impossibly far, the subject. *Vocab: first-person vantage from extreme altitude, enormous out-of-focus wing entering the lower frame attached to the viewer, looking down across [subject] far below, vertigo, 24mm, deep focus on the distant ground.*
- **T4 — Photograph the Face That Sees, Not the Thing Seen.** Frame the angel entirely out; a human face in unbearable off-frame light. *Vocab: extreme close portrait, weathered face turned up into blinding off-frame light, pupils fully constricted, mouth open in awe, tears streaking through dust, harsh key from outside the frame, 85mm f/1.4, Cinestill 800T halation.*
- **T5 — Creation Notices Before We Do.** Every animal turned to the same empty point; a child staring at a doorway while adults face away; every bird leaves at once. *Vocab: documentary photography, [ordinary scene], every animal turned to face the same empty point off-camera, unnatural stillness, humans unaware, one child looking at the empty doorway, natural light, 35mm, handheld.*
- **T6 — Make the Sound Visible in Matter.** Cymatics — dust/sand/water self-organizing into geometry no wind explains. Ties the art to the cipher (pattern hidden in matter as pattern is hidden in text). *Vocab: cymatic standing-wave pattern, dust motes suspended in a shaft of light in radial geometry, fine sand self-organized into concentric symmetry on ancient stone, water holding an impossible stationary interference pattern, no wind, macro, raking light.*
- **T7 — Hide a Glyph in Every Image (drawn from the song's own derivation).** A hidden Paleo-Hebrew letter in every piece of artwork, **always** — but the letter is chosen **per song, from that song's own derivation** (its key Picture-Letter), not one catalog-locked glyph. A crack, a flock, negative space between wings, a shadow, a star-trail bend. The discipline that never changes is *a glyph is always hidden*; the specific letter changes with the song, tying each image to that song's own cipher (richer than a single fixed mark). Name the chosen letter and its hiding place in the prompt; keep it findable, never obvious. *(Not optional — every image, always. The same letter may recur where two songs' derivations call for it, but it need not be — and by default is not — the same everywhere.)*
- **T8 — Arrive After (The Aftermath Frame).** Skip the event; photograph what it left. Grass flattened in a vast radius still smoking; stone vitrified in the shape of two feet; a dropped jar; footprints running away; nothing in frame, everything just happened. *Vocab: documentary aftermath photography, wide circle of grass flattened outward from an empty center, faint smoke rising, stone fused to glass in the shape of two footprints, a dropped clay jar, footprints leading away at a run, no figure, overcast, 24mm deep focus.*
- **T9 — Break the Century.** One or two per catalog span, set today: a 3 a.m. parking lot, a hospital corridor, a wheat field by a highway — and a figure too tall, too still, in the unlit corner. *Vocab: contemporary documentary photography, [modern mundane location] at night, single sodium vapor lamp, in the far unlit corner a human-shaped figure far too tall standing motionless, feet not quite meeting the ground, face lost in shadow, security-camera plainness, 35mm, Cinestill 800T.* **1 maximum. Never the lead cover.**
- **T10 — Say It Twice (The Diptych).** Two frames, seconds apart, identical composition; one variable changed (a shadow with no owner, dust lifted, a door now open). *Vocab: matched pair — identical camera/lens/light/framing, Frame A [ordinary scene, nothing present], Frame B identical except [the single anomaly], locked tripod. Generate A first as reference for B; deliver as one wide canvas.*

**The Priority Three (when resources are constrained): T4, T7, T8.**

## 5. The Excavation Aesthetic

The artwork lives inside the website's "biblical archaeologist" identity.

**The Dust-to-Glory Gradient:** every image contains both *earth* and *radiance* — the buried and the breaking-through. Dust catching light; stone giving way to gold; something covered being uncovered.

**Core palette:**
- **The Buried:** deep bituminous black, wet stone grey, ash, iron oxide, clay ochre, aged parchment
- **The Breaking Through:** molten gold, brass, amber, ember orange, bone white
- **The Celestial:** cold star-silver, deep lapis, ultraviolet edge, pale cyan for distant choir light

Warm gold against cold void. Never a pastel. Never a full-saturation rainbow. Never neon.

**Atmosphere is mandatory.** Every image contains suspended particulate: dust, ash, pollen, sand, spark, or snow. Volumetric shafts of light through dust are the house look.

**Texture over polish.** Cracked stone, weathered bronze, salt-crusted rock, ancient plaster, worn parchment. Nothing chrome, nothing plastic, nothing new.

## 6. Lyric-to-Image Method (the six passes)

Do not illustrate the words. **Excavate the moment underneath them.**

1. **Locate the Vantage** — where is the singer standing? Above the void, at a tomb's threshold, on the firmament's rim, over a sleeping city. *That vantage is the camera position.* (Most important decision.)
2. **Extract the Physical Anchor** — the one concrete, photographable noun (water, stone, fire, dust, a doorway, a wing, a horizon, a hand). Build the image on that object in absolute physical realism.
3. **Name the Emotional Register** (exactly one): Dread · Awe · Grief · Triumph · Longing · Holy Terror. It governs light and composition.
4. **Choose the Angelic Placement** (vary aggressively): Foreground Dominant (rare) · Background Activity (workhorse) · Peripheral/Edge · Reflected Only · **Implied Only** (most shared — wingshadow, scorched earth, flattened grass, a light with no source, displaced dust).
5. **Withhold Something** — one element the viewer cannot fully resolve; the eye must return to it.
6. **Select the Techniques** — at least one the lyric asks for, plus one the album has not used. Match: stars/singing→T1 · overwhelming glory→T2 · sung by the angel looking down→T3 · a human witnessing→T4 · animals/children/earth→T5 · sound/voice/pattern→T6 · departure/what remains→T8 · the ongoing/still-present→T9 · a single instant of change→T10. **T7 always.**

## 7. Prompt Architecture

Build every prompt in this fixed order (early tokens weigh most — so the first 250 characters must carry the compact realism anchor, the vantage, the primary subject, and the angelic register):

```
[MEDIUM + REALISM ANCHOR]  →  [VANTAGE / CAMERA POSITION]  →  [PRIMARY PHYSICAL SUBJECT — the anchor noun, richly specified]  →
[ANGELIC PRESENCE — register + placement]  →  [LIGHT]  →  [ATMOSPHERE — particulate/volumetrics]  →  [PALETTE]  →
[COMPOSITION + SCALE CUES]  →  [OPTICS — lens, aperture, film stock, grain]  →  [EMOTIONAL REGISTER]
```

**Realism anchor (open every prompt with a variant):** *Photorealistic, shot on 65mm large-format film, natural light, documentary realism, extreme detail, subtle film grain, no illustration, no painting, no CGI.* (Compress it when the 250-char budget is tight.)

**Optics vocabulary:** Vastness 14–24mm deep focus f/11 · Intimacy/dread 85mm f/1.4 · Distant choir 200mm compressed haze · Handheld-witness slight motion blur + imperfect framing. Film stocks: **Kodak Vision3 500T** (night/cold), **Kodak Portra 400** (warm gold), **Cinestill 800T** (halation/bloom). *Halation is the secret weapon for night/fire.*

**The Standing Negative Prompt (documented once; applies to every generation — do NOT repeat it inside each song's IMAGE PROMPT field):**
```
NEGATIVE: cartoon, anime, illustration, painting, digital art, 3D render, CGI, cherub, putti, baby angel,
blonde woman in white robe, feathered swan wings, flat golden halo ring, harp, fluffy clouds, pearly gates,
greeting card, smiling at camera, direct eye contact, rendered angelic face, face of God, face of Christ,
depiction of deity, neon, pastel, oversaturated, HDR, plastic skin, airbrushed, chrome, glossy, symmetrical,
centered, posed, stock photo, watermark, text, logo, modern clothing, extra fingers, deformed hands, uncanny anatomy
```

## 8. Worked Examples (abridged — see v2.0 source for full)

- **"We sang while the deep was still without form"** → high-altitude vantage over an endless black ocean, glass-smooth, a warm gold glow rising from far beneath the surface (source unseen); a horizon-wide band of distant luminous winged forms in slow concentric motion in the top eighth of frame; volumetric shafts; 20mm f/11, Vision3 500T halation; overwhelming awe. *(Awe · Distant Choir · Cosmic · Water · T1.)*
- **"He does not know we are standing here"** → ancient stone dwelling at dusk, a man kneeling with his back to camera, low oil lamp; in the unlit doorway behind, a human-shaped figure far too tall, motionless, face lost in bloom, dust near it drifting upward; 85mm f/1.4, Cinestill 800T; mounting dread. *(Dread · Watcher/Background · Human · Flesh · T7.)*
- **"The stone remembers what the earth forgot"** → macro crouch over a half-unearthed limestone slab, Paleo-Hebrew cut deep, half buried in dust; across it falls the sharp shadow of an enormous wing cast by nothing visible; a low raking sun-shaft thick with dust motes; 50mm f/2.8 Portra 400; aching longing. *(Longing · Implied Only · Macro · Stone · T7 + wing-shadow.)*
- **"And the ground could not hold the sound of it"** (stacked) → aftermath: a vast circle of scrub flattened radially, faint smoke; inside, sand self-organized into a cymatic pattern; center vitrified to glass; a dropped jar, footprints running away; upper-right emulsion blistered to pure white; the cracks in the glass form a Paleo-Hebrew character; 24mm f/11 Portra 400, physical film damage; holy terror. *(T8 + T6 + T2 + T7 — four techniques, no wing.)*

## 9. Album Cover Specifications

Square, 3000×3000 min (deliver 4000×4000). A deliberate low-detail **title safe zone** (upper or lower third) — negative space is the design. **Thumbnail test at 120×120px** — one dominant shape, one gesture, one column of light; if it becomes noise, it fails. No text baked in. **Vertical variant (1080×1920)** recomposed, not cropped.

---

## 10. Variety Enforcement

Rotate deliberately; **no two adjacent albums may share the same Placement + Scale pairing.** **At least two of every seven images must be "Implied Only"** (no visible angel).

**Technique rationing per seven-image span:** T7 every image, always (the letter chosen per song from its derivation — not a single catalog-locked glyph) · T4 2–3 · T8 2–3 · T1 1–2 · T6 1–2 · T5 1–2 · T3 1 · T10 1 · T2 1–2 (never a wash) · **T9 1 maximum, never the lead.** T3/T9/T10 are shock instruments — used once, unforgettable; used thrice, wallpaper.

---

## 11. Emotional Compulsion Checklist (≥5 yes, plus all mandatory)

Half-second delay · looks photographed · something withheld · scale (viewer feels small) · dust in the light · angel unaware of us · survives the thumbnail · carries the excavation · screenshot-and-send-without-caption.
**Mandatory regardless:** the glyph is in it (T7) · it looks *photographed* not "rendered" · at least one Technique is doing real work.
**The test:** if it merely looks beautiful, it failed — it must look *found.* Strip the caption and title: does it still stop the scroll?

## 12. Production Workflow

0. Identify the song's glyph (T7) — the key Picture-Letter from its derivation — to hide in the image (per song; no single catalog-locked glyph).
1. Receive the finished song + full lyric sheet.
2. Run the six-pass method (§6); write the excavation notes (vantage, anchor, register, placement, withheld, techniques).
3. Check the variety matrix + rationing (§10) vs. adjacent albums.
4. Compose the prompt in the fixed architecture (§7); the standard negative (§7) applies separately.
5. Generate ≥12 variants; diptychs as locked pairs (A first as reference for B).
6. Composite the glyph (T7) if not produced naturally — findable, never obvious.
7. Score the checklist (§11): ≥5 + all mandatory.
8. Thumbnail test at 120px.
9. Theological review — no depicted deity, no rendered angelic face, no eye contact, nothing trivializing.
10. Deliver: square master (4000²), vertical (1080×1920), diptych wide canvas where applicable, and the excavation notes.

**Torah Sings production integration:** the **IMAGE PROMPT** field at the end of each song block (in the album lyrics file) is the composed §7 prompt, ≤1500 chars with the first 250 weighted heaviest; it is generated from the song's lyrics via §6. The full excavation notes and variant selection happen at production time.

## 13. Quick Reference

**Always:** hidden glyph · dust in the light · faces obscured · angels unaware · one real object rendered perfectly · negative space · something withheld · warm gold against cold void · film grain · named lens and stock.
**Never:** cherubs · swan wings · flat halos · eye contact · rendered angelic face · any depiction of Yahuah or Yeshua · pastel · neon · symmetry · posed figures · edge-to-edge detail · anything painted.
**Priority three:** T4 · T7 · T8. **The test:** *If it looks beautiful, it failed. If it looks found, it worked.*

*The stars sang. The angels sang. Now they are photographed mid-song, and they never once looked at the lens.*
