# TORAH SINGS — VOICE CASTING MATRIX
## The Twelve Inspire Family Voices · Canonical Casting Reference v1.0

**Rule (owner's standing directive):** every Torah Sings song is cast from the twelve on a **most-suitability basis** — evaluated on **gender, voice type, and music lane** against the song's locked vocal design. All twelve voices are used across the catalog. **Gabriel Inspire never fronts.**

**THE GENDER-BALANCE RULE (owner's standing directive, 2026-07-08) — for human/marketing/branding appeal:** every album should aim for a **50-50 female-to-male ratio among its LEAD singers**, even where that does not perfectly fit a persona's genre profile. The roster is built for it — 6 female (Jubilee, Melody, Zariah, Eliana, Imani, Nova), 6 male (Zev, Amir, Caleb, Elias, Santiago, Tahoma). A powerhouse voice (e.g., Jubilee) may be cast on an intimate track and a quiet voice on a bold one; when balance and pure suitability conflict, **balance wins, and the arrangement flexes to fit the voice** (adapt register/instrumentation, keep the derivation locked). Practical form:
- Balance is measured on **lead vocals** (the song's assigned `ARTIST:`). Features are a secondary balancing lever.
- Even song counts target an exact split (6 songs → 3F/3M); odd counts target the closest split (7 → 4/3 or 3/4).
- Suitability still governs *which* female and *which* male voice takes each slot — balance sets the ratio, suitability fills it.
- Record the balance tally in the album's lyrics-file header and the book `~index.md`.
**THE STRANGENESS LANE — ZEV FIRST (owner's standing directive, 2026-07-08):** When a song is **unusual, strange, uncanny, eerie, disorienting, or liturgically severe** — the dead beginning to speak, a Masoretic gap sung as engineered silence, a Picture Letter personified in a vertiginous way, a cataclysm sung as oratorio — **consider Zev Inspire first.** His exacting cantorial/hazzanut gravity, krechts and whisper-cantillation, and precise sacred-Hebrew diction make strangeness read as *holy* rather than gimmicky. This is a **first consideration, not a lock:** if another Inspire voice is genuinely more suitable to the song's locked vocal design, cast that voice instead — **especially when the design calls for a female lead** (Zev is male; the 50-50 gender-balance rule and the song's vocal design still govern). Zev holds the strange songs by default and yields them only to a better fit.

**THE JUBILEE STYLES RULE (owner directive, standing, 2026-07-08):** when **Jubilee Inspire** is the lead `ARTIST:`, the song's `Styles:` / Music Style block must **not** use the word **"celebration."** Describe her lane as *revival-scale, anthemic, festival-scale,* or *jubilant* worship instead. Her genre is unchanged — only the word is retired from her Style blocks.

**Where the evaluation lives:** a compact `CASTING (12-voice suitability evaluation)` block inside each song's entry in the album lyrics file (Caleb Inspire format); the full reasoning may expand in the song's blueprint.
**Sources:** `J:\music\albums\inspire\persona-music-styles.md` (canonical lanes, 2026-07-04) · per-persona blueprint Vocal Identity sections (`.prompts/music_*inspire*.md`) · persona portraits (`personas/`) · **owner roster ruling (2026-07-08, supersedes all legacy files): 6 male · 6 female — female: Jubilee, Melody, Zariah, Eliana, Imani, Nova; male: Zev, Amir, Caleb, Elias, Santiago, Tahoma.** (The legacy Tahoma blueprint's feminine pronouns are in error.)

## How to run the evaluation (per song)

1. Read the song's locked vocal design from its identification (song sheet / blueprint): lead register, choir behavior, features, the station's energy.
2. Score all twelve on three axes: **gender** (does the locked design call for a male or female lead — or an antiphon of both?), **voice type** (range, weight, ornament against the design), **music lane** (primary/secondary lane against the song's genre + the Hebraic Music Mandate).
3. Cast the lead; cast features where the design has them (bridges, harmonies, antiphons). Name 2–3 runners-up with one-line reasons — the record proves the evaluation happened.
4. **Album gender balance (the 50-50 rule):** before locking the album, tally lead genders and adjust toward a 50-50 split — reassign leads (flexing the arrangement to fit the new voice) until the album hits its target ratio. Then **catalog-level check:** watch the spread — all twelve voices land where genuinely suitable across the catalog; no voice hoards, no voice is padded in. The twins and buddy pairs (Jubilee↔Melody, Elias↔Eliana, Zariah↔Amir, Caleb↔Imani, Zev↔Santiago, Nova↔Tahoma) are natural duet/split-lead options.
5. Record the casting in the album lyrics file's `CASTING` block and set `ARTIST:` and `VOCAL GENDER:` accordingly.
