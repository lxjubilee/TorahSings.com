### Part 8: Complete Lyrics

> **TORAH SINGS NOTE:** For Torah Sings work, the reference-track lyric rules (grounding document, section 4) apply on top of every standard below: lines of **6-12 syllables** for clean Suno vocal mapping; short **hammering phrases** that mirror the Vav chain rhythmically ("And she saw, and she reached, and she took, and she tasted"); **percussive triplets** for construction and action ("He killed. He cut. He stitched." / "Wood on wood, plank on plank"); **parenthetical lines** reserved for the Outro and chorus reinforcement — they trigger Suno's echo/background choir: *(We who sang at the foundations) (Now guarded what remained)*; **join-in moments** — where human voices enter the celestial praise — marked plainly and used sparingly; commas and ellipses as phrasing cues; no ALL CAPS, and **exclamation marks only at a final-chorus climax, and rarely**; and the **final line of every song written to land in silence** ("And we… stood… still.").

Write the full lyrics meeting all standards:

#### Pictographic Lyrical Standards

- **Every image traces to a pictograph** — If the lyric says "the Foot came through the garden," it traces to Genesis 3:8 (mithalekh) through the Gimel pictograph. No imagery invented from air.
- **Every chain traces to the text** — If the lyric says "And she saw, and she reached, and she took," it traces to the Vav consecutives in Genesis 3:6. The "and… and… and…" IS the Vav.
- **Pictographs provide imagery; grammar provides rhythm** — The staccato Vav chain becomes rhythmic structure. The circular Tet/Basket becomes the returning chorus. The journeying Gimel/Foot becomes narrative arc.
- **Three-act mapping** — Verse/section 1 = first pictograph. Verse/section 2 = second pictograph. Bridge/Chorus = third pictograph (deepest revelation).
- **Numbers never appear in lyrics** — Present through imagery only.

#### Theological and Emotional Standards

- **Scripture accuracy** — Every concept faithfully represents the passage. The songwriter has no authority to edit God's Word for rhyme.
- **Emotional authenticity** — If the passage contains anguish, the lyrics contain anguish. If mystery, honor mystery. Do not flatten.
- **Fresh language** — No recycled worship clichés. Every phrase must feel written for THIS passage. If a line could appear in any worship song, it's not specific enough.
- **Cinematic specificity** — Place the listener inside the moment with sensory detail rooted in the passage's own imagery.
- **Voice distinction** — God's voice sounds different from the narrator's. The angels sound different from the characters. Perspective shifts are marked.

#### Singability Standards

- **Chorus power** — Singable after two listens. Under twelve words for the core hook. Open vowels on sustained notes.
- **Quotable density** — Two to three lines per movement designed for extraction and sharing.
- **Replay architecture** — Subtle variations across repeated sections that reward repeated listening.
- **The lyrical surprise** — At least one moment where the lyric goes somewhere the listener didn't expect. Mark with `[★ DISCOVERY]` — the moment the Paleo-Hebrew extraction reveals what was hidden.

#### Formatting

- Section headers: `[Celestial Intro]`, `[Solo]`, `[Verse]`, `[Pre-Chorus]`, `[Chorus]`, `[Bridge]`, `[VOICE OF GOD]`, `[WITNESS TESTIMONY]`, `[Selah]`, `[Declaration]`, `[Benediction]`
