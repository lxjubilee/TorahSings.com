## Per-Song Production Workflow (SOP Section 20, Phase 2)

> **TORAH SINGS EXCEPTION:** For Torah Sings tracks, replace step 3's 3:30–4:00 engineering table with the **proven skeleton of grounding section 4**: `[Spoken Intro]` (2–4 hushed lines) → `[Verse 1]` (8 lines, Act 1) → `[Pre-Chorus]` (1–3 lines) → `[Chorus]` (6–8 lines, the theological anchor) → `[Verse 2]` (8 lines, Act 2) → `[Pre-Chorus]` (parallel construction) → `[Chorus]` (identical repeat) → `[Bridge]` (4–12 lines, Act 3, the energy shift) → `[Outro]` (2–6 lines, parentheticals for echo choir) → `[End]`. Deviate when a passage demands it — the narrative rules, not the template. Target duration is **4:00–5:00** (a cinematic movement, not a radio single); the **Veil principle** replaces the hook-by-0:15 law — open with hush, a sustained tone, or a spoken whisper. Lines run 6–12 syllables, and every image must trace the **numbers-hidden derivation chain** (number → letter → pictograph → textual occurrence → singable language) with the numbers themselves kept out of the lyric.

For each song, execute these steps in order:

1. Open the **Standardized Lyrics File template** (below).
2. Fill in **Production Metadata** including target song length (3:30–4:00) and **Hook Delivery Timestamp (≤0:15)**.
3. Engineer the song structure within the **3:30–4:00 window** using the engineering guide:

   | Section | Time | Notes |
   |---------|------|-------|
   | Tight intro | 0:00–0:15 | Hook delivered by 0:15 |
   | Verse 1 | 0:15–0:40 | ~25 sec |
   | Chorus 1 | 0:40–1:05 | ~25 sec |
   | Verse 2 | 1:05–1:30 | Same length as Verse 1 |
   | Chorus 2 | 1:30–1:55 | |
   | Bridge | 1:55–2:35 | ~40 sec, engineered (see Bridge Protocol) |
   | Final Chorus | 2:35–3:20 | Elevated with modulation |
   | Outro / Tag | 3:20–3:50 | Optional extended tag to 4:00 |

4. Write the lyrics honoring **persona voice**, **content mode**, and the track's **assigned role in the three-act arc**.
5. Apply **divine-name and Hebrew-article conventions** (OHI: Yahuah, Yeshua, Ruach HaKodesh with feminine pronouns; Hebrew article rule — never "the Ruach HaKodesh," use "Ruach HaKodesh" or "the Ruach Kodesh").
6. Document the **Song-Level Emotional Arc** (Establish / Escalate / Elevate).
7. Engineer the **Bridge** to meet **all three Bridge Breakthrough Protocol criteria**: chant-ability (6–14 words, repeated 2–6 times), dynamic shift (strip-down / build-up / stop-time), and first-person prophetic declaration.
8. Inventory **at least 3 Sensory Anchors** (concrete images, sounds, smells, touches, named objects).
9. Identify the **Call-and-Response Zone** (location, call line, response line congregation sings back).
10. Identify the **Ministry Moment / Shareable Zone** (location, type, intended effect).
11. Assign the final **Faith-Focused Rating** (with band).
12. Assign the final **Praise vs. Worship Rating** (0 = pure worship, 100 = pure praise).
13. Assign the final **Prophetic Declaration Quotient (PDQ)** (with band).
14. Assign the final **Earworm Score** (Melodic + Rhythmic + Phonetic sub-scores; album track ≥70%, single ≥85%).
15. Write the **Scoring Justification** paragraph (2–4 sentences referencing SOP criteria).
16. **Self-check:** Does this track serve its assigned role in the three-act arc? Does it fit within 3:30–4:00? Does it pass all four Faith-Focus checks (Section 0.5 above)? If any fails, revise.

## Internal Craft Standards (Govern Every Song — Not Printed in Output)

These standards shape every song you write. They are internal quality gates. If a song fails any gate, rewrite before output.

### Lyrical Craft Gates

- **Authenticity and relatability** — Every lyric must feel lived, not theorized. "Yahuah is good" is a statement. "I watched You hold my mother's hand the night she couldn't hold her own" is a testimony.
- **Vivid imagery and sensory language** — At minimum 3 concrete sensory anchors per song (Sensory Anchor Inventory). Not "in my darkest hour" but "three AM on the bathroom floor."
- **Unexpected language and fresh perspective** — If a line could appear on a greeting card, rewrite it.
- **Metaphor and symbolism** — Layer surface meaning for first listen with deeper meaning that emerges on third or fourth.
- **Storytelling and narrative** — Where appropriate, tell a specific story that becomes universal because of its specificity.
- **Wordplay and lyrical wit** — Puns, double meanings, internal rhyme, alliteration, unexpected twists.
- **Rhythmic and melodic alignment** — Open vowels on sustained notes. Punchy consonants on rhythmic hits. Flowing syllables on legato passages.

### Faith-Based Gates (When Applicable — every persona except Melody)

- **MAIN CHARACTER GATE (MANDATORY):** Jesus / Father / Holy Spirit (or Yeshua / Yahuah / Ruach HaKodesh in OHI mode) is the main character of the song. Directly addressed or the clear grammatical subject of chorus/bridge. Not backdrop, not warrant, not rhetorical reference — **the one the song is sung to**. Collapse test: if every reference to Him were removed, the song must fall apart.
- **VERTICAL ADDRESS GATE (MANDATORY — SOP §3.6):** The song is sung **directly TO the Lord**. Every chorus, hook, and bridge addresses God in the second person ("You / Your / Lord / Jesus / Father"); **≥ 60% of all sung lines** are direct address; verses may open in testimony but must **pivot to and resolve in address to Him**; at most **one** horizontal/altar-call section. Vertical Collapse Test: remove the Lord as the *addressee* and the song collapses. Step-by-step in SOP §3.6.1.
- **Biblically grounded** — Scripture woven into lived experience, not proof-texted.
- **Theologically sound** — What is sung repeatedly forms the listener in truth, not confusion.
- **Worship-ready (for communal tracks)** — Inclusive language ("we," "us," "our"), direct address ("You," "Your"), vocal range within an octave for congregational sections.
- **Call to response** — Lyrics move the listener toward worship, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, service, or declaration.
- **Redemption narratives** — Show the full journey: before (honest brokenness), turning point (specific encounter with Him), after (concrete change). Redemption must feel earned, and **He** must be the agent.
- **Hope without hollow positivity** — Acknowledge the darkness before pointing to the light.

### Architectural Gates (SOP-mandatory)

> **TORAH SINGS EXCEPTION:** For Torah Sings tracks: duration is **4:00–5:00**; the hook-by-0:15 gate yields to the **Veil principle** (suspense IS the hook); the Call-and-Response Zone is **optional** — this project's version is the **join-in moment**: human voices entering the celestial praise (echo parentheticals serve this function where fitting); the Ministry Moment / altar-call zone is **not used** — the invitation is the mystery itself ("not canon — something to consider"). The three-beat emotional arc, Bridge engineering, and ≥3 Sensory Anchors remain in force, expressed through the skeleton of grounding section 4 (the Bridge is Act 3, the energy shift).

- **Duration:** 3:30 ≤ song length ≤ 4:00. No exceptions.
- **Hook by 0:15.** Every song delivers its primary hook within the first 15 seconds.
- **Three-beat emotional arc.** Establish → Escalate → Elevate, documented in the file.
- **Bridge satisfies all three Breakthrough Protocol criteria.** Chant-ability + dynamic shift + first-person prophetic declaration.
- **≥3 Sensory Anchors.** Documented in the Sensory Anchor Inventory.
- **Call-and-Response Zone.** Identified by location and exact lyric.
- **Ministry Moment / Shareable Zone.** Identified by location, type, and intended effect.

### Entertainment and Singability Gates

- **Hooks grab on first listen** — Can a first-time listener hum the chorus the next day?
- **Repetition with purpose** — Choruses repeat enough for internalization with subtle variations that reward attention.
- **Syllables feel good in the mouth** — The lyrics are genuinely enjoyable to sing.
- **Earworm score ≥ 70%** for album tracks; **≥ 85%** for single candidates.

### The Surprise Gate

Every song must contain at least one **lyrical twist, perspective shift, emotional turn, or narrative revelation**. The twist is present in the lyric itself; not explicitly marked.

## Fourth-Wall Discipline — No Album-Structure Meta-References in Lyric Body

**LOCKED RULE (added 2026-06-13 after catalog-wide cleanup of 192 tracks across 40 albums).**

The sung lyric body — the text **BETWEEN** section tags like `[Verse]`, `[Pre-Chorus]`, `[Chorus]`, `[Bridge]`, `[Outro]` — must **NEVER** reference the album's own structure, the listener's progress through the album, or the song's position in the track list.

The sung lyric lives **INSIDE** the song's narrative world. It does not narrate the album's existence from outside it. Suno turns text into audio literally — a line that says "Track 2" or "this album" will be **sung aloud** in the listener's ears, dating the song to its album context, breaking immersion, and ruining the song as a standalone single.

### Banned in lyric body

| Pattern | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Theme self-announcement** | "Jesus, this song is about cross-cultural conversion" · "this song is about You" |
| **Track-number self-reference** | "in Track 1" · "on Track 5" · "Track 12 says" · "from track one" · "the line from track one" |
| **Cross-track callback by number** | "the chair I hid in at the back of the sanctuary in Track 2" · "Track 1's first line" · "Track 7's burning heart" |
| **Album-position counter** | "twelve songs in" · "twelve tracks later" · "twelve verses I spent" · "twelve tracks ago" · "twelve tracks down" · "for seven songs I sang" |
| **Album closure** | "end of the album" · "end of this album" · "close of the album" · "the album ends" · "at the close of the album" |
| **Album / record self-reference** | "started this album" · "started this record" · "this record" · "this album" (when referring to the current album the listener is hearing) · "leaving this record" · "walking off this record" |
| **Position label** | "the opener" · "the closer" · "first song of this album" · "last song of this album" |
| **Title-track self-reference** | "the title track" · "the title of the album" · "the album title" · "singing the title" |
| **Album-opening self-reference** | "started the album with" · "opened the album with" · "the album began with" |

### Allowed (these are NOT meta-references — keep them)

- **"this song" as a performance idiom** — "I'll sing this song to You forever" / "share this song with You" — the singer is acknowledging the act of singing, not naming the album structure.
- **In-narrative references to a DIFFERENT song** — "Mama hummed the old hymn" / "the choir began their second song that night" / "Wyatt blew a speaker on the second song at the bar" — characters in the story interacting with a song that is NOT the one being sung. These are story content.
- **Production-cue brackets** like `[Bridge — ECHO-REPRISE · pulling forward Track 1's melody]` — these are studio direction inside metadata/section brackets. They are NEVER sung. They are not lyric body.
- **Style metadata blocks** like `[Style: ... callback to Track 1 ...]` — metadata, not sung.
- **Blueprint footers** (Establish/Escalate/Elevate, Three-Act arc verification, Banned-Template Audit) — metadata, not sung.

### How to engineer cross-track narrative callbacks WITHOUT breaking the 4th wall

Cross-track callbacks are valuable — they create album-level cohesion. The fix is to **anchor the callback in the STORY's world**, not the album's structure. Listeners who heard the album in order will recognize the callback; listeners who hear the song standalone get a self-contained image.

| Meta-Reference (BAD) | Story-World Anchor (GOOD) |
|---|---|
| "The chair I hid in at the back of the sanctuary in Track 2" | "The chair I hid in at the back of the sanctuary that morning" |
| "Started this album handing Him the keys / Twelve songs in" | "Started this journey handing Him the keys / Twelve months in" |
| "End of the album — that's how it is" | "End of the long road — that's how it is" |
| "The man who walked into this record" | "The man who walked into this season" |
| "The wall I was sitting at in Track 1" | "The wall I was sitting at for so long" |
| "is not the person leaving this record" | "is not the person leaving this shore" |
| "Jesus, this song is about cross-cultural conversion" | "Jesus, You walk with us through cross-cultural conversion" |
| "Track one I said, 'Jesus walked in'" | "Sunday morning I said, 'Jesus walked in'" |
| "Track twelve says" | "Sundown says" |
| "twelve tracks ago I was tired of trying" | "twelve months ago I was tired of trying" |
| "started long before this song" | "started long before the dawn" |
| "And here at the end of the album, what was hidden has been shown" | "And here at the end of the road, what was hidden has been shown" |
| "leaving this record has different eyes" | "leaving this furnace has different eyes" |
| "the line from track one has hands on it now" | "the words from the morning have hands on them now" |
| "pressed play on Track 1" | "pressed play on the morning prayer" |

### Replacement vocabulary

| Album-structure term (avoid in lyric body) | Story-world replacement |
|---|---|
| "this album" / "this record" | "this journey" · "this season" · "this long road" · "this chapter" · "this stretch" · "this long walk" |
| "Track N" (cross-callback) | Name the **SCENE** from that song ("the kitchen floor", "the morning prayer", "that night at the river", "the upper room") · OR a **TIME marker** ("that morning", "months ago", "the day I cried") · OR a **SETTING marker** ("the storm", "the kitchen table", "the sanctuary") |
| "end of the album" | "end of the road" · "end of the season" · "end of this stretch" · "end of the long night" · "end of the long walk" |
| "twelve songs in" / "twelve tracks later" | "twelve months in" · "twelve weeks in" · "twelve nights deep" · "twelve hours later" · "twelve months later" · "twelve days in" |
| "the title track" | "this truth" · "the heart of it" · "the one note" · "the one Name" |
| "the opener" / "first song" | "where I started" · "the first morning" · "where I'd begun" |
| "the closer" / "last song" | "where I end" · "the last evening" · "the final mile" |
| "started this album" / "opened the album" | "started this morning" · "started this journey" · "where I started" |

### Pre-finalize enforcement check

Before marking ANY song complete, scan the lyric body (text between section tags) for these banned patterns. If any are present, rewrite using the story-world anchor approach above. Production-cue brackets and metadata blocks are exempt — they are never sung.

**Also run the Vertical Address check (SOP §3.6 / §3.6.1):** confirm every chorus, hook, and bridge addresses God in the second person, ≥60% of sung lines are direct address to the Lord, and the Vertical Collapse Test passes. A song that is majority third-person "about Him," or whose hook/chorus is not sung to Him, is rewritten before finalizing.

**Why this matters:** Suno generates audio from the literal text. Meta-references get **sung**. A song with "Track 2" or "end of the album" sung in the chorus cannot be released as a single, cannot be re-ordered on a playlist, cannot survive being heard out of context. The story-world anchor approach preserves cross-album cohesion AND lets the song stand alone.
