## THE CHANGELOG — WHAT MOVED, AND WHY

**Diff discipline (adversarially verified):** exactly four lyric lines differ from the original; both choruses, the intro, the declarations, and the outro are character-identical.

| # | Original | Remastered | Why · trace |
|---|----------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | Before the **mountains** learned to sleep. | Before the **waters** learned to sleep. | No mountains exist in Genesis 1–2 (a Psalm 90:2 leak). One-word swap; 8→8 syllables; the sleep/deep rhyme survives. **Trace:** Genesis 1:2 — the deep, and Ruach hovering over the waters. Bonus: the sleeping waters of stanza 1 are the same waters Day 3 wakes to gather in fix 2 — Verse 1 becomes one arc. |
| 2 | And where it pressed, the waters **parted**, | And where it pressed, the waters **gathered**, | Day 3's verb is *gathered* (*yiqqavu*, 1:9) — "parted" belongs to Day 2 and the Exodus register. 9→9 syllables. **Fidelity upgrade:** gather is Gimel's own core sense (walk, carry, **gather**) — the Foot now does in the line exactly what the letter means. |
| 3 | Shaped from dust **but filled with flame**, | Shaped from dust, **a breathing frame**, | Genesis 2:7 gives breath, not flame. 7→7 syllables, stress-for-stress; the frame/name rhyme survives. **Trace:** *vayyitzer* (He formed — the potter root whose noun *yetser* means frame) + *nishmat chayyim* (the breath of life): both verbs of the verse inside three words, and "frame" feeds the tent-pole image the living nail pins. |
| 4 | Seven times He **spoke the word**, | Seven times He **saw it was...** | The formula is *vayar* — He SAW (1:4, 10, 12, 18, 21, 25, 31); Elohim never speaks the word *tov*. 7 syllables, trochaic shape kept; the ellipsis suspends the clause so the untouched next line lands as the seen thing: "He saw it was... Tov... Good." |
| 5 | *(credits, full package)* "The prefix וַ appears in approximately 34 of the 31 verses" | **Vav Opening Count:** every verse of Genesis 1 except the first opens with the letter Vav — **30 of the 31** (only בְּרֵאשִׁית, 1:1, stands outside the chain): 26 open with the vav-consecutive וַ, and 4 (vv. 2, 15, 18, 30) with the conjunctive וְ/וּ. Checkable in any Masoretic edition. | The original claim was arithmetic nonsense. Every figure verified verse-by-verse against the Masoretic text. |
